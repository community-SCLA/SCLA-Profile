## Communication norms

**Slack is the primary internal channel.** Use it for day-to-day questions, quick updates, and pinging Kierra when you drop a document in the community Drive.

**Gmail is for external communication.** Partner emails, member inquiries, and anything going outside the team.

**The Team Projects tracker (Canva canvas) is where work lives.** If a task isn't in the tracker, it doesn't officially exist for the purposes of project reporting. Drop your tasks there; Amy screenshots it for leadership updates.

**The community weekly sync canvas is for context, not tasks.** Drop your quick wrap-up bullets there before Monday meetings so Amy can compile the leadership update. Tasks go to the tracker.

**The community Google Drive is the document home.** Finished or in-progress documents go here. Do not leave work-product in your personal Drive, in Gmail drafts, or in a local folder. When you add something, ping Kierra in Slack so she can ingest it into Claude.

**Zoom for meetings.** Gemini takes notes automatically. You do not need to take manual notes in most cases — review the Gemini output and correct any errors.

## How decisions get made

Most decisions happen in the Monday meeting or in Slack, proposed by whoever owns the relevant work area, ratified by Amy (or by group consensus for team-wide changes).

Notable decisions go in [decisions-log.md](./decisions-log.md). Copy the template at the bottom of that file and add your entry at the top (newest first). What counts as notable? Any decision that:
- Changes a tool, process, or workflow
- Assigns a new project lead
- Sets a deadline or launch date
- Involves spending money (e.g., Claude Pro, Zoom license upgrade)
