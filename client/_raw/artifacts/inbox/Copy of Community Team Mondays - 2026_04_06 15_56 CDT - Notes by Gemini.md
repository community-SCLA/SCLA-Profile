### Summary

Team discussions on establishing a morning music tradition and implementing a paid AI agent led to a strategic pivot toward defining target audience needs and the future course pricing model.

**Team Tradition and Tool Updates**  
Discussions on implementing a Mars Rover-inspired morning music tradition led to the decision to upgrade the Claude account to the Pro level for approximately $17 per month. This upgrade will allow for greater functionality, potentially replacing the requested native Slack AI bot for resource management.

**Content and Template Progress**  
The course catalog work was finalized and sent for design, while the development of weekly email templates remains delayed awaiting conversion to MJML. The team has secured an interim solution by scraping the design to generate the necessary HTML code, which will remove the reliance on another team member for template creation.

**Student Needs and Course Strategy**  
The primary strategic focus shifted to the target audience, arguing that the greatest need and revenue opportunity lies with underrepresented students who require foundational career readiness. A decision was made to implement a baseline survey during orientation to categorize student needs and inform a new subscription-based pricing model for high-level content.

### Details

* **Shared Birthday Month**: Iman Lomax mentioned that their birthday is on December 5th, which is close to Yesse Ordonez’s birthday on December 7th, also known as Pearl Harbor Day.

* **Introduction to the Mars Rover Morning Song Tradition**: Amy Westby shared details from the documentary \*Good Night Oppy\*, explaining that NASA's Mars mission teams start their day by playing a wake-up song, which is chosen in rotation by a team member. They suggested implementing a similar tradition for the team by creating a collective Spotify playlist and playing songs randomly each morning.

* **Cool Vibes and Meditational Music**: Alyssa Phillips shared that they listen to a Spotify list called "Cool Vibes," which features meditative and mantra-like music focused on themes like self-worth, frequencies, and attracting wealth. Alyssa Phillips uses this music to set a positive tone every morning.

* **Lo-Fi and Genre Diversity**: Iman Lomax mentioned listening to R\&B and low-fi reggae, hip-hop, and Afro-beats, which they find more upbeat than traditional low-fi music, and committed to sharing some of their selections. Alyssa Phillips and Amy Westby both agreed that low-fi music helps them get into a productive zone for typing and working.

* **Morning Music Traditions**: Zeketra Grandy shared that they primarily listen to '90s R\&B and maintain a tradition of listening to "O Chariot" by Gavin DeGraw and "Sunday Morning" by Maroon 5 every day since college. Amy Westby encouraged adding these songs to the collective playlist.

* **Eclectic and Mood-Based Playlists**: Yesse Ordonez described their personal playlist as "messy," containing everything from '90s rap artists like Will Smith and Eminem to Beyoncé and Shakira, and including piano and string mixes like Bond. They explained that their music selection is driven by mood, requiring shifts between high-energy and calming music to maintain flow.

* **Taylor Swift and Music Curation**: Mayeth Gueta shared that their preferred music is Taylor Swift, specifically mentioning her \*Red\* album from 2021\. Amy Westby noted that their "head boss, Chuck," is also a huge Taylor Swift fan, and they are committed to adding her music to the playlist.

* **Weekly Announcements and Email Template Delays**: Amy Westby provided a quick update on current tasks, noting that the Google group issue with Sean is ongoing due to a known Google error message. Zeketra Grandy has successfully created the content in Canva for weekly announcements, but the team is waiting for Sean to convert them into MJML email templates for use in campaigns, preventing a consistent rhythm from starting.

* **Obtaining HTML Code for Email Templates**: Kierra Woekel shared that the current design does not support direct HTML download, so they used their Manis account to scrape the design and generate the HTML code. Kierra Woekel will verify the scraped HTML against the actual design and commit to sending the working HTML code, which, if successful, would remove the reliance on Sean for creating the templates.

* **Course Catalog Progress and Database Needs**: Mayeth Gueta received praise for their work on the public-facing course catalog, which has been sent to Nollie for design finalization and public readiness. Amy Westby noted that the organization has 61 or 62 courses, all of which require syllabi and coding, and stressed the need for further work on the database to manage the content.

* **Email Triage and Access Management**: Kierra Woekel, Amy Westby, and Yesse Ordonez are continuing to work offline on developing a system to triage incoming emails, which could involve creating an app script in Google to automate rerouting based on keywords or partners. Kierra Woekel confirmed that the Google Groups issue is a glitch, and while granting access manually is possible, they are looking for the permanent solution that the groups feature would provide.

* **Development of a Free AI Agent for Slack**: Kierra Woekel has been developing a free Gemini-based AI agent to host in Slack that can summarize threads, pull action items, autogenerate weekly summaries, and help locate resources. Amy Westby confirmed that the Google group error is a known issue being corrected by Google.

* **Proposal for Paid Claude Pro Account**: Kierra Woekel suggested that the best investment would be to upgrade their Claude account to a paid Claude Pro level for approximately $17 a month, as this would open up MCP calls and allow for greater functionality, potentially serving as a replacement for the requested native Slack AI bot. Amy Westby agreed to proceed with the request for the Claude Pro upgrade.

* **Micro-Internship Program Expansion**: Amy Westby discussed the micro-internship program, noting that they currently have many asynchronous relationships where the company provides a project and the organization handles all mediation and grading. The goal is to establish deeper, more formal partnerships where companies might run channels, be guest speakers, or collaborate on "behind-the-scenes" tours.

* **New Program Partnership Strategy**: The organization is actively seeking more companies to partner with for the micro-internship program, which has contracts and samples available for engaging new partners. Amy Westby believes the program has potential for in-person events, live internships, and grant funding through their nonprofit to support workforce development.

* **Future Course Strategy and Pricing Model**: The discussion pivoted to building a marketplace of paid and free content, noting that while they want to avoid nickel and diming members, charging for high-level content, such as one-on-one coaching or high-level certificates, is necessary to fund the platform and pay instructors. Amy Westby posed the question of how to structure the pricing and course list moving forward.

* **Subscription-Based Pricing Tiers**: Alyssa Phillips suggested a tiered pricing model, such as an initial $95 fee for foundational information and access, with additional paid tiers (e.g., $20 or $50 per month) for specialized expertise. Kierra Woekel cautioned that the structure and messaging must be mindful of the target market (students vs. professionals) to avoid making people feel "nickel and dimed".

* **Target Audience and Communication Clarity**: Zeketra Grandy emphasized the importance of clear communication regarding paid courses, recounting past issues where students felt deceived or misled into thinking paid courses were mandatory. Amy Westby noted that the current "Honors" community is for students with a GPA of 3.0 or higher, but they plan to open the "Academy" to all students soon.

* **Honors Status and Target Demographics**: Yesse Ordonez suggested maintaining the \*Honors\* designation as a VIP, prestigious level to incentivize high standards among all members, suggesting that non-honors members should "build up" to those standards. Iman Lomax challenged the focus on high-achieving students, arguing that top schools already provide the necessary resources, and the real need and revenue opportunity lie with underrepresented students in community colleges or trade schools who are underexposed to professional concepts.

* **Workforce Readiness and Career Services Gaps**: Iman Lomax and Zeketra Grandy agreed that the target students are those needing foundational career readiness, noting that they have seen "horrible" resumes and "absolutely awful" interview skills from applicants with bachelor's and master's degrees. Zeketra Grandy asserted that campus career services are "dropping the ball bad," failing to provide necessary support to many students who desperately need it.

* **Discussion on SCA Academy and Support Services**: Zeketra Grandy emphasized the necessity of the SCA Academy due to the underfunding and understaffing of current support services, which leads to staff burnout and an inability to help students effectively. They noted that the academy should be built over time and incorporate the ideas discussed by Iman Lomax. Alyssa Phillips and Amy Westby agreed that these necessary discussions should be integrated into the orientation process.

* **The Changing Value of a College Degree**: The participants agreed that a college degree is no longer a guarantee for a job, particularly with the rapid changes driven by AI, meaning the 'days are over' for this expectation. Amy Westby pointed out that even honor students from top universities are not guaranteed jobs upon graduation.

* **Student Career Preparedness and Strategies**: Iman Lomax identified two critical paths for students: those who need to learn foundational skills like resume writing and professional communication, and those who need to learn how to stand out and create a personal brand. Yesse Ordonez detailed a two-part session they conducted, called "how to get you unstuck," which focuses on determining what prevents students from securing second interviews and includes follow-up homework.

* **Need for an Orientation Baseline Survey**: The group agreed that a survey should be implemented during orientation to establish a baseline of student needs and expectations. This survey would help determine if students require fundamental skills or upper-level branding assistance.

* **Project Strategy and Strategy Sprint**: Amy Westby proposed taking a "30,000 foot" strategic approach to simplify and organize the overall platform, focusing on consumer needs before diving into specific projects like the "member journey project". Iman Lomax and Zeketra Grandy volunteered to participate in a strategic organization sprint. Kierra Woekel offered to help with information input once their access to Claude is set up.

* **Upcoming Project Communications and Assignments**: Iman Lomax confirmed they would send emails regarding the outreach for the pledge project and the talent cards. Yesse Ordonez shared their presentation with Amy Westby to provide context on their discussion points and mentioned they are also working on the video script.

* **Technical Issues and Bug Reporting**: Amy Westby reported that some students were experiencing a 500 error when attempting to publish their credentials to LinkedIn. Yesse Ordonez offered to be notified if any transcripts are returned.
