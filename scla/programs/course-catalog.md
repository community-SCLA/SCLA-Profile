## Standalone courses

### Brand You

**Full title:** Brand You: Crafting and Amplifying Your Unique Professional Identity

Professional branding course covering how to define, build, communicate, and sustain a personal brand.

**5 modules:**
| Module | Focus |
|---|---|
| 1 | Understanding Professional Branding — what it is, myths, self-assessment |
| 2 | Defining and Articulating Your Brand — UVP, brand statement, career-aligned goals |
| 3 | Building Your Brand Online and Offline — LinkedIn, digital presence, networking, consistency |
| 4 | Communicating and Amplifying Your Brand — elevator pitch, public speaking, thought leadership, advocates |
| 5 | Evolving and Sustaining Your Brand — acting on feedback, adapting, maintaining authenticity during transitions |

Each lesson includes curated articles, videos, and scripts/activities.

### Hidden Job Market for Graduates {#hidden-job-market}

Career course focused on the truth that most jobs are filled through relationships, not job boards.

**Core message:** The job market rewards relationships, not just résumés. Entry-level roles are increasingly competitive — AI tools flood employers with applications; ghosting and automated rejections are at all-time highs for online applicants. Companies still hire people they trust.

**Key advantage:** SCLA members have a built-in advantage — a network of high-achievers and alumni.

### Leader of the Future {#leader-of-the-future}

SCLA's flagship leadership development framework. A multi-module program covering the full arc of modern professional leadership.

**Core pillars:**
- Personal Brand & Identity
- Assertiveness & Networking
- Productivity & Well-being
- Emotional Intelligence & Reinvention
- Team Dynamics & Collaboration

**Sample lesson structure (each lesson ~45 min):**

**Lesson 1 — Crafting Your Personal Brand**
- Readings: HBR "Building Your Personal Brand," Fast Company "How to Stand Out"
- Videos: Brené Brown – The Power of Vulnerability (TED), Simon Sinek – Start With Why (TED)
- Activity: Write your Brand Statement & Reflection Journal

**Lesson 2 — Assertiveness & Networking**
- Readings: Fast Company "Networking That Works—LinkedIn Tips," Forbes Women
- Videos: LinkedIn Learning "Making Genuine Connections," TEDx "How to Make a Great First Impression"
- Activity: Identify and message 2 new LinkedIn contacts; journal results

**Lesson 3 — Productivity & Well-being Tools**
- Readings: Forbes "Time Management for Busy Professionals," Psychology Today "Science of Breaks"
- Videos: Khan Academy "Growth Mindset Explained," TED "Habits of Highly Productive People"

**Lesson 4 — EQ & Reinvention**
- Readings: Daniel Goleman EQ excerpt, HBR "Resilience at Work"
- Videos: "Embracing Change: Growth Mindset" (YouTube), Daniel Goleman interview
- Activity: Create a 6-month personal growth plan

**Key Rock goals for FA25:**
1. Optimize content delivery and student experience (mobile-first, UX clarity)
2. Drive measurable student engagement (streaks, leaderboard, badges, nudges)
3. Launch and refine Credential Central
4. Coordinate robust lifecycle communications

## 2026 content strategy principles

- **Lighter engagement:** 50%+ of learners want async, bite-sized formats. Less always-on; more curated micro-content.
- **Active learning first:** Reflection prompts, knowledge checks, polls embedded in every module.
- **4+ courses/series per month** targeted for 2026 catalog cadence.
- **Micro-dosed content** alongside full courses for members who can't commit to a full program.
