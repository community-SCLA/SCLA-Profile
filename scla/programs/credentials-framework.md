# SCLA Credentials Framework

How SCLA learning connects to recognized, shareable credentials students can use in job searches and on LinkedIn.

---

## The C2C Framework

Every SCLA program is organized around three pillars:

| Pillar | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Connections** | Building a lifelong professional network — peer connections, mentors, employers |
| **Credentials** | Earning recognized certifications and badges that prove readiness |
| **Career** | Translating learning into real career outcomes — jobs, internships, clarity |

The [SCLA Leadership Program](./scla-leadership-program.md) maps directly to this: Steps 1–3 are Connections, Step 5 is Credentials, Step 4 is Career.

## Credential stack

Credentials scale from micro to macro:

```
XPs (Experience Points)
  ↓
Badges
  ↓
Certifications / Credits
```

- **XPs** — earned for completing modules, activities, or community engagement
- **Badges** — stackable, earned per course or competency cluster; exportable to LinkedIn
- **Certifications** — full program completions (e.g., SCLA Career Readiness Certification)
- **Credits** — select completions may carry academic credit (e.g., NIC fellows received 6 academic credits for Executive Leadership in AI and Innovation)

## Engagement model (FA25)

To drive credential completion, the education team is building:

- **Streaks** — daily/weekly activity tracking
- **Daily nudges** — behavior-triggered email/text/push (e.g., sent when module completion drops off)
- **Leaderboard** — public recognition for active learners
- **Incentives** — recognition badges, early credentialing access, spotlights
- **Peer/community cohorts** — accountability groups tied to specific badges
