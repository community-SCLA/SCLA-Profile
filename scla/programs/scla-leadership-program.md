# SCLA Leadership Program

The flagship credentialed program for SCLA members. Built around three core objectives — **Connections, Credentials, and Career** (the C2C Framework) — it guides students through a structured journey of leadership development, professional growth, and community building.

## Program overview

- **Delivery modes:** Asynchronous (all 5 steps) and Live Online (Steps 1 and 5 only; Steps 2–4 remain async in both modes)
- **Completion:** Earns the SCLA Career Readiness Certification
- **Final video:** Congratulations message from Chuck (SCLA founder/president) at certification completion
- **Next step after completion:** Members Dashboard → social media post prompt

---

## The 5-Step Journey

### Step 1 — Orientation / Welcome

**Objective:** Introduce students to SCLA, complete the True Colors personality assessment, and begin engaging with the community.

- Available in both Live and Async formats
- True Colors assessment identifies one of four personality types (Orange, Blue, Gold, Green)
- Students receive a personalized welcome video referencing their school name and school logo
- Key message: "You are now part of a nationwide community focused on leadership and career success."

**Video structure:**
1. Scene 1 — Welcome (personalized to first name and school)
2. Scene 2 — What to Expect (flexible, self-paced format)
3. Scene 3 — Your SCLA Learning Journey (5-step infographic walkthrough)
4. Scene 4 — Who You Are (student-specific data from Synthesia)

---

### Step 2 — SMART Goal Module

**Objective:** Set leadership goals and track progress.

- Students define goals using the SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Async only

---

### Step 3 — Peer Connections

**Objective:** Engage with other students through discussions and community activities.

- Students complete a Peer Connections activity
- Async only
- CTA: Engage with fellow SCLA members in discussions

---

### Step 4 — Career Connections

**Objective:** Discover networking opportunities and explore career options.

- Uses the Resume tool (if not completed in a prior module)
- Covers types of networking for career/professional purposes
- Students learn how to reach out to different types of career connections
- CTA: Reach out to different types of career connections; students complete their Career Networking activity
- Async only

---

### Step 5 — Certification

**Objective:** Complete the final reflection and earn the SCLA Leadership Program certification.

- Available in both Live and Async formats
- Final congratulations video from Chuck — talking head, congratulates student, includes next steps for using their certification
- Members referred back to the Members Dashboard
- Members prompted to post their certification on social media
