## General

### What is SCLA?

The Society for Collegiate Leadership & Achievement (SCLA) is an accredited honor society that celebrates academic excellence and empowers students with the skills, connections, and credentials needed to succeed in college and beyond. With over 100,000 members at 850+ campuses, SCLA is your platform to stand out, grow, and launch your career.

### Is SCLA accredited?

Yes. SCLA is an accredited organization. College credit earned through SCLA programs is recognized through accredited academic partnerships and can be added to your official academic record.

## Membership
<!-- route: membership -->

### How do I become a member?

Membership is invitation-based. Students are invited when they meet the GPA and credit hour eligibility requirements at a partner campus. Your invitation is your opportunity to join a distinguished network of leaders.

For students at campuses without an SCLA chapter, online membership is available — see [What if my school doesn't have an SCLA chapter?](#what-if-my-school-doesnt-have-an-scla-chapter) below.

### What are the eligibility requirements?

To join, you must:

- Be enrolled as an undergraduate or graduate student at an accredited institution
- Have accumulated 12+ undergraduate credit hours or 9+ graduate credit hours
- Have achieved a minimum GPA of 3.0 (undergraduate) or 3.4 (graduate) on a 4.0 scale
- Be in good academic standing with your institution

### What if my school doesn't have an SCLA chapter?

Even if your campus doesn't have an SCLA chapter, you can still join. Our online membership offers the same exclusive benefits as on-campus chapters — career-building resources, leadership opportunities, and a nationwide network of high-achieving peers.

If you've already received an email invitation, enter your invitation code to get started. If you don't have a code, you can apply for membership directly at thescla.org.

### What are the membership benefits?

SCLA membership includes:

- Career Readiness Certifications from partners including Google and Microsoft
- College credit at no additional cost upon completing eligible programs
- 15-Minute Career Chats with industry professionals
- Exclusive job board access to top employers and internships
- Mentorship connections and a nationwide peer network
- Scholarship opportunities

## Programs & Certifications
<!-- route: programs -->

### How do I get my Career Readiness Certification?

The Career Readiness Certification is the first step in your SCLA experience. It is designed to:

- Equip you with essential career skills valued by employers
- Award you college credit at no additional cost upon completion
- Unlock advanced certifications and exclusive benefits

Every activity you complete earns Experience Points (XP) that contribute to certifications, badges, and recognition.

### How do I earn college credit through SCLA?

1. Find credit-eligible courses in the SCLA course catalog
2. Complete all course requirements and assessments
3. Download your unofficial transcript from your Credentials Wallet
4. Request an official transcript to be sent to your college or university

These credits are recognized through SCLA's accredited academic partnerships.

## Administrator Questions

### What is SCLA? (for administrators)

SCLA is a multi-disciplinary honor society with 100,000+ members at 850+ colleges nationwide. We maximize student potential through a skill development platform covering hundreds of career-critical skills, a mentor and peer community, career insider interviews, and direct access to hiring managers and internship opportunities.

### Is there a cost to host SCLA on our campus?

No. SCLA is completely free for colleges and universities. Our programs are designed to integrate seamlessly with your existing initiatives without additional budgetary requirements.

### How does SCLA benefit our institution?

SCLA offers a turnkey solution to enhance student engagement and success. Key benefits include:

- Customizable programs tailored to your campus needs
- College credit and certifications that help students stand out
- Real-time reporting tools to measure program impact
- No-cost partnership model

### How does SCLA support its chapters?

SCLA provides dedicated support to all campuses regardless of chapter size, including:

- Program implementation assistance
- Chapter management resources
- Ongoing student engagement strategies
- Visibility into program success through data reports

SCLA provides on-demand reporting including student achievement tracking and program impact data. Reporting is available through the admin portal.
