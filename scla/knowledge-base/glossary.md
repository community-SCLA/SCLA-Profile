## ISPI

- **Canonical form:** ISPI
- **Full name:** Innovation Strengths Preference Indicator™
- **Also seen as:** "ISPI strengths assessment"
- **Confidence:** high

A structured strengths assessment integrated into SCLA's member experience. It is listed in SCLA's assessments library with 3 sections and 64 questions.

ISPI is designed to help students identify their natural behavioral tendencies and leadership patterns — not to label, but to equip. It focuses on *application* of self-awareness to career decisions and professional positioning.

**What ISPI measures (four dimensions):**

1. **Decision-Making Style** — analytical vs. intuitive vs. collaborative vs. fast-execution
2. **Communication Tendencies** — direct/concise, diplomatic/relational, detail-oriented, or vision-driven
3. **Leadership Orientation** — initiative-taker, stabilizer, persuader, or structure-builder
4. **Work Environment Preferences** — structured systems, fast-paced startups, collaborative teams, or independent roles

**How SCLA uses it:** Integrated into the broader leadership development experience. Members use results in resume strategy, interview framing, career path selection, and goal-setting. Described as differentiating from tools like Myers-Briggs by emphasizing application over labeling.

> "The ISPI strengths assessment is a structured development tool designed to help students identify their natural behavioral tendencies and leadership patterns… ISPI focuses on application. It's not about labeling you—it's about equipping you."

## Career Readiness Certification

- **Canonical form:** Career Readiness Certification
- **Also seen as:** "Accredited Career Readiness Certification", "SCLA Career Readiness Certification", "Professional Readiness Program"
- **XP value:** 315 XP upon completion
- **Confidence:** high

The first and foundational step in a member's SCLA program journey. It is a 5-section, self-paced online program (~4 hours, 22 lessons) that awards a certificate of completion, college credit (at no additional cost), and 315 XP.

**Five sections:**

1. **Welcome & Orientation** — Introduction to SCLA, the leadership journey, and your Chapter (includes True Colors assessment)
2. **Setting SMART Goals** — Set and track personal leadership goals
3. **Peer Connections** — Build meaningful relationships with like-minded peers
4. **Career Connections** — Streamline job searching and professional networking
5. **Celebration** — Course completion and certificate award

**Skills earned:** Career Advocacy, Interview Skills, Active Listening, Adaptation to Change, Emotional Intelligence, Intrinsic Motivation, Critical Thinking, Communication, Time Management, Resilience

**College credit:** Awarded upon completion at no additional cost; enhances academic journey and résumé.

**Accreditation:** SCLA is described as "an accredited organization." The specific accrediting body is not named in available source material.

> "Begin your journey with the Career Readiness Certification, an exclusive opportunity to build skills, gain recognition, and earn college credit—all included with your membership."
