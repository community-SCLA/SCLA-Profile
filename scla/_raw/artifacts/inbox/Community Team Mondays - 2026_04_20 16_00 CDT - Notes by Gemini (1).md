They established that Jenna Heath and Iman own the initial "pledge" part of the process, including the info session, and that Zeketra Grandy and Alyssa are focusing on the central "onboarding" experience. They further clarified that they will work with Yesie regarding the "member support" section, ensuring all efforts are cohesive and avoid duplication.

* **The Funnel Vision and Process Documentation**: Amy Westby summarized the member journey as a funnel, starting with Jenna Heath and Iman's big info session for potential members, followed by an orientation, and then Zeketra Grandy and Alyssa's onboarding experience. Yesie is positioned as the case-by-case catchall for students needing extra support. Amy Westby concluded that Kierra Woekel's role is to document this process and create standard operating procedures (SOPs).

* **Document Location and Merging Progress**: Kierra Woekel inquired about where the project documents are currently stored, emphasizing the need for proper documentation. Zeketra Grandy explained that they are holding off on publishing the documents until they merge their work with Alyssa’s to avoid conflicting documents, and they plan to seek Kierra Woekel's guidance on where the completed version should reside. Kierra Woekel specified that all documents should be stored in the community Google Drive.

* **Timeline and Iteration Process**: Kierra Woekel asked for a timeline for the merged documents, as they wanted to provide input without disrupting an already finalized document. Kierra Woekel proposed that once the document is placed in the community Google Drive, they can facilitate iteration using the comment feature, with the overall goal of creating a dashboard that provides a bird's-eye view of document activity and connections. Amy Westby advised Zeketra Grandy to bring Kierra Woekel in at the "sweet spot" where the document is not completely blank but not fully fleshed out to utilize Kierra Woekel's organizational expertise.

* **SOP Creation and Template Request**: Amy Westby noted that Kierra Woekel is the "guru" for ensuring consistency in the creation of SOPs. Jenna Heath asked if Kierra Woekel could provide a template so that they and Aman could simply plug in their existing information. Kierra Woekel responded that they do not have a template but explained that Amy Westby and they are planning to use a Claude Pro account to scrape all information, create a knowledge base, and then automate the creation of a suitable structure.

* **Google Drive Access and Troubleshooting**: Jenna Heath mentioned they did not have access to the community Google Drive and requested access. Kierra Woekel provided a troubleshooting tip, noting that if the browser is signed into a non-SCA Google account, access will be blocked, and suggested creating separate Chrome profiles as a hack.

* **Solo Projects and Dashboard Development**: Amy Westby moved on to solo projects, briefly referencing the Community of Champions project, which Yesie primarily handles, before focusing on Kierra Woekel's update. Kierra Woekel reiterated the request for team members to drop their work into the community Google Drive and ping them so they can feed it to Claude for the dashboard.

* **Figma Focus Modes and User Journey Connection**: Kierra Woekel inquired about continuing work on the Figma document, which Amy Westby clarified contains the "focus modes," a mockup for the new dashboard. Amy Westby explained that focus modes are intended to help students, such as career transitioners or newcomers, filter out dashboard "noise" and find relevant content, confirming that this concept is directly tied to the user journey. They plan to blend the user journey and focus modes, with the rollout planned for August 1st, after having time to prepare during the summer.

* **Focus Mode Content and Content Building**: Amy Westby noted that each focus mode (e.g., career transitionist) will require dedicated content, including relevant channels, events, and asynchronous or live courses. Anushka has been tasked with building the content for these focus modes so they will be ready for the August launch.

* **AI Resume Builder and Tool Optimization**: Kierra Woekel brought up the AI resume builder, which was a recent topic in Slack, and Amy Westby clarified that it is not closed and covered. Amy Westby suggested that tools like the resume builder should only appear on relevant focus modes, such as the "newbie to a career" mode. They stressed the need for the team to compile feedback about tools, identifying bugs or needed features, to pass on to the tech team for resolution or feature request backlog.

* **Communication Gap and Bottlenecks**: Zeketra Grandy voiced frustration regarding configuration barriers and the lack of movement on their team's deliverables, suggesting that Kierra Woekel's talent is underutilized to move their projects forward. Kierra Woekel acknowledged configuration barriers, particularly with email communications, due to Shawn's specific requirements for the MJML code used for email templates. Amy Westby explained that the tech team is a small startup crew of three people focused on building sustainable long-term solutions, not just one-off fixes.

* **Email Campaign Technical Requirements**: Amy Westby provided a demonstration of the email source code, showing that each campaign must be custom-coded in MJML to ensure proper formatting, color schemes, and token usage, which is time-intensive. Kierra Woekel offered to use Claude to build an MJML template based on three desirable samples, which would then be provided to the tech team for implementation. Amy Westby agreed to work with Kierra Woekel offline to execute this plan.

* **Resume Builder Improvement Strategy and Content Needs**: Discussing the resume builder, Amy Westby highlighted the need to define whether improvements require a technical upgrade or a content/teaching solution, which is where the two teams intersect. Kierra Woekel suggested comparing their resume builder to popular external tools to make it a more intuitive "plug-and-play" experience. Amy Westby and Zeketra Grandy countered that many students struggle with the content itself, specifically identifying transferable skills and knowing what words to write.

* **AI Prompts for Resume Building**: Kierra Woekel offered the idea of an AI agent that could translate a student's duties into outcome-driven language, similar to a skills file they developed for another organization. Zeketra Grandy agreed, suggesting students should input their duties and target job description to ask the AI to determine transferable skills and help communicate them effectively. Kierra Woekel proposed using Claude's user-friendly interface to create a pop-up guiding the student through a back-and-forth conversation to extract information, rather than just filling out boxes.

* **Defining the Resume Builder Project for the Tech Team**: Amy Westby stressed the need to define the resume builder solution as a project for the tech team, complete with a Product Requirements Document (PRD), outlining both the technological changes and the content/teaching component the team will manage. They acknowledged the difficulty in prioritizing tasks, as the tech team is balancing the backlog of problems with the build of the future ecosystem, including work on a CRM and advisor tools.

* **Member Maintenance and Foundational Stability**: Zeketra Grandy emphasized their concern is less about their ideas being ignored and more about members being in limbo, which affects the ability to maintain current members. Zeketra Grandy argued that the focus should have been on strengthening the product and creating a solid foundation for existing members and partnerships before prioritizing the rapid acquisition of new ones. They expressed concern that the current strategy of "flying the plane while building it" risks losing established members.

* **Engagement Data and Low Refund Rate**: Kierra Woekel suggested analyzing Google Analytics data to identify where members are dropping off in the current client journey. Amy Westby shared that since December 1st, they have onboarded nearly 15,000 students and have only processed 317 refunds out of 30,000 total members since July 1st, a very low rate. Amy Westby confirmed that their team's increased involvement since January is leading to greater engagement, but Zeketra Grandy expressed concern that the low refund rate does not guarantee maintenance as contracts are guaranteed until June.

* **Concerns Regarding Contract Duration and Team Maintenance**: Zeketra Grandy raised concerns that the current four-month contracts, two months of which have passed, could lead to a lack of maintenance and support for community members if the contracts are not renewed, resulting in no one responding to questions or handling orientation. Amy Westby clarified that the four-month contract period was intended as a momentary barometer or milestone and not as a set time for people to leave, emphasizing that the goal is deeper member engagement provided by a consistent team of community humans. They added that the goal is to grow the community and they want the current team to remain in place.

* **Role of AI Tools and Structure in Community Growth**: Amy Westby explained that the plan to implement AI tools and improve structure is intended to solve problems better, enhance communication, and fix things more effectively, but not to replace the current team. The aim of these improvements is to enable more events, deeper content, and better focus, ensuring human support is available if an AI tool encounters issues. Kierra Woekel also noted the importance of preserving institutional knowledge of the work that has been completed.

* **Member Engagement and Tool Utilization**: Amy Westby shared an example of a member who indicated they did not require the resume builder or interview tool, stating they only needed an asynchronous component of the platform and nothing else. They used this example to suggest that the team should be comfortable with members utilizing only parts of the platform, even if certain tools, like the resume builder, still need to be fixed. Amy Westby mentioned that they are tracking drop-offs and the reasons for member unhappiness, confirming that engagement remains a key focus.

* **Process Improvement for Change Log and Prioritization**: Amy Westby emphasized the need for a better process or tool for managing the change log, future requests, and backlog, which would allow the team to document input from the field. They suggested that Sean, the team, and Chuck could prioritize the rollout of changes based on this documentation, noting the communication differences between Sean's zero documentation and the team's extensive documentation. Amy Westby is open to a better tool for communicating plans, ideas, and dreams, and Kierra Woekel suggested that the Claude tool would help with this.

* **Historical Context and Current Vision for Member Support**: Zeketra Grandy questioned whether the original SCLA had adequate member support, noting that the current situation cannot continue with just one person. Amy Westby confirmed that the original SCLA did not have a dedicated member support team because their program was solely an assessment that gave members PDFs for their resumes, thus requiring very little support. Amy Westby noted that the current organization is acting like a startup to build something different from the previous organizations by offering hundreds of courses, events, Slack channels, and a robust peer-driven experience.

* **Commitment to Team Stability and Consistency**: Zeketra Grandy reiterated the importance of consistency and ensuring that the work being done is maintained for the members, expressing concern that a drop-off in personnel could lead to new people struggling to understand the current direction. Amy Westby reassured the team that they do not want to hire a new team and are committed to keeping the current team for the long haul, adding that the uncertainty surrounding the contracts has been a source of dread for them. They expressed hope that the team members want to stay and emphasized their efforts to make the work life of the team as easy as possible.

* **Enhancing Team Camaraderie and Communication**: Amy Westby highlighted the importance of enhancing team camaraderie by connecting the current team with other members they have not yet met, such as Boris, Victor, and the new CFO, Brian. They expressed hope that building these relationships and sharing information with the larger team will strengthen the group and aid in communication. Zeketra Grandy acknowledged that the value of the team is noticeable and wonderful to see, affirming the team's uniqueness.
