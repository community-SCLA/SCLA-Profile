# SCLA Voice and Tone

> Source: Google Drive "SCLA Voice & Tone" in the Branding & Communications folder. Supplemented with verbatim copy from thescla.org.

## Who we're talking to

SCLA speaks primarily to high-achieving college students who are navigating the transition from campus to career. Many of them are uncertain, underestimating their own potential, or unsure whether they belong in professional spaces. SCLA's voice should meet them there — confident on their behalf, not above them.

## Voice axes

| Axis | Position | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Formal ↔ Casual | Leans casual with professional grounding | Use contractions. Address readers directly. Marketing copy is professional without being formal; blog copy is warmer and more conversational. |
| Technical ↔ Plain | Plain throughout | Avoid jargon. Explain concepts before using them. Language is accessible to any college student, regardless of major or background. |
| Earnest ↔ Playful | Earnest, with occasional lightness | Mission is serious and we mean it. Playfulness is allowed in titles and on-ramps ("Your Secret Weapon," "Without Being Awkward"). Humor is light — never sarcastic. |
| Authoritative ↔ Supportive | Supportive | SCLA is an enabler, not a gatekeeper. We don't talk down; we lift up. Position SCLA as the student's platform, not their judge. |
| Institutional ↔ Human | Human | Write like people, not organizations. Short sentences. Direct address. Acknowledge student doubts instead of talking past them. |

## Write this, not that

| Write this | Not that |
| --- | --- |
| "The SCLA" | "SCLA" (no article) in prose |
| "Honor Society" (capitalized) | "honor society" when used as SCLA's title |
| "you don't need years of experience to…" | "even beginners can…" |
| "SCLA is designed as an active development experience" | "SCLA membership gives you access to…" |
| "Free for colleges and universities. Period." | "There are no fees for colleges or universities" |
| "from campus to career" | "post-graduation success" |

## Phrases to avoid

| Avoid | Why |
| --- | --- |
| "exclusive club" | Contradicts SCLA's open, invitation-based framing. SCLA is selective, not exclusive in a gatekeeping sense. |
| Competitor honor society names | Brand clarity. Never reference competitor organizations in SCLA communications. |
| Heavy formal register ("pursuant to," "heretofore") | Inconsistent with the plain, human voice throughout the site. |
| "just" as a minimizer ("just a student," "just getting started") | Undercuts student confidence — the opposite of what SCLA stands for. |
| Passive voice ("students are empowered by") | SCLA copy consistently uses active voice ("SCLA empowers students"). Keep it active. |
| "prestigious" used alone without context | Can read as gatekeeping. Always pair it with what makes the recognition meaningful to the student. |
