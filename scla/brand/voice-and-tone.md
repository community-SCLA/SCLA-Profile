## Phrases and patterns to avoid

Patterns below are grounded in observed copy from the HTML exports. Items marked
`TODO: needs confirmation` should be validated with SCLA staff before treating as hard rules.

| Avoid | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| "exclusive club" | Contradicts open, invitation-based framing — SCLA is invitation-based but positioned as expansive, not restrictive |
| Competitor honor society names | Brand clarity |
| Heavily legalistic register ("pursuant to," "heretofore") | Inconsistent with the plain, human voice throughout the site |
| "just" as a minimizer ("just a student," "just getting started") | Undercuts student confidence — opposite of SCLA's empowerment positioning |
| Passive voice constructions ("students are empowered by") | Site copy consistently uses active voice ("SCLA empowers students") |
| "prestigious" used alone without context | Can read as gatekeeping; always pair with what makes it meaningful to the student |

## Write this, not that

Patterns drawn from observed copy across all scraped pages.

| Write this | Not that | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "The SCLA" | "SCLA" (no article) in running prose | Site body copy and blog posts consistently use "The SCLA" with capital T in prose |
| "Honor Society" (capitalized) | "honor society" (lowercase) when used as SCLA's proper title | Page titles and marketing copy treat it as a proper noun |
| "you don't need years of experience to…" | "even beginners can…" | Direct second-person address is warmer; "even beginners" frames the reader as less-than |
| "SCLA is designed as an active development experience" | "SCLA membership gives you access to…" | Active framing positions the org as purposeful; passive access framing sounds transactional |
| "Free for colleges and universities. Period." | "There are no fees for colleges or universities" | Short declarative sentences are on-brand; long qualifications dilute impact |
| "from campus to career" | "post-graduation success" | Tagline language should be used consistently; institutional synonyms drift from brand voice |
