# SCLA FAQs

Frequently asked questions from SCLA members, prospects, and campus administrators.

---

## General
<!-- route: general -->

### What is SCLA?

The Society for Collegiate Leadership & Achievement (SCLA) is an accredited honor society that celebrates academic excellence and empowers students with the skills, connections, and credentials needed to succeed in college and beyond. With over 100,000 members at 850+ campuses, SCLA is your platform to stand out, grow, and launch your career.

### Is SCLA accredited?

Yes. SCLA is an accredited organization. College credit earned through SCLA programs is recognized through accredited academic partnerships and can be added to your official academic record.

### How is SCLA different from other honor societies?

SCLA is a tech-focused, workforce-oriented honor society built around an online learning platform. Rather than a traditional chapter-based model with mandatory ceremonies or dues, SCLA is designed around self-guided, voluntary benefits — certifications, career connections, and professional development tools you can use on your own schedule. The focus is on building real-world skills and workforce readiness, not just recognizing GPA.

### How will SCLA help me with my specific career goals?

SCLA's platform is multidisciplinary and not tied to any single career path. The professional network connects members with experienced mentors across a wide range of industries and roles. Rather than preparing you for one specific job, the platform is designed to help you build transferable skills, make professional connections, and explore career directions — whatever field you're heading into.

### How do I contact SCLA?

You can reach SCLA Member Support by phone, email, or live chat on the SCLA website. Phone and chat are available during business hours. If you reach voicemail, leave a message and a staff member will follow up. You can also email membership@thescla.org directly.

## Membership
<!-- route: membership -->

### How do I become a member?

Membership is invitation-based. Students are invited when they meet the GPA and credit hour eligibility requirements at a partner campus. Your invitation is your opportunity to join a distinguished network of leaders.

For students at campuses without an SCLA chapter, online membership is available — see [What if my school doesn't have an SCLA chapter?](#what-if-my-school-doesnt-have-an-scla-chapter) below.

### What are the eligibility requirements?

<!-- Eligibility facts canonical: scla/member-support/products-services.md — update there first, then mirror here. -->
To join, you must:

- Be enrolled as an undergraduate or graduate student at an accredited institution
- Have accumulated 12+ undergraduate credit hours or 9+ graduate credit hours
- Have achieved a minimum GPA of 3.0 (undergraduate) or 3.4 (graduate) on a 4.0 scale
- Be in good academic standing with your institution

### What if my school doesn't have an SCLA chapter?

Even if your campus doesn't have an SCLA chapter, you can still join. Our online membership offers the same exclusive benefits as on-campus chapters — career-building resources, leadership opportunities, and a nationwide network of high-achieving peers.

If you've already received an email invitation, enter your invitation code to get started. If you don't have a code, you can apply for membership directly at thescla.org.

### What are the membership benefits?

SCLA membership includes:

- Career Readiness Certifications from partners including Google and Microsoft
- College credit at no additional cost upon completing eligible programs
- 15-Minute Career Chats with industry professionals
- Exclusive job board access to top employers and internships
- Mentorship connections and a nationwide peer network
- Scholarship opportunities

### Who nominated me for SCLA membership?

Invitations are sent based on enrollment data provided by your school's registrar, cross-referenced against SCLA's eligibility criteria (GPA and credit hours). SCLA is an independent honor society — your school did not personally select you; the process is automated from enrollment records.

### My GPA isn't high enough. Why did I receive an invitation?

The enrollment data SCLA receives is not always precise enough to filter out every student who falls below the eligibility threshold. If you received an invitation but don't currently meet the 3.0 GPA requirement, the invitation was sent as an opportunity in case your GPA improves — eligibility requirements still apply at the time of registration.

### Can I join if my GPA doesn't meet the requirement?

No. SCLA must apply eligibility standards consistently to maintain the integrity of the honor society. Exceptions to the GPA requirement are not possible. If your GPA improves in the future, contact us at membership@thescla.org and we'll extend a new invitation.

### What happens to my membership if my GPA falls below 3.0 after I join?

Nothing. Once you are a member, your membership is for life and will never be revoked due to a GPA change. You are encouraged to use SCLA's platform and resources to continue growing professionally.

### Is there an induction ceremony or process I need to complete?

No induction process is required. Completing your registration and paying the membership fee makes you a lifetime member immediately. There is no ceremony, pledge, or activation step.

### I missed the enrollment deadline. Can I still join?

Yes. Deadlines can be extended. If you missed the deadline, you can still register using your school's invitation code at thescla.org, or contact the Membership Team and we'll help you get started.

### Can I nominate myself for SCLA membership?

Yes. If you meet the eligibility requirements and haven't received an invitation, contact SCLA at membership@thescla.org to request access. A staff member will verify your eligibility and provide an invitation code.

### I already signed up. Why am I receiving another invitation email?

Invitation emails are sent automatically and don't always account for existing members. If you have already registered, you can ignore follow-up emails. To confirm your account status, log in at [app.thescla.org](https://app.thescla.org) using the email address you registered with.

### I lost my invitation code. How do I get a new one?

Contact the SCLA Membership Team at membership@thescla.org. We'll verify your eligibility and send you a new code along with instructions to complete your enrollment at thescla.org.

### How do I stop receiving emails from SCLA?

Contact the SCLA Membership Team and request removal from the mailing list. We'll confirm once your address has been removed. Note that if you are a current member, some account-related emails may continue.

### When does my membership become active?

Your membership is activated once your payment has been processed in full. If you are on a payment plan, membership activates after the final installment is received. You will receive a welcome email confirming your active membership status.

## Payments & Fees
<!-- route: payments -->

### How much does membership cost?

The membership fee is $95. This is a **one-time lifetime fee** — there are no annual dues, monthly charges, or renewal fees of any kind. The fee covers:

- Development of career readiness programs and certifications
- Maintenance of networking platforms and mentorship connections
- Funding for scholarships and other member benefits
- Access to a comprehensive library of professional development resources

Flexible payment options are available.

### I cannot afford the membership fee. What can I do?

SCLA offers a payment plan that splits the $95 fee into three installments:

- $30 upfront
- $30 at 30 days
- $35 at 60 days

The payment plan is arranged by phone with a member support representative. Note that membership does not activate until the full balance is paid. Contact us at membership@thescla.org to set one up.

SCLA also offers **Buy Now, Pay Later** options through PayPal and Klarna for eligible students who prefer a self-service checkout option at the time of enrollment.

### Do you offer fee waivers for financial hardship?

A formal fee waiver application is currently in development and not yet available. In the meantime, the payment plan and Buy Now, Pay Later options are the best paths for members who need financial flexibility. Contact member support to discuss your options.

### If SCLA is a nonprofit, why is there a membership fee?

SCLA is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization. The $95 fee funds the operations that make SCLA's benefits possible: platform development and maintenance, member events, professional certifications, internship programs, and chapter programming. As a nonprofit, SCLA does not distribute profit — the fee goes directly toward the services and infrastructure members use.

### What is your refund policy?

Membership fees are generally non-refundable per the Terms of Use agreed to at registration. Exceptions may be made within a short window after registration — contact member support promptly if you'd like to request a refund.

If a refund is approved, it is processed within **3–5 business days**. You will receive a confirmation email with a receipt. Once a refund is issued, your SCLA account will be closed.

If you initiated payment within the last 24 hours, a **void** may be possible instead of a refund — a void cancels the transaction before it settles and is typically faster. Contact member support immediately if this applies to you.

## Programs & Certifications
<!-- route: programs -->

### How do I get my Career Readiness Certification?

The Career Readiness Certification is the first step in your SCLA experience. It is designed to:

- Equip you with essential career skills valued by employers
- Award you college credit at no additional cost upon completion
- Unlock advanced certifications and exclusive benefits

Every activity you complete earns Experience Points (XP) that contribute to certifications, badges, and recognition.

### How do I earn college credit through SCLA?

1. Find credit-eligible courses in the SCLA course catalog
2. Complete all course requirements and assessments
3. Download your unofficial transcript from your Credentials Wallet
4. Request an official transcript to be sent to your college or university

These credits are recognized through SCLA's accredited academic partnerships.

### What is the Certificate of Accomplishment and how do I earn it?

The Certificate of Accomplishment is earned by accumulating **50,000 XP** on the SCLA platform. XP is earned through:

- Completing self-assessment quizzes (initial set: 2,000 XP; repeated at 6 months: 2,000 XP)
- Taking actions in your Individual Development Plan (IDP)
- Connecting with professionals in the mentor network (500 XP per new connection, subject to a cap)

Once earned, the Certificate of Accomplishment can be added to your resume and shared as a professional credential.

### How do I order an honor cord?

Honor cords are available exclusively to current SCLA members. Order at [honorsgraduation.com/order/scla-honor-cords](https://www.honorsgraduation.com/order/scla-honor-cords). Contact membership@thescla.org if you need assistance.

### Are there any scholarships available through SCLA?

SCLA does not currently offer direct scholarships. The platform includes a freelance section and internship postings, which can help members find paid opportunities. Scholarship offerings may be added in the future.

## Account & Technical
<!-- route: account -->

### How do I log in to my SCLA account?

Log in at [app.thescla.org](https://app.thescla.org). Enter your email address in **lowercase** — the system is case-sensitive. Use the password you created during registration.

### I'm having trouble logging in. My email says it's already been taken, or I'm being asked to pay again.

You likely already have an account from a previous registration attempt. Go to [app.thescla.org](https://app.thescla.org) and log in using your email as your username. If you've forgotten your password, use the "Forgot password" link on that page. Do not create a second account — contact member support at membership@thescla.org if you cannot recover access.

### How do I reset my password?

Use the "Forgot password" link on the login page at [app.thescla.org](https://app.thescla.org). If the self-service reset isn't working, contact SCLA Member Support — a staff member can reset your account and send a temporary password to your email on file.

### I requested a password reset but I still can't log in.

Contact the Membership Team with a **screenshot of the error message** you're seeing. We'll investigate and follow up within 48 hours. Email membership@thescla.org for fastest response.

### I paid for membership but I can't access my account.

Contact SCLA Member Support with a **screenshot of your payment confirmation or bank statement** showing the charge. We'll verify your payment and make sure your account is properly set up so you can log in at [app.thescla.org](https://app.thescla.org).

### How do I upload my transcript?

Log in to your account at [app.thescla.org](https://app.thescla.org) and navigate to your dashboard to find the transcript upload section.

SCLA accepts transcripts in the following formats: **PDF, Word document (.doc), or screenshot**. If using a screenshot, make sure your name, school name, and cumulative GPA are all visible.

You have **30 days** after completing registration to submit your transcript. If you cannot upload directly, email your transcript to membership@thescla.org with a brief note about any error you encountered. A staff member can assist you through the process.
