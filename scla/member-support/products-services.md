## Membership

### Standard (Campus-Based) Membership

**Who is eligible:**

> "Membership in SCLA is a distinction for undergraduate and graduate students whose academic success demonstrates their commitment and drive."

Requirements (from Eligibility page — canonical owner of eligibility criteria; update here first, then sync `faqs.md` and `programs-overview.md`):

- Enrollment as an undergraduate or graduate student at an accredited institution
- 12+ undergraduate credit hours completed, or 9+ graduate credit hours
- Minimum GPA of 3.0 (undergraduate) or 3.4 (graduate) on a 4.0 scale
- Maintain good academic standing with the institution

**How students are invited:**

Membership is invitation-based. Students at partner campuses who meet the eligibility criteria receive an invitation (email) with an invitation code to activate their membership.

## The Chapter Model

SCLA operates through campus-based chapters at partner institutions. Each chapter is tied to a college or university that has entered into a no-cost partnership with SCLA.

**How chapters work:**

- Students at a partner campus who meet eligibility criteria are invited to join.
- Chapter members access the full SCLA program and benefits.
- Institutions receive reporting on student engagement and program outcomes.

**Chapter support from SCLA:**

> "SCLA provides the same dedicated support to all campuses, regardless of chapter size, while offering visibility into program success through data reports. Our team assists with: program implementation; chapter management resources; ongoing student engagement strategies."

**Equal support model (differentiator):**

> "Chapter Support: Equal support for all campuses, regardless of member sign-ups, with comprehensive success tracking tools." — The SCLA Difference page
