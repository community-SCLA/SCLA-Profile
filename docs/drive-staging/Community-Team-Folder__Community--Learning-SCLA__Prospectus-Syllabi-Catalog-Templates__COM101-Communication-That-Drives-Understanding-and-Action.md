# COM101: Communication That Drives Understanding and Action

GENERAL INFORMATION

Course Title & Number: COM101 – Communication That Drives Understanding and Action
Term & Year: Fall 2025 – Spring 2026

Email: awestby@thescla.org

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Communication That Drives Understanding and Action is a professional communication course designed to help students speak and write in ways that lead to clarity, trust, and results. In academic, professional, and leadership environments, communication is most effective when it not only shares information, but also creates understanding and motivates action.

This course teaches students how to organize ideas clearly, deliver messages confidently, listen actively, and communicate in ways that influence decisions. Students will learn how to adapt their communication style for different audiences, avoid misunderstandings, and present ideas in a way that leads to cooperation and progress.

Through guided exercises, real-world scenarios, and practical applications, students will practice communication skills used in meetings, presentations, teamwork, leadership, and professional conversations.

By the end of the course, students will have a structured communication approach they can use to improve relationships, increase effectiveness, and create positive results in their careers.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the purpose of effective communication
• Learn how to organize ideas clearly
• Improve speaking and writing skills
• Practice active listening
• Adapt communication for different audiences
• Reduce misunderstandings and conflict
• Communicate with confidence
• Present ideas that lead to action
• Strengthen professional relationships
• Develop a personal communication framework

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will present a personal communication framework demonstrating how they will communicate clearly, build understanding, and motivate action in academic, professional, and leadership situations.
