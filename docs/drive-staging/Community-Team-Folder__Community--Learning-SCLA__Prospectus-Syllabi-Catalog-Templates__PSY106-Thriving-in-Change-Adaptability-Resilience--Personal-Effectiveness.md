COURSE DESCRIPTION

Thriving in Change: Adaptability, Resilience & Personal Effectiveness is a leadership development course designed to help students succeed in environments that are constantly evolving. In today’s workplace, the ability to adapt, stay resilient, and remain effective under pressure is one of the most valuable professional skills.

This course explores how individuals respond to uncertainty, stress, and change, and teaches strategies for maintaining focus, motivation, and productivity during difficult or unfamiliar situations. Students will learn how mindset, emotional regulation, and decision-making habits influence performance and leadership potential.

Through reflection exercises, applied activities, and practical frameworks, students will develop the ability to adjust to new challenges, recover from setbacks, and continue making progress even when conditions are unclear. By the end of the course, students will have a personal system for staying effective, flexible, and confident in changing environments.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand how people respond to change
• Develop adaptability skills
• Build resilience under pressure
• Improve emotional regulation
• Strengthen problem-solving ability
• Increase personal effectiveness
• Maintain focus during uncertainty
• Learn stress-management techniques
• Improve decision-making in complex situations
• Create a personal adaptability plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create a Personal Adaptability & Effectiveness Plan describing how they will respond to change, manage stress, stay productive, and maintain performance in academic, professional, and personal settings.
