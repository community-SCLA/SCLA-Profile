# NET101: 5 Ways to Network When You Don’t Have a Network

Course Title & Number: NET101 – 5 Ways to Network When You Don’t Have a Network

COURSE DESCRIPTION

5 Ways to Network When You Don’t Have a Network is a practical career development course designed to help students build meaningful professional relationships even when starting with little or no existing connections. In today’s workplace, opportunities often come through relationships, trust, and visibility rather than applications alone.

This course teaches five proven networking strategies that allow students to connect with professionals, mentors, and organizations without relying on traditional contacts. Students will learn how to initiate conversations, build credibility, create value in relationships, and develop confidence in professional communication.

Through guided exercises, role-play, and real-world outreach activities, students will practice networking in a structured, supportive environment. The course focuses on authenticity, preparation, and consistency so students can build networks that lead to real opportunities.

By the end of the course, students will have a repeatable networking system they can use throughout their career.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Learn five proven networking strategies
• Build confidence initiating professional conversations
• Develop scripts for outreach and follow-up
• Practice informational interviews
• Improve listening and communication skills
• Build trust and credibility in professional settings
• Learn how to create opportunities through relationships
• Use online tools to expand professional reach
• Create a personal networking plan
• Complete real networking activities during the course

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments and reflections must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will demonstrate their networking system by presenting a personal networking strategy, including outreach scripts, relationship map, and real conversations completed during the course.
