COURSE DESCRIPTION

Mastering the Eisenhower Matrix is a productivity and decision-making course that teaches students how to prioritize tasks effectively and focus on what truly matters. Many students and professionals feel overwhelmed by competing demands, deadlines, and distractions. This course introduces the Eisenhower Matrix as a simple but powerful framework for organizing work based on urgency and importance.

Students will learn how to separate urgent tasks from important ones, avoid time-wasting activities, and make better decisions about where to invest their time and energy. The course also explores how poor prioritization leads to stress, procrastination, and burnout, while effective prioritization improves performance, leadership, and long-term success.

Through exercises, real-life scenarios, and planning activities, students will practice using the matrix to manage schoolwork, career goals, and personal responsibilities. The course emphasizes discipline, clarity, and intentional decision-making as essential life skills.

By the end of the course, students will have a practical system for managing responsibilities, reducing overwhelm, and staying focused on high-impact work.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the Eisenhower Matrix
• Learn the difference between urgent and important
• Identify time-wasting activities
• Improve prioritization skills
• Reduce procrastination
• Plan tasks more effectively
• Manage deadlines with less stress
• Focus on long-term goals
• Make better daily decisions
• Build a personal productivity system

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create and present a Personal Priority System using the Eisenhower Matrix, including a weekly planning structure, task categorization method, and strategy for maintaining focus on important long-term goals.
