COURSE DESCRIPTION

Working Across Difference: Cultural Competence & Inclusion is a leadership and professional development course designed to help students work effectively with people from different backgrounds, perspectives, and experiences. In today’s workplace, success depends on the ability to communicate respectfully, understand cultural differences, and collaborate with individuals whose ideas and values may not be the same as our own.

This course explores how culture, identity, and personal experiences influence communication, decision-making, and teamwork. Students will learn how to recognize bias, listen with openness, and respond thoughtfully in situations that involve disagreement or misunderstanding. The course also introduces practical strategies for building inclusive environments where people feel respected and valued.

Through reflection exercises, discussions, and applied activities, students will develop the skills needed to interact professionally across differences, resolve conflict constructively, and contribute to positive and productive teams.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand cultural competence
• Recognize personal bias
• Improve listening skills
• Communicate respectfully across differences
• Build inclusive habits
• Work effectively in diverse teams
• Manage disagreement professionally
• Strengthen empathy
• Develop awareness of identity and perspective
• Create an inclusion action plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create a Cultural Competence & Inclusion Plan describing how they will communicate respectfully, work across differences, and contribute to inclusive and professional environments in academic, workplace, and community settings.
