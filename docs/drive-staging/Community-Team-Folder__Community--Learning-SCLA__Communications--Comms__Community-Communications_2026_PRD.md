Onboarding Process

Phase 1: Onboarding & Planning (Weeks 1–2)

Focus: Establish leadership presence, trust, and early emotional buy-in
 Primary Outcome: Members feel welcomed, oriented, and excited to participate

1. Role Rollout: Establishing Community Leadership & Vision

Goal

Create clarity around:

Who is leading the community

Why this space exists

What members can expect over the year

Members should immediately understand:

“This is a thoughtfully designed space, and I belong here.”

Key Actions

Define visible community leadership roles:

Community Lead / Facilitator

Engagement Moderator(s)

SCLA Staff or Fellow Ambassadors

Clearly articulate:

The purpose of the online community

How it complements SCLA’s broader programming

The year-long arc of engagement (even at a high level)

Deliverables

“Meet the Community Team” post or short video

Community values & norms (leadership, respect, contribution)

One-page “What to Expect This Year” overview

2. Intentional First Impressions: Creating Excitement Early

Strategy

Design intentional releases that spark curiosity and momentum.

The goal is for members to think:

“I’m excited to be here—what else can I do?”

Key Actions

Launch with a staggered content release, not everything at once:

Day 1: Welcome + vision

Day 3: First engagement prompt

Day 5: Upcoming events teaser

Use warm, human language—not institutional tone

Highlight real people, not just programs

Deliverables

Founding welcome post or video from SCLA leadership

“Start Here” pinned post

First community question or reflection prompt tied to leadership identity

3. Initial Experience Presence: Showing Up Consistently

Goal

Members feel seen and supported immediately.

Key Actions

Commit to high-touch presence during the first two weeks:

Prompt replies to introductions and questions

Visible engagement from community leaders

Model the behavior you want:

Thoughtful responses

Encouraging follow-ups

Tagging members to invite participation

Deliverables

Daily engagement plan for Weeks 1–2

Response-time expectations (e.g., within 24 hours)

Engagement tracking (who’s posting, who’s not)

4. Platform Immersion: Experience the Community as a Member

Goal

Ensure the platform feels intuitive, welcoming, and aligned with learner needs.

Key Actions

Community leaders fully walk through the platform as a member:

Join discussions

Access content

Register for events

Identify friction points:

Confusing navigation

Unclear calls to action

Overwhelming or empty sections

Deliverables

Platform walkthrough checklist

List of improvements or simplifications

Refined navigation labels or instructions

5. Community Content & Early Experiences

Goal

Provide just enough structure to guide engagement without overwhelming members.

Key Actions

Audit existing SCLA content:

What translates well to an online community?

What needs to be revised or shortened?

Create low-barrier engagement opportunities:

Introductions with guided prompts

Reflection questions tied to leadership and belonging

Polls or short challenges

Deliverables

2–3 starter discussion prompts

1 short live or asynchronous experience scheduled for Week 2–3

Content calendar draft for the first 30 days

6. Welcome & Onboarding Materials

Goal

Make joining feel personal, not transactional.

Key Actions

Prepare onboarding materials before launch:

Welcome message templates

First-week guidance

Clear “next steps” for new members

Include incentives:

Recognition badges

Shoutouts

Early access to events or resources

Deliverables

Automated or manual welcome message

New member onboarding checklist

“Your First 7 Days in the SCLA Community” guide

Phase 1 Success Indicators (End of Week 2)

By the end of Phase 1, you should see:

Strong introduction participation

Consistent leadership presence

Members responding to one another (not just staff)

Clear understanding of how the community fits into the SCLA experience

Phase 1 Post Templates (Weeks 1–2)

1. Foundational Welcome Post

Release: Day 1
 Pin: Yes (only pinned post initially)
 Posted by: SCLA Leadership or Community Manager
 Location: “Start Here” or Main Feed

Title: Welcome to the SCLA Community 👋

Post Body:

Welcome—we’re really glad you’re here.

This community exists to support your growth as a leader, a learner, and a person navigating meaningful work and impact. It’s a space to reflect, ask honest questions, share experiences, and learn with one another.

Over the year, you can expect:

Thoughtful conversations rooted in leadership and belonging

Opportunities to connect across cohorts and experiences

Events, reflections, and moments designed to support your growth beyond the classroom

Most importantly, this space works best when we all show up as ourselves—curious, respectful, and engaged.

Start here:
 👉 Introduce yourself in the Introductions space
 👉 Explore the community guidelines
 👉 Jump into the first reflection later this week

We’re excited to learn alongside you.

2. Introductions Prompt

Release: Day 1 (or Day 2 if pacing)
Pin: Yes (after Welcome is unpinned)
Posted by: Community Manager
Location: Introductions Space

Title: Let’s Get to Know Each Other

Post Body:

Let’s start by putting names to faces.

Share:

Your name and role or area of interest

What drew you to SCLA

One thing you’re hoping to gain from this community

You can keep it short or share more—it’s up to you.

We encourage you to reply to at least one other introduction. Connection starts there.

3. Community Norms & Expectations

Release: Day 2
Pin: No (link from Welcome post)
Posted by: Community Manager
Location: Start Here / Announcements

Title: How We Show Up in This Space

Post Body:

To create a community rooted in trust and belonging, we ask that everyone:

Leads with curiosity and respect

Engages thoughtfully, even when perspectives differ

Protects confidentiality and personal stories shared here

Contributes when you can—your voice matters

This is not about perfection. It’s about presence.

If something feels unclear or you need support, reach out—we’re here to help.

4. First Leadership Reflection Prompt

Release: Day 3
Pin: Yes (for 3–4 days)
Posted by: Community Manager or Facilitator
Location: Leadership Reflections Space

Title: Leadership, Right Now

Post Body:

Let’s begin with a simple reflection.

What does leadership look like in your life right now—not in theory, but in practice?

This could show up in:

Your work

Your studies

Your community

How you’re supporting others (or yourself)

There’s no “right” answer here. Share what feels honest for you.

We encourage you to respond to at least one other post—connection deepens when we listen to one another.

5. Leader Engagement Reply Template

Use: Throughout Weeks 1–2
Posted by: Community Manager / Moderators

Reply Example:

Thanks for sharing this, [Name]. I appreciate how you named [specific insight].

I’m curious—what’s one challenge you’re navigating in that right now?

(This models depth, care, and follow-up without taking over the conversation.)

6. “What’s Coming” Teaser Post

Release: Day 5
Pin: No
Posted by: Community Manager
Location: Announcements / Main Feed

Title: What’s Ahead in the SCLA Community

Post Body:

Over the next few weeks, you’ll see:

Guided leadership reflections

Opportunities to connect live and asynchronously

Space to share challenges, wins, and questions

You don’t need to participate in everything—engage in ways that work for you.

We’re building this space with you, and your presence already matters.

7. Incentive / Recognition Post

Release: End of Week 2
Pin: No
Posted by: Community Manager
Location: Main Feed

Title: Thank You for Showing Up

Post Body:

We want to take a moment to thank everyone who has introduced themselves, shared reflections, and engaged with others over the past two weeks.

Communities don’t become meaningful by accident—they grow because people show up.

If you haven’t posted yet, it’s never too late to jump in. We’re glad you’re here.
