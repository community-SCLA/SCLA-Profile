Meet Forage
At Forage, we help motivated students to get great jobs. But there's a
disconnect between early talent, employers, and educators. University
students lack the confidence and career awareness to pursue the roles
of their dreams. Employers have particular desires and expectations of
how candidates should prepare to secure a role. Educators are caught
in the middle - acting as both teacher and career advisor, trying to
bridge the gap.
Forage Job Simulations allow students to explore careers, experience
work in practice, better understand their major and build the necessary
confidence to take the next step in their career journey. The best part?
Forage donates these bite-sized virtual career exploration programs to
colleges at no cost, and the Forage for Education platform enables you
to effortlessly utilize them in your classes.

The Benefits
Our mission is to provide every student with the opportunity to
experience what it is like to work at a leading organisation. Through a
series of hypothetical tasks that are designed to simulate the sort of
work undertaken as an intern or graduate within their chosen
organisation, students are able to acquire and hone the skills that
employers are looking for. Furthermore, students feel more confident in
applying to positions having completed our programs as they are able
to better explain why they are seeking that role, why that specific
organisation, and why them.

FORAGE FOR EDUCATION SETUP GUIDE 05
Registering on Forage
To create a custom class page and view the dashboard, you have to
be registered as an educator on the Forage platform.
To register as an educator, head to www.theforage.com/university
official-signup and insert the following information into the form: First
Name;
Last Name;
Institution;
Role;
Email (we ask that you use your institution email); and
Create a Password.

---

Once registered, you will be redirected to a limited version of the class
page editor.
You will receive confirmation of your signup (approved, denied, request
for further information) via email within 2 business days from the time
of the request.

SETUP GUIDE 07
Creating a Class Page
After customizing the text on your page, you can add a background
image and your institutions logo. Requirements for the images are set
out below:
Background Image: minimum 1200 x 800 px (JPEG or PNG)
Logo: [dimensions] (JPEG or PNG)
The final step is to add the Virtual Job Simulations to your custom class
page. Click on the green 'Add Job Simulations' button under the
experiences section of the page editor. This will take you to the
experiences list where you can search by career interest, company, or
skills. Once you have located the experiences that you would like to add,
click on the 'Select' button. Alternatively, you can click 'Select All' to add
all Forage programs to your page.

Creating a Class Page
Congratulations, your page is now complete and ready to go live
to students!
Firstly, ensure that the page is up to date by clicking the blue
'Update Page' button at the bottom of the page editor. Then, click
on the 'Toggle to Publish this Page' button. You will then receive a
message saying that the page is live, and you will be provided with
a link that you can share with students to access the page.
If you need to access the link in future, either head back in to the
class page editor for your class and click 'Copy Page URL'
(above)or locate your class in the Class Pages tab and click 'Copy
Page Link'.

FORAGE FOR EDUCATION SETUP GUIDE 09
Tracking Participation
To view specific participation data for your class, click on the All
Students button in the top right of the dashboard. From here, click on
the name of your class.
Once selected, the dashboard will only display participation data from
students who accessed Forage through your bespoke class page.
When filtering data by class, you will be able to export this data as a
spreadsheet (CSV and XLSX files) to view:
Student names;
Student email addresses;
The program that students have enrolled in; and
The date that they completed the programme.

Assignment Guidance
When embedding virtual job simulations into your class, including a
reflective assessment is a great way for students to not only gain the
skills that employers are looking for, but also to:
think critically about the skills and experiences they've developed
through their participation; and
reflect deeply on how the experience influenced their
understanding of the careers available to them as they transition
from education to the workforce, and any next steps that they can
take to get there.
A few examples are provided below. This is by no means exhaustive, and
we encourage you to think creatively about the type of assessment
best suited to your particular cohort of students.
Reflective Journal

---

Require students to keep a journal during their participation
on the program. Possible entries include:
Before - any preconceptions they have about a certain
career
During - what they have learnt and discovered through
each task
After - the skills they have gained and next steps that
they might like to take towards a career.
Oral Presentation
One of the best ways to demonstrate knowledge is to
explain or share that knowledge with others. Students can
be asked to deliver a presentation where they explain what a
career in a particular field looks like as if they were speaking
to younger students with no experience or prior knowledge.
This is a great way of empowering students to develop their
presentation skills and to reflect on their learnings.
