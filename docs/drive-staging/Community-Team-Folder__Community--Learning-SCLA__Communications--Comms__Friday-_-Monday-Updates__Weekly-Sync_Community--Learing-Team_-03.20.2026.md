Whoa!  LinkedIn Just Rewired How Its Feed Decides What You See

Previously, the platform was pulling content from several separate systems at once - one tracking trending posts, one mapping what similar users engage with, and another handling topic-based suggestions. The new system replaces all of that with a single AI model that understands what topics and industries are genuinely related, not just keyword-matched.

In practice, that means the feed can now connect dots it couldn't before! Take a social media manager who primarily creates organic content but keeps engaging with posts about paid media and performance marketing - the new model understands those are related disciplines from the start, without needing months of engagement history to figure it out. The practical upshot is that you'll start seeing relevant content from authors you don't follow, on topics you care about, even when they're using different terminology than you would.

The feed also now tracks where your interests are heading rather than just where they've been. If you've spent the week reading posts about a particular topic, it leans into that thread rather than resetting with every scroll. And when industry news breaks, LinkedIn says relevant posts now surface within minutes - so you're seeing things while they're still worth engaging with.

For newer users, all of this lands earlier than it used to. A fresh profile no longer needs months of activity before the feed starts feeling useful, the model makes educated guesses from your job title and headline alone!

Side Note:  LinkedIn is cracking down on engagement pods and automated comments that artificially inflate post reach
