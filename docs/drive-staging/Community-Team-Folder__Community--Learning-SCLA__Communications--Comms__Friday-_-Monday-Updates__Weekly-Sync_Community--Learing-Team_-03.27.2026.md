NEW FEATURE: The AI Interview Prep Tool / Conversation Tool

The interview prep tool feature is now live in our platform. Please feel free to take a look at it. It is listed under our "Virtual Career Center." For everyone unfamiliar with the tool and its function, please see below.

What It Is

The AI Interview Prep is an end-to-end interview simulation tool, built to help students bridge the gap between knowing their experience and being able to articulate it under pressure. Students are presented with a live AI Avatar in a video call format. The questions and conversation are dynamic and do not use canned questions. It allows students to practice for a specific role or explore what interviews in their field actually look and feel like. The tool puts them in the seat before the stakes are real.

How It Works

Students start with the role. They can paste in an actual job description, or let the system generate a realistic hypothetical one based on their career focus, industry, or major. Either way, the prep is grounded in a real hiring context, not generic interview trivia.

Through our platform integration, their resume is already associated with the activity. The AI Interview Prep connects directly with SCLA's AI Resume Builder, pulling in documented experience, skills, and achievements to personalize every question. In that sense, "the interviewer" already "knows" the student, just as a real hiring manager would when reviewing their materials before an interview.

Students are guided to answer questions as candidates, not as students. They are asked a structured sequence of simulated interview questions drawn from three dimensions:

Applied Experience: Questions rooted in what they've actually done, based on their resume

STAR Framework: Behavioral prompts that push students to structure answers using Situation, Task, Action, and Result

Communication Style: Questions that assess how clearly, confidently, and concisely they can express their thinking.

Students receive feedback that actually moves the needle. After each session, they receive targeted, constructive feedback, not a score out of 10, but specific guidance on what landed, what fell flat, and how to sharpen their answer before the real thing.

The Hiring Signal

At the close of each session, students receive a "Hiring Signal," a contextual read on where their interview performance stands relative to what a hiring manager would expect for that role. Think of it less as a grade and more as a recruiter's honest gut check, would I move this candidate forward?

The Hiring Signal is designed to be motivating, not punishing. It shows students exactly where they are, gives a clear sense of what stronger looks like, and pushes them to re-enter the simulation with intention.

Feature Intent

Most students lose interviews they were qualified for, not because their experience wasn't strong enough, but because they hadn't practiced translating that experience into compelling, structured answers in a high-pressure setting. The AI Interview Prep aims to help change that. It's the difference between showing up and showing up ready.
