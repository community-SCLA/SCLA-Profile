# Script: The Three Archetypes of Innovation

Builder

"The ISPI classifies you as a Builder. In innovation, Builders focus on making things better by improving what already exists. Instead of chasing risky or radical ideas, they look for practical, proven solutions that bring stability and efficiency."

"Builders are methodical, prudent, and dependable. They thrive within systems, know the rules, and ensure that work is consistent, reliable, and safe. Their strength lies in optimizing processes, paying attention to detail, and providing continuity when others may want to move too fast.

Unlike Pioneers, who push boundaries, or Connectors, who explore new directions, Builders anchor organizations or teams. They solve problems with order and discipline, often preferring steady, incremental progress over disruptive change. In short, they make sure good ideas can actually work in the real world."

"Why are Builders so important? Because without them, innovation risks becoming chaotic or unsustainable. Builders bring balance by grounding creativity in structure, making improvements practical, measurable, and repeatable.

Every group needs Builders—people who safeguard stability, optimize systems, and ensure that innovation doesn’t just spark, but lasts."

Connector

"The ISPI classifies you as a Connector. In innovation, Connectors are the bridge builders. They link the stability of Builders with the bold creativity of Pioneers, helping teams move forward in ways that are practical but also fresh."

"Connectors challenge problem definitions—not to break everything apart, but to look at them differently. They balance respect for existing systems with a willingness to experiment and adapt.

What makes Connectors unique is their adaptability. They can see multiple perspectives, flex between order and exploration, and communicate across different mindsets. Connectors are the ones who can take a bold idea and make it workable, or help a cautious group see why a new approach matters."

"Why are Connectors so important? Because innovation doesn’t happen at the extremes alone. Builders keep us grounded. Pioneers push boundaries. But Connectors are the translators—the ones who make collaboration possible, ensure ideas don’t get stuck, and help organizations adapt to change.

Every successful group needs Connectors to bridge differences, spark collaboration, and turn ideas into progress."

Pioneer

"The ISPI classifies you as a Pioneer. In the world of innovation, Pioneers are the explorers. They’re not focused on improving what already exists—they’re driven to create something entirely new."

"Pioneers think differently. They break paradigms, challenge assumptions, and generate a flood of ideas—many unconventional, some impractical, but often containing the spark that cracks complex problems.

They are bold risk-takers who see rules as opportunities to be challenged rather than boundaries to be followed. Their process is personal and improvisational, often jumping into action without a set plan, experimenting, and shifting direction as new opportunities appear.

Pioneers thrive in unstructured environments, working on multiple problems at once and preferring quick, frequent change. Their thinking is stimulating and often provocative, sparking fresh conversations and unexpected breakthroughs."

"Why are Pioneers so important? Because they push us beyond what’s comfortable. Without them, organizations risk becoming stuck in routine, too focused on what is safe and familiar.

Pioneers bring the bold ideas that redefine industries, inspire progress, and open the door to the future. Every successful group needs Pioneers to imagine what others can’t yet see—and to take the first steps into uncharted territory."

Script: The Three Archetypes of Innovation

[Opening Hook — Big Picture]
 "When people hear the word innovation, they often think of one kind of person — the person with the big idea that changes everything. But here’s the truth: innovation takes a team. And every team needs three types of people — the Builders, the Connectors, and the Pioneers."

[The Builder]
 "First, meet the Builder. Builders are the steady hands of innovation. They don’t just dream about ideas — they make sure those ideas actually work.

They thrive on structure, details, and process. Builders are the ones who refine, optimize, and improve what already exists. They create stability when everything else feels chaotic. Without Builders, innovation collapses under its own weight. They are the reason good ideas last."

[The Connector]
 "Then, there’s the Connector. Connectors are the bridgers. They translate between perspectives, link bold ideas with practical solutions, and spark collaboration across differences.

They see problems from multiple angles, adapt quickly, and help others see the value in new approaches. Connectors keep innovation moving forward by making sure great ideas don’t get stuck in silos. Without them, Builders and Pioneers would struggle to understand each other. Connectors make collaboration possible."

[The Pioneer]
 "And finally, the Pioneer. Pioneers are the explorers — the ones who challenge the status quo and imagine new possibilities. They thrive in the unknown, take bold risks, and generate ideas that others can’t even see yet.

Sometimes their ideas seem impossible… until they spark the breakthroughs that change industries, communities, and even the world. Without Pioneers, innovation stalls in routine. They are the visionaries that open doors to the future."

[Closing — Bringing It Together]
 "So here’s the secret: no one style is better than the others. Innovation isn’t about choosing between Builders, Connectors, and Pioneers. It’s about bringing them together. Builders keep us grounded. Connectors keep us connected. Pioneers keep us pushing forward.

The ISPI helps you discover which role you naturally play on this continuum. And once you know that, you’ll understand not only how you innovate best — but how to team up with others to make innovation unstoppable."
