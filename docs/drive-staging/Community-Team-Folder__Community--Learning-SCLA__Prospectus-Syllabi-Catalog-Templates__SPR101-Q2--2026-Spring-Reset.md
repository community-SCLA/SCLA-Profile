# SPR101: Q2 – 2026 Spring Reset

GENERAL INFORMATION

Course Title & Number: SPR101 – Q2: 2026 Spring Reset
Term & Year: Spring 2026
Credits: 3.0
Instructor: Amy Westby, M.Ed
Office Location: Orlando, Florida
Office Hours: TTH, 3:00 – 4:00 pm ET (Schedule by appointment)
Email: awestby@thescla.org

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Q2: 2026 Spring Reset is a guided reflection and planning course designed to help students evaluate their progress, reset priorities, and prepare for the next phase of academic, personal, and career development. Mid-year is a critical time to step back, assess goals, and make intentional adjustments to stay aligned with long-term success.

This course provides a structured opportunity for students to review what they have learned, identify strengths and challenges, and create a clear plan for the upcoming term. Students will revisit personal goals, career direction, leadership development, and learning strategies while developing new action plans for the remainder of the year.

Through guided reflections, planning exercises, group discussions, and goal-setting activities, students will strengthen self-awareness, accountability, and focus. The course emphasizes growth, resilience, and the ability to adapt when plans change.

By the end of the course, students will have a written reset plan that clarifies priorities, strengthens motivation, and prepares them for the next stage of the Career Readiness Accelerator program.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Reflect on academic and personal progress
• Evaluate current goals and priorities
• Identify strengths and areas for improvement
• Reset short-term and long-term goals
• Improve focus and time management
• Strengthen motivation and accountability
• Review career direction
• Develop a new action plan
• Build resilience and adaptability
• Create a structured plan for the next term

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will submit and present a Spring Reset Plan that includes reflections, updated goals, and a clear action strategy for the remainder of the academic year.
