# WRK102: Teamwork in Action: Collaborating for Shared Success

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Teamwork in Action: Collaborating for Shared Success is a leadership and professional skills course designed to help students develop the ability to work effectively with others in academic, workplace, and community settings. Success in today’s world requires strong collaboration skills, clear communication, emotional awareness, and the ability to contribute to shared goals.

This course explores how teams function, why conflict happens, and how individuals can become reliable and respected team members. Students will learn how to communicate clearly, listen actively, manage disagreements, and support group success while maintaining personal responsibility.

Through activities, reflections, and applied exercises, students will practice teamwork skills that are essential for leadership, career readiness, and professional growth. By the end of the course, students will understand how to contribute to a team, handle challenges, and help groups perform at a high level.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the importance of teamwork
• Learn how effective teams operate
• Practice communication skills
• Develop active listening skills
• Manage conflict respectfully
• Build trust with others
• Contribute to group goals
• Take responsibility in team settings
• Improve leadership through collaboration
• Create a teamwork development plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create a Teamwork Development Plan describing how they will communicate, collaborate, manage conflict, and contribute to team success in academic or professional settings.
