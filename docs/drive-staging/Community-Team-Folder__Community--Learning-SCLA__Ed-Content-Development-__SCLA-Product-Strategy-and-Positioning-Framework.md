# SCLA Product Strategy and Positioning Framework

Overview

Society for Collegiate Leadership and Achievement

Product Strategy and Positioning Framework

This document establishes guardrails for how SCLA Academy and SCLA Honor Society are visually, experientially, and emotionally differentiated while remaining part of one unified brand. The goal is to preserve the prestige of Honors, ensure the inclusivity of the Academy, and provide consistent cues across design, user experience, and messaging.

Product Tiering Strategy for SCLA

1. Anchor the Brand

SCLA (Umbrella): Always positioned as an honor society at its core.

SCLA Academy: Inclusive learning and readiness arm within SCLA, open to all students.

SCLA Honor Society: Exclusive recognition tier for high-achieving students, invitation-only.

Language Cue: Academy = access to academic lift, skills, and career readiness. Honor = recognition, prestige, and exclusive opportunities.

2. Tier Breakdown

Evolving into a two-tier ecosystem that balances inclusivity with prestige. SCLA Academy opens access to leadership, skills development, and career readiness resources for all students, ensuring every learner has the opportunity to grow and succeed.

The SCLA Honor Society remains an exclusive, invitation-only tier that recognizes academic excellence and achievement, while offering elevated opportunities, recognition, and networking. Together, these tiers create a unified platform where every student can participate, while high-achieving students enjoy the distinct prestige of honors membership.

This layout makes it crystal clear that:

Academy = access for all → inclusivity, utility, growth.

Honors = recognition for top achievers → exclusivity, prestige, long-term impact.

3. Potential Pricing Models

With a commitment to making leadership development and career readiness accessible to every student while preserving the prestige of honors recognition. The Academy offers an affordable entry point for all students, while the Honor Society remains an exclusive, invitation-only tier for high-achieving students. The pricing structure ensures that students who begin in the Academy can seamlessly transition into Honors without duplicating costs. Two potential models are outlined below.

Stepped Pricing Model (Clean & Fair)

Student message: Start with the Academy, and your investment carries forward if you qualify for Honors.

Subscription / Annual Access Model

Student message: Affordable monthly access for all students, with the option to level up to lifetime honors recognition.

4. Brand & Experience Guardrails

Visual Identity

Academy colors: blue + silver (growth, inclusivity)

Honor colors: gold + deep navy (prestige, recognition)

Logo: same SCLA mark with tier labels (“Academy” / “Honor Society”)

Icons: Academy = progress symbols (badges, arrows, pathways)
Icons: Honor = ceremonial symbols (medallions, laurel wreaths, seals)

User Experience

One unified dashboard for all students

Honor features shown but locked for Academy with upgrade prompts

Faculty Members: PD, CEUs, assessments, community access

Faculty Advisors: add chapter management, reporting, analytics

Clear tier headers for all users in navigation

Recognition & Signaling

Academy: digital badges and micro-credentials (LinkedIn, resumes)

Honor: physical regalia (cords, medallion, certificate, ceremony)
Academy credentials = academic ift and career readiness signal

Honor recognition = an academic distinction signal

Emotional Positioning

Academy: “Every student belongs here, start your journey.”

Honor: “Excellence deserves recognition, join a national community of top achievers.”

Guardrail: Honors = added distinction, not replacement or superiority.

Sales Language

A. Academy Invitation / Outreach (Not “Invite”)

Format:

“Your school is giving you access to SCLA Academy – a national student success platform.”

Tone:

Inclusive, developmental, supportive.

Call-to-action:

Join the Academy to build skills and readiness.

B. Honor Society Invitation (Formal)

Format:

“You have been selected for membership in the Society for Collegiate Leadership & Achievement based on your academic excellence.”

Tone:

Ceremonial, prestigious, earned.

Call-to-action:

Accept your invitation to the Honor Society.

Conclusion

Separating the invitations is not only better — it’s essential.

It protects:

the honor society’s prestige

the Academy’s inclusivity

campus trust

SCLA’s long-term positioning

your brand architecture
