COURSE DESCRIPTION

Working with Agentic AI at Work is a professional development course designed to prepare students for a workplace where artificial intelligence systems can act independently, make decisions, and assist with complex tasks. As organizations increasingly adopt agentic AI tools, employees must learn how to collaborate effectively with technology while maintaining human judgment, creativity, and responsibility.

This course introduces the concept of agentic AI, including systems that can plan, execute, and adapt with limited human direction. Students will learn how to use AI agents to improve productivity, support decision-making, and manage workflows while also understanding the risks, limitations, and ethical considerations involved.

Through applied exercises, case studies, and guided practice, students will develop the skills needed to work alongside AI systems in professional settings. By the end of the course, students will understand how to delegate tasks to AI, evaluate AI outputs, and maintain accountability in environments where humans and intelligent systems work together.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand what agentic AI is
• Learn how AI agents are used at work
• Practice delegating tasks to AI
• Evaluate AI-generated results
• Use AI to increase productivity
• Understand risks and limitations
• Apply ethical decision-making
• Improve human-AI collaboration
• Adapt to technology changes
• Create a personal AI work strategy

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create a Personal Human-AI Collaboration Plan describing how they will use AI agents responsibly, increase productivity, evaluate outputs, and maintain professional accountability in modern workplaces.
