COURSE DESCRIPTION

The 20-Minute Odyssey Plan (Stanford Method) is a career and life design course based on principles developed at Stanford University’s Life Design Lab. The course teaches students how to make better decisions about their future by exploring multiple possible life and career paths instead of feeling stuck with only one choice.

Students learn how to use the Odyssey Plan framework to design different versions of their future, test ideas, and make intentional decisions about education, career, and personal goals. The course emphasizes creativity, flexibility, and action rather than perfection.

Through guided exercises, reflections, and planning activities, students will create multiple future scenarios, evaluate their interests and values, and develop the confidence to move forward even when the path is uncertain. This approach helps students reduce anxiety about the future and replace it with curiosity, planning, and experimentation.

By the end of the course, students will complete several Odyssey Plans and develop a clear strategy for their next steps in school, career, or personal growth.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the Odyssey Plan concept
• Explore multiple career possibilities
• Identify personal values and interests
• Practice life design thinking
• Reduce fear of decision-making
• Learn structured planning methods
• Evaluate different future paths
• Build confidence in choices
• Create multiple life plans
• Develop an action strategy

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will present three Odyssey Plans and a final decision strategy explaining which direction they plan to pursue and why.
