# Hidden Job Market for Graduates

**Slide 1**

CRACK THE HIDDEN JOB MARKET

---

**Slide 2**

70%
NEVER POSTED
Most Grads Are Competing for Only 30% of Available Jobs
Up to 70–80% of jobs are never publicly posted — they're filled through referrals, internal promotions, and direct outreach.
The average posted job on LinkedIn receives 250+ applications.
Applying online alone puts you in a race to the bottom — competing on volume, not value.

---

**Slide 3**

DECISIONS HAPPEN BEFORE POSTINGS DO
What Is The Hidden Job Market?
Positions filled before they're ever advertised — through trusted networks and internal referrals.
Companies prefer referrals: referred candidates are  4x more likely  to be hired.
Hiring managers often reach out directly to people they already know or have been introduced to.
Your goal:  be in the room (or inbox) before the job is officially posted.

---

**Slide 4**

WHY THIS MATTERS RIGHT NOW
The Job Market Rewards Relationships, Not Just Résumés
Entry-level roles are increasingly competitive — AI tools flood employers with applications.
Ghosting and automated rejections are at an all-time high for online applicants.
Companies still hire people they trust — and trust is built through connection
SCLA members have a built-in advantage:  a network of high-achievers and alumni.

---

**Slide 5**

STRATEGY 1
Start With Who You Want, Not What's Available
Identify 20–30 companies you genuinely want to work for — regardless of open postings.
Research their culture, recent news, and key decision-makers on LinkedIn.
Follow their company pages and engage with their content (comment, share, react).
Knowing your target makes every conversation more intentional and memorable.

---

**Slide 6**

STRATEGY 2
Your Existing Network Is Your Fastest Path In
Warm connections = people you already know: professors, classmates, alumni, former supervisors.
Start with a simple message: "I'm graduating and exploring roles in [field]. Would you have 15 minutes to share your experience?"
Informational interviews are not job asks — they're relationship builders.

---

**Slide 7**

STRATEGY 3
A Strong LinkedIn Profile Works While You Sleep
Connect with 2nd-degree contacts at your target companies and send a personalized note.
Post content that shows your expertise — even 1 post/week builds visibility.

---

**Slide 8**

STRATEGY 4
A Well-Crafted Cold Message Can Open Doors No Job Board Can
Find the hiring manager or team lead on LinkedIn — not just HR.
Message formula: Compliment their work → State your interest → Ask for a conversation (not a job)
Keep it under 5 sentences. Personalized > polished.
"I've been following [Company]'s work in [area]. I'm a recent grad with a background in [X] and would love to learn more about your team's direction. Would you be open to a 15-minute call?"

---

**Slide 9**

STRATEGY 5
Your SCLA Membership Is a Career Credential — Use It
SCLA connects you to a national network of high-achieving alumni and professionals.
Use your SCLA membership to open doors: "As a fellow SCLA member..." is a warm introduction.
Attend SCLA events, webinars, and community spaces to meet professionals in your field.
Ask SCLA alumni for informational interviews — shared identity = instant trust.

**Slide 12**

TIPS & TRICKS TO FINDING JOBS NOT POSTED
Search LinkedIn Posts, Not Just Jobs
Searching for posts about open opportunities can surface jobs that are not posted on traditional boards.
And you now have a direct contact to reach out to!
Leverage Community Chats
Communities such as Slack or Discord are a great way to surface hidden opportunities. When you reach out, it already feels like a warm connection since you both belong to the same community.
