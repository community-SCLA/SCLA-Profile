House rules

Values | This community is based on authenticity, connection, ambition, respect, vulnerability, empathy, and an abundance mindset. We are not here to perform for each other.

Mindset | There is enough room at the top for all of us, and rising tides lift all boats.

Format | Please use threads to keep us organized, vs replying in the main channels.

Medium | This is primarily an async community to relieve us of the pressure of performance and schedule coordination.

Participation | Keep this workspace open and make it part of your daily life. Ask for help and give help in return. Over-index on support and encouragement.

Tone | This isn't about being perfect - bring your authentic self, with all your flaws, doubts, mess-ups, victories, comebacks, and everything in between.

Engagement guidelines

Start discussions and ask questions generously- they're what fuel this community.

-	Our community thrives on open dialogue and collective learning - the majority of posts should be discussion starters or questions, not broadcasts.

-	We're committed to fostering an environment where diverse perspectives are not only welcomed, but encouraged - keep this in mind as you craft your posts and participate.

-	Keep any promotional/broadcast content to the designated #share channels (ex. #share-your-business and #5-share-events ).

Please refrain from cross-posting.

-	Most people browse most channels or will find what they’re looking for via search. By posting the same thing on multiple channels, you create a sense of overwhelm and mental load, which we’d like to avoid.

-	Instead, please try to select the most relevant channel for your post. If you really feel it needs to go in two channels, feel free to DM a team member.

Use the anonymous bot sparingly, and provide context when you do.

-	While our community is built on authenticity and personal connection, we understand that sometimes anonymity is necessary.

-	If you use the anonymous bot, please include a brief note on why you're posting anonymously (e.g., "I haven't announced my job search yet" or "My manager is in this community"). This helps maintain trust while respecting privacy needs.

Respect DM boundaries- treat it like knocking on someone's door.

-	Always approach DMs with respect and consideration, and with good reason and ample context.

-	Any form of unsolicited selling or solicitation in DMs will result in removal from the community - there are lots of other places on the internet for that!

Skip the emails- stay in Slack as much as possible.

-	There's rarely a need to email another member directly – everyone's inbox is full enough! Stick to Slack-native communications whenever possible. LinkedIn DMs are another option.

Search in Slack and the archives before posting.

-	Before asking a question, search for the topic in Slack to find previous conversations from the past three months.

-	If there's nothing there, check out our chat archives from the past 18 months, found here.

-	If you find similar discussions but need additional specific information, mention what you've already found and clarify what additional details you're seeking. This keeps conversations fresh and valuable for everyone.

Future Skills You May Not Know Yet — But you'll Want to start Growing

The world of work is shifting fast. But not just in obvious ways. A new set of skills is starting to take shape — the kind you won’t find in most job ads yet, but that forward-thinking creatives, leaders, and teams are already exploring.

Some might sound unfamiliar. Others might feel a bit wild. But that’s the point.

Here are six emerging skills you’ll want to keep an eye on — and maybe even start practicing.

Polyworking

Mastering multiple roles instead of one fixed career path.

Polyworking is about ditching the “one job” mindset and designing a career that’s made up of multiple roles, gigs, or areas of interest. It’s not multitasking. It’s multi-pathing.

 Why it matters: As career paths become more fluid and self-directed, polyworkers can adapt faster and build broader, more future-proof identities.

Neurobranding Strategy

Using brain science to build emotionally intelligent brands.

This one’s about using neuroscience to design branding that resonates emotionally — not just visually. Think less about fonts, more about feelings. Why do certain stories, images, or sounds stick? Neurobranding helps you figure that out.

 Why it matters: Brands that tap into the subconscious and create emotional connections with customers build deeper, longer-lasting loyalty.

Resilience Mapping

Planning for change before it arrives.

Resilience Mapping means designing systems and strategies that help you or your team respond to disruption — before it hits. It’s part strategy, part foresight, part “just in case” thinking.

 Why it matters: The future may surprise you. But with the right preparation, it won’t shake you.

Metacognition-Driven Leadership

Leading with self-awareness and reflective thinking.

This skill centers on conscious leadership: understanding how your own thinking, biases, and habits impact those around you — and using that to lead better. It’s leadership that reflects before it reacts.

 Why it matters: When everything is changing fast, the most grounded leaders are the ones who know how to pause, think clearly, and create trust.

Real-Time Personalisation

Delivering tailored experiences based on live behaviour and feedback.

This is about using AI and data to adapt content, offers, or communication to the unique needs of each user in the moment – whether it’s a product recommendation, a message, or a live session that adapts to what someone actually wants.

 Why it matters: People expect relevance. Real-time personalisation helps you meet them where they are — not where they used to be.

Hybrid Event Design

Blending physical and digital to create engaging experiences.

This involves crafting events that bring together in-person energy with digital accessibility — using intentional design to maximize interaction across formats.

 Why it matters: Whether it’s workshops, launches, or conferences — the future of connection is hybrid. Great design makes all attendees feel connected, not compromised.

So, where do you start?

You don’t have to master all of this at once.

Instead, start by picking one idea that caught your attention. Reflect on how it already shows up in your work — or how you wish it did.

Then start small. Read about it. Talk about it. Test it in a real project.

These aren’t just buzzwords. They’re signals of what’s coming next. And the best way to meet the future? Practice it — one skill at a time.
