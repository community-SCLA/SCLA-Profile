# NET202: How to Stop Networking and Start Building Relationships that Last

COURSE DESCRIPTION

How to Stop Networking and Start Building Relationships that Last is a professional development course focused on creating meaningful, authentic, and long-term connections instead of transactional networking. In modern careers, opportunities often come from trusted relationships rather than large contact lists. This course teaches students how to build genuine professional relationships based on trust, consistency, and shared value.

Students will learn why traditional networking often fails and how relationship-based communication leads to stronger personal and professional outcomes. The course explores listening skills, follow-up habits, empathy, credibility, and long-term connection strategies. Students will practice conversations, outreach, and relationship maintenance techniques that can be used in academic, career, and leadership settings.

Through exercises, reflections, and real-world practice, students will develop the ability to connect with others in a natural and confident way. The course emphasizes authenticity, respect, and reliability as the foundation of lasting professional relationships.

By the end of the course, students will have a personal relationship-building strategy and the skills needed to create connections that lead to opportunities, collaboration, and long-term success.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the difference between networking and relationship building
• Develop authentic communication skills
• Practice active listening
• Learn effective follow-up habits
• Build trust and credibility
• Improve conversation skills
• Maintain professional relationships
• Handle introductions with confidence
• Create long-term connection strategies
• Build a personal relationship system

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE
