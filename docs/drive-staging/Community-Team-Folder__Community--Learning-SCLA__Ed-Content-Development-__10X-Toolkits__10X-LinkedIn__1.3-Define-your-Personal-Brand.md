# 1.3 Define your Personal Brand

1.3 Define your Personal Brand

Overview

Your personal brand is the impression you leave on others and is something that you can intentionally craft through your words and actions. This brand shows up at every step of the job search process, so it’s important that you create an impression that helps you stand out and highlight what makes you unique.

Throughout the job search process, lots of strangers will be making quick judgments about you. In order for them to see you the way you want to be seen, it’s helpful to have consistent messaging. Anyone reviewing your job search materials (Resume, Cover Letter, LinkedIn profile, etc) or talking with you (in a conversation or Interview) should be left with a cohesive idea of who you are.

For example, if you believe you’re a creative person and creativity is part of what you have to offer as a professional, then “creativity” is a part of your Personal Brand. You want to make it clear in every touchpoint that you have throughout the job search process that you’re a creative person. That means that your Resume, Cover Letter, LinkedIn profile, and Interviews should all mention or show creativity at some point since that’s core to who you are and what you have to offer to an employer.

The following exercise will help you define your Personal Brand by selecting the keywords and phrases that you identify with and that an employer would find valuable.

Develop your Personal Brand keywords

Hiring managers won’t always have time to read through everything you’ve written in your application materials and they can’t possibly remember all of the details about you. To really stand out, you need to emphasize the most important keywords and phrases you want hiring managers to remember about you. We call these your Personal Brand Keywords.

As you create your application materials throughout the course, you’ll frequently include these Personal Brand Keywords to convey your most important attributes to potential employers.
