Getting started
Supercharging your bio comes down to one main goal: positioning your story in order to attract
high-paying clients.
In fact, you may start to see immediate results in your client funnel, just from changing how you
communicate your value through your bio.
You’ll use this bio on your website, if you have one, and on your LinkedIn page.
You’ll also use these tools as a foundation for how you communicate your value moving forward
in every business or social scenario where someone inevitably asks, “So, what do you do?”
Why is this piece so critical?
Think of your bio as a sales tool. Because quite frankly, it is.
It’s the first thing people read when they want to know, “What does this person do?”
It’s your opportunity to craft your own story, to open doors and to close the deal before you’ve
even started the conversation.
How you format your bio and the words you choose have everything to do with how effective it is
at opening doors and sourcing big opportunities.
The following is a guide to help you create a winning bio that extracts your value story and allows
your best features - and more importantly, the ones that clients are looking for - to truly shine.
Let’s get started.

Writing for the future
Before embarking on communicating your value, understand that you are writing about your
FUTURE self, not your past self.
This isn’t necessarily a bio about what you’ve done where you rattle off a laundry list of tasks and
job positions. It’s a bio about who you are today and who you will be to the person who hires
you. Your bio will answer these important client questions:
What do you do and what can you do for me?
Why should I hire you and not someone else?
Why would I be willing to pay top dollar for your services?
The First Sentence
There is nothing more important for amplifying your value than the first sentence of your bio. If
you don’t get the first sentence right, they probably won’t read any further. It’s your hook, and the
reader’s first impression.
The first sentence should answer at least one of the following questions as succinctly as possible:
What do you do and for whom?
What is your industry expertise?
What do you bring to the table?
What pain point can you solve?
What is your focus?
What do you sell?
What result can I expect from hiring you?
What is your special sauce?

Best practices for making that first sentence shine:
Incorporate one or more value verbs such as:
GROW
CREATE
BUILD
GENERATE
DRIVE
LEAD
DEVELOP
LAUNCH
IMPACT
PRODUCE
...or powerful nouns such as:
EXPERT
STRATEGIST
LEADER
Avoid cliché words and phrases such as creative problem solver, results-driven, successful track
record, proven ability, accomplished, dynamic, creative, unique, and strategic thinker. These
dilute your value, not enhance it. Instead, prove it to the reader with results and pedigree.
Avoid esoteric or generic terms like “project flow” or acronyms like VR, AR, AP or VC that are
industry-specific. Use terms and phrases that an 18-year-old would understand.
Avoid the urge to use flowery, cutesy, creative or unnecessary adjectives which may dilute your
message, value or perceived confidence.
Include phrases like “award-winning” (it could have been any award) or “best-selling author”
(could have been in any category) to enhance your credibility.
Avoid creating a timeline of what you’ve done in the past, rattling off job after job. Instead, focus
on your skills, results and accomplishments.
Don’t be afraid to be direct and to the point.
Sometimes communicating your value feels like bragging - something we’ve been taught
not to do! But if you aren’t willing to brag about your value and your accomplishments, you
will limit the effectiveness of your bio. This is not the time to be humble!

First Sentence Option 1: Lead with Pedigree
If you are in an industry where years of experience and pedigree matter to the end-client, such as
coaching, law or C-suite advisory, you will want to highlight your pedigree and/or establish your
years of experience right out of the gate. Use one of the following five templates as your guide:
1. After earning a degree from [your school(s)], [your name] spent [#] years [your main career
focus here] for world-class organizations such as [2 - 3 companies here] before launching [your
company name] which works with [target client such as "senior executives" or "corporate
lawyers" for example] to accomplish [the service and/or result you provide here].
After earning degrees from Northwestern and Oxford, Kerrian Fournier spent 25 years on Wall
Street working for world-class organizations such as Morgan Stanley, Merrill Lynch, and Marsh &
McLennan before launching Vybrante Ventures to coach leaders and organizations on how to
unlock their highest level of performance.
2. After spending [#] years in [your industry or sector here] doing [your roles or main career
focus here], [your name] launched [your company name] which does [the service and/or result
you provide here] for [target client].
After a 15-year career spanning Big 4 accounting and two CFO roles, Wendy Fraulo launched
Abingdon Square Advisors to support executive management teams to scale operations and
provide strategic support through moments of change.
3. After [your signature career or business accomplishment here], [your name] launched [your
company name] which does [the service and/or result you provide here] for [target client].
After launching, building and exiting her own $20M beauty company, Angela Randall launched
AR Beauty Advisory to help emerging beauty businesses raise capital and scale to 8-figures.
4. After building a [#]-year career in [your industry or sector here] across [pick one:
notable/world-class/Fortune 500 companies/organizations] such as [2 - 3 companies here], [your
name] launched [your company name] which helps [target client] achieve [the service and/or
result you provide here].
After building a 20-year career in communications across notable companies such as NBC, ABC,
Nickelodeon, and Lehman Brothers, Deborah Munies launched the “Let's Figure It Out" podcast
and her executive coaching practice to help high-achieving working moms move forward
professionally and create balance personally.
5. [Your name] is the [host/author] of the [best-selling/award-winning/popular thing you do] and
a [your title] helping [target client] achieve [the service and/or result you provide here].
Deborah Munies is the host of the popular podcast “Let’s Figure It Out” and an executive coach
helping high-achieving working moms move forward professionally and create balance
personally.

First Sentence Option 2: Lead with Results
If your expertise falls under the top-line growth category such as marketing, PR or content, then
years of experience and education pedigree matter much less than results. Clients want to know:
What do you do?
Who do you do it for?
What value and results can I expect if I hire you?
Use the following 4 templates as a guideline to help answer those questions and communicate
immediate value.
1. [Your name] helps [target client] to achieve [results you provide] through [service you provide].
Jane Smith helps private investment funds attract institutional-style capital through compelling
marketing and fundraising packages.
2. [Your name] [service you provide] that helps [target client] achieve [results you provide].
Jane Smith transforms dry financial data into compelling capital raising packages that help her
investment fund clients meet and exceed their fundraising goals.
3. [Your name] is a [your title] who works with [target client] to achieve [results you provide].
Jane Smith is a growth strategist for private investment funds looking to meet or exceed their
fundraising goals.
4. [Your name] is the founder of [your company name] which helps [target client] achieve
[results your provide].
Jane Smith is the founder of Alternative Growth Consulting which helps private investment
funds attract institutional-style capital.
More examples:
Erin Lopez is an eCommerce strategist who guides online businesses through the digital sales
funnel, uncovers hidden opportunities, and boosts online sales.
Melissa Marra is a growth strategist for disruptive media and technology companies looking to
boost engagement and revenue through strategic brand partnerships.
Danielle Yannotta is a growth strategist who drives revenue through digital content strategies
for parent, baby and kid brands.
Andrea Mullan is the founder and CEO of Victory Public Relations, a communications strategy
firm that generates valuable buzz for its beauty, wellness and physician clients.

First Sentence Option 3: Lead with Expertise
Depending on your industry (like tech or fashion) or offering (like professional speaker or author),
you may want to consider highlighting “expertise” to the reader in order to stand out from the
crowd and claim your high value. In the examples below, you’ll see words and phrases that point
to leadership, expertise and pedigree.
Data Science Consultant
Jessica Graves is a leader in data science and fashion, and an expert on personalization and
machine learning strategies for Fortune 500s.
Product/Market Fit Consultant
With almost 2 decades of experience in human-computer interaction, digital product
development, and design technology, Lindsay Tabas is a leading expert on product/market fit,
helping founders to sell the right product before building the wrong one.
Virtual Reality Expert
Cortney Harding is a global thought leader, published author, and speaker on emerging
technology and the role of virtual and augmented reality, as well as the founder & CEO of a
transformational VR/AR agency focused on creating innovative, powerful, and effective
experiences for training.
Operations Expert for High-Growth Companies
Rachele Haber-Thompson is a leader in providing financial and strategic advisory to emerging
and high-growth businesses looking to raise outside capital.
First Sentence Option 4: Keep it Simple
Sometimes less is more, creating an even higher impact:
[Your Name] [service you provide] [results you provide]
Lynze Ballay creates talked-about onboarding programs that result in faster quota retainment and
greater employee productivity and satisfaction.
-or- [Your Name] [service/results you provide] [target client]
Lynze Ballay creates employee satisfaction and retention programs for global Fortune 500 teams.

The Second Sentence
The second sentence should either QUANTIFY or JUSTIFY the value you’ve conveyed in the first
sentence.
For example, can you quantify how much growth you’ve provided clients (% or $), either alone or
within a team? Or can you justify your value by listing impressive clients/businesses you’ve
worked with in the past (if you haven’t already in your first sentence)? What about awards you’ve
won?
INSIDER TIP: The phrase “as much as” allows you to throw in your very best statistic, so
don’t be afraid to use it!
Here are some examples:
To date, Jane has helped clients raise more than $2.4 billion in institutional funding.
Jessica's luxury background includes design at Oscar de la Renta, data science at Ralph
Lauren, and former Alvanon data consulting clients like Burberry.
To date, Ebony has overseen the awarding of $11 million to almost 1,000 organizations and
over 6,000 individuals.
Through her 15+ year career, Lindsay has worked with over 100 startups and investors, as well
as teams across Fortune 500 firms.
To date, Danielle has developed and executed over 75 fully integrated marketing plans for
companies ranging from startups to Fortune 500s.
To date, Erin has evolved over 63 brands’ online marketing plans, increased online revenue as
much as +83% YOY, and improved conversion as much as +7% YOY.
To date, Micki has created brand partnerships valued at more than $15 million, increased
Facebook traffic by as much as 600%, drove YouTube shares by 10X, and developed messaging
strategies for a fintech startup that led to $3 million in new clients in less than 30 days.
Prior to becoming a Certified Professional Career Coach (CPCC), Beth held senior marketing
and business development roles at Fortune 100 companies including AT&T, Kraft, Time
Warner and JPMorgan Chase.

Your Second Sentence:
The Rest of It
By this stage, you’ve already hooked your audience with your first two sentences. Congrats!
Now is the time to list accolades, MBAs, Ivy-league education, prestigious companies you’ve
worked with and other awards, or even more quantifying and/or discussing results.
List any charities or boards you’re involved in, meaningful past speaking engagements, published
articles, as well as any other relevant achievements. Speak a few languages? Have a “special
sauce” that makes you particularly unique, or a backstory that helped make you successful?
Mention that too.
Stumped? Add short, 1-sentence testimonials. Not enough people do this but it can be
a true game-changer. You can even pull from an existing recommendation on your
LinkedIn page. Just remember to focus in on results, not just that you were a nice
person to work with.
