COURSE DESCRIPTION

Empathy in Action: Self-Assessment is a personal development course designed to help students better understand themselves and others through structured self-assessment, reflection, and applied empathy skills. In academic, professional, and leadership settings, the ability to recognize emotions, understand perspectives, and respond thoughtfully is essential for effective communication and collaboration.

This course guides students through a series of self-assessments that measure personality tendencies, communication preferences, emotional awareness, and interpersonal style. Students will learn how empathy improves teamwork, leadership, problem solving, and conflict resolution. The course emphasizes understanding differences without judgment and using that understanding to work more effectively with others.

Through guided reflections, assessment tools, and real-world scenarios, students will practice applying empathy in conversations, decision making, and group situations. The course focuses on building awareness first, then developing the ability to respond in ways that strengthen trust and cooperation.

By the end of the course, students will have a written self-assessment profile and a personal plan for using empathy more effectively in school, career, and leadership environments.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the meaning of empathy
• Complete structured self-assessments
• Increase self-awareness
• Recognize personal communication style
• Identify strengths and blind spots
• Understand different perspectives
• Improve listening skills
• Respond more thoughtfully in conversations
• Build stronger relationships
• Create a personal empathy development plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.
