# Step Three: Peer Connections and Crafting a Winning Resume 

**Slide 1**

Step Three: Peer Connections Overview & Crafting a Winning Resume

**Slide 2**

AN EFFECTIVE RESUME TELLS THE STORY OF WHY YOU ARE THE RIGHT FIT FOR A JOB.
Employers don't want to know all the tasks you performed in your previous work.  They want to know how you created significant value for your former employers so they can be confident you’ll create value for them too.

---

**Slide 3**

A great resume:
Showcases the many ways you can create value and deliver results to an employer or a school or in your portfolio or on your website
Highlights your most relevant experiences, skills, and qualifications
Communicates your unique personality (True Colors)
Key considerations
Role-specific:  You need to have a specific role in mind
Focus on impact:  Don’t just say what you did, tell us the impact you created.
Concise:  Keep it as short, clear, and concise as possible

**Slide 7**

Before you start sending your resume to employers, share it with at least three trusted people in your network who can give you honest and thoughtful feedback. Having other people review your resume will help you find and revise any confusing sections, catch minor grammar/formatting errors, and generally tell the story of your candidacy better.

Peer Connections

**Slide 9**

Be concise .
Keep the subject line short, specific, and engaging.
Keep the reading time for the body of your message to under 30 seconds
Say what you mean .
Don’t dance around the ask or the offer, put it at the top of the message.
Don’t use passive language, take ownership of your intent.
Make it easy for them.
Consider their biggest hesitancy and what they would need to make a decision.
Reduce friction to get to the next step.
