# PRD101: Optimizing Your Time

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Optimizing Your Time is a productivity and performance course designed to help students manage responsibilities effectively, reduce overwhelm, and make better use of their time. Many students struggle with balancing school, work, personal life, and long-term goals. This course teaches practical strategies for planning, scheduling, prioritizing, and completing tasks with greater consistency and less stress.

Students will learn how to analyze how they currently spend their time, identify habits that reduce productivity, and build systems that support focus and efficiency. The course introduces proven time-management frameworks, including scheduling methods, task batching, priority planning, and structured work routines.

Through exercises, reflections, and planning activities, students will develop a personal time-management system they can use in school, work, and leadership roles. The course emphasizes discipline, awareness, and intentional decision-making as key skills for long-term success.

By the end of the course, students will have a clear structure for organizing their time, completing work more efficiently, and staying focused on important goals.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand how time is used each day
• Identify time-wasting habits
• Improve planning skills
• Learn scheduling strategies
• Prioritize tasks effectively
• Reduce procrastination
• Increase productivity
• Balance short-term and long-term goals
• Build consistent work routines
• Create a personal time system

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create and present a Personal Time Optimization System that includes a weekly schedule, task management method, priority structure, and strategies for maintaining productivity in academic and professional work.
