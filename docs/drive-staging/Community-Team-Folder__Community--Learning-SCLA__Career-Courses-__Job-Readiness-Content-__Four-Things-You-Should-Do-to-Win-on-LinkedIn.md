# Four Things You Should Do to Win on LinkedIn

**Slide 1**

Four Things You Should Do to Win on LinkedIn
Want to use LinkedIn as your business growth engine? Adam broke it down into four actionable strategies that transformed his presence and drove massive engagement.

---

**Slide 2**

#1 Steady Posting
Post Daily
Optimal for serious growth. LinkedIn posts disappear after 48-72 hours, so consistency matters.
Repurpose Content
Adam has shared posts 20 times with different hooks. His top three posts generated 7m+ impressions total.
Proven Format
Hook about free AI tools + easy-to-digest infographic = 4m impressions on his highest post.

---

**Slide 3**

Aim For Scroll Stoppers
When scrolling LinkedIn, people skip job updates and personal stories. But a handy one-pager that teaches something valuable? That stops the scroll.
"I think a lot of people on LinkedIn are looking to self improve in work or productivity, and when you can just grab that one page on how to do X, and save it to your desktop, it adds instant value."

---

**Slide 4**

Systemize Content Creation
Adam's first viral post was a ChatGPT comic book tutorial that generated 1m+ impressions, tripled his followers, and doubled newsletter subscribers.
80%
Bread and Butter
Easy wins and tried-and-true content. Redistribute infographics with new hooks.
20%
Testing New Things
Experiments that feed back into your core content pile when they work.
Using this system, Mindstream posted 1,000+ pieces in 18 months — twice daily for nine months.

---

**Slide 5**

Don't Forget CTAs
Use clear calls-to-action in every post. Don't fear external links — the tradeoff is worth it to convert rented audiences into owned ones.
"LinkedIn is a billboard, really. I distribute high-performing content, and in every single post, we try to capture people's emails to move them onto our newsletter."
Early Days
Follow me for more content
Community Building
Let's have a conversation
High-Ticket Sales
Book a call
Subscribers
Subscribe to my newsletter (use lead magnets)

---

**Slide 6**

#2 Build Community
"If you're serious about growing on LinkedIn, you need to find people who are also playing the game."
Start with Peers
Find creators at your level or slightly bigger in your niche. Share strategies, engagement, and support.
Connect with Big Names
Provide value to larger creators. Offer services in exchange for coaching or mentorship.

---

**Slide 7**

Make Sure Relationships Are Meaningful
Adam performs two "vibe checks" before investing time in a relationship:
1. Profile Check
Well-designed banner?
Utilizing featured buttons?
Clear CTAs in profile?
2. Activity Check
Actively engaging in posts?
Posting regularly?
Getting engagement?
Try this opener:  "Hey, I really like this post that you made. I can see that you're trying to grow your LinkedIn account — I'm doing the same, and I have found X hack is working really well for me."

---

**Slide 8**

#3 Engage with Your Community
01
Build a Custom Feed
Do a Boolean search on LinkedIn, add creators you like, and create a custom feed. Save it as a bookmark for easy access.
02
Bring Your A-Game in Comments
In the early days, being in others' comment sections is more important than your own posts. Most people will discover you there first.
03
Get Your Profile Ready
When people click through from comments, make sure there's something compelling to see. Use Adam's vibe checklist.

---

**Slide 9**

How Comments Drive Growth
Find High-Impression Posts
Locate creators getting thousands of impressions per post.
Add Value in Comments
Provide extra information, make a funny joke, or ask a thoughtful question.
Rise to Top Comment
Get likes and replies to become the top comment.
Gain Thousands of Impressions
People click through to your profile and potentially follow you.

---

**Slide 10**

Case Study: Mindstream's Early Growth
1
Pre-Launch
Studied AI creators on LinkedIn. Started posting AI-relevant content seven days before first issue launched.
2
Launch Week
Messaged everyone who interacted with posts, asking them to join the newsletter.
3
Growth Phase
Found creators slightly bigger in following. Started commenting on their posts, hoping for reciprocal engagement.

---

**Slide 11**

#4 Network, And Be Human About It
In Mindstream's first months, Adam had 2-3 calls daily with other LinkedIn creators. They started with under 500 followers. Now they all have 100k+.
"It's very easy to look at these relationships as transactional, but the ones who are really going to provide you value and look out for you are the people you treat like people, and build real friendships with."
Don't be afraid to go on calls
Don't be afraid to get personal
Don't be afraid to hop on a flight to meet someone from LinkedIn

---

**Slide 12**

Growth Clinic
If your LinkedIn growth is stagnant even after doing all four things, go through this diagnostic checklist.
Find Good Creators
Stack their accounts next to yours. Are you using similar formats?
Check Your Writing
If doing text-based posts, are you a good enough writer? Using similar hooks?
Evaluate Engagement
Are you engaging in the same way as successful creators?
Assess Consumption
Is your information easy enough to consume? Do readers have to work hard to get your message?
"If you're working really hard and not seeing results, something's wrong. So take a step back, change your approach, go again, and keep tinkering until you find something that works."
