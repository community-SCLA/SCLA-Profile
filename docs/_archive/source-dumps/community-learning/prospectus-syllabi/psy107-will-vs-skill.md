COURSE DESCRIPTION

Will vs. Skill is a personal development course that explores the relationship between motivation, discipline, and ability in achieving success. Many people believe that talent alone determines performance, but research shows that persistence, effort, and mindset often matter more than natural ability. This course helps students understand how determination, habits, and personal responsibility influence outcomes in school, work, and life.

Students will examine how motivation works, why people lose momentum, and how to develop the discipline needed to follow through on goals. The course also explores how skills are built over time and how consistent effort can lead to long-term improvement and confidence.

Through reflection exercises, applied activities, and goal-setting practice, students will learn how to strengthen their work ethic, build productive habits, and take ownership of their growth. By the end of the course, students will have a personal plan for developing both the will and the skill needed for success.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the difference between will and skill
• Learn how motivation works
• Build discipline and consistency
• Develop productive habits
• Improve persistence
• Strengthen personal accountability
• Learn how skills are developed
• Increase confidence through effort
• Apply growth mindset principles
• Create a personal performance plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create a Personal Performance Plan describing how they will build discipline, develop skills, maintain motivation, and take responsibility for their academic, professional, and personal growth.
