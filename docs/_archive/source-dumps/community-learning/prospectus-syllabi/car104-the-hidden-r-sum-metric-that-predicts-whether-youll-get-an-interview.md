COURSE DESCRIPTION

The Hidden Résumé Metric That Predicts Whether You’ll Get an Interview is a career readiness course that teaches students how hiring decisions are actually made and why some applicants get interviews while others do not. Many people believe that résumés are judged only by grades, experience, or formatting, but employers often evaluate candidates using deeper signals related to clarity, relevance, credibility, and positioning.

This course explores the hidden factors that influence hiring decisions, including how skills are presented, how experience is framed, and how well a candidate communicates value. Students will learn how to analyze job descriptions, identify what employers are really looking for, and adjust their résumé, profile, and communication to match those expectations.

Through practical exercises, students will improve their résumé, strengthen their professional story, and learn how to present themselves as a strong candidate. By the end of the course, students will understand the key metric that predicts interview success and will apply it to their own career strategy.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand how hiring decisions are made
• Learn what employers actually look for
• Identify the hidden résumé metric
• Improve résumé clarity and relevance
• Align experience with job requirements
• Write stronger bullet points
• Communicate value effectively
• Increase chances of getting interviews
• Build a stronger professional profile
• Create a job search strategy

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.
