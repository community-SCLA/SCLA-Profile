# CRA302: 18 Actionable Strategies to Accelerate Your Career

Course Title & Number: CRA302 – 18 Actionable Strategies to Accelerate Your Career
Term & Year: Fall 2025 – Spring 2026

Office Location: Orlando, Florida
Office Hours: TTH, 3:00 – 4:00 pm ET (Schedule by appointment)
Email: awestby@thescla.org

COURSE DESCRIPTION

This course presents eighteen practical strategies designed to help students accelerate their career growth in a rapidly changing, AI-driven workplace. Students will learn how to think strategically, act decisively, and build the professional habits required for long-term success.

Through interactive exercises, reflections, and real-world applications, students will develop confidence, leadership presence, and a clear plan for career advancement. Each strategy focuses on execution, influence, and decision-making skills that help students stand out in competitive environments.

By the end of the course, students will have a complete career acceleration system they can use throughout their professional life.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Apply 18 actionable strategies for career acceleration
• Develop clarity about long-term goals
• Strengthen leadership and communication skills
• Improve decision-making and focus
• Build a professional identity and personal brand
• Learn modern networking and job search strategies
• Use AI tools for career growth
• Increase confidence in interviews and negotiations
• Create a repeatable system for success
• Present a complete career acceleration plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments and reflections must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students present a complete career acceleration strategy using all 18 methods learned in the course.

APPROVED ACADEMIC HONESTY STATEMENT

Honesty and integrity are integral components of the academic process. Students are expected to be honest and ethical at all times in their pursuit of academic goals.

Plagiarism — to take and pass off as one’s work the work or ideas of another — is a form of academic dishonesty. Any student found in violation of academic integrity will receive an “F” for that assignment or may be removed from the program.

RETAKE POLICY

According to our policy, a student may retake a module, lesson, activity, or course in its entirety when prior attempts have not demonstrated sufficient mastery of the learning material (score below 70%).

Unlimited attempts are allowed within the three-semester time limit to complete the course.
 Grade points and credits earned in the original attempt will be replaced by those gained in subsequent attempts until a grade of 70% or higher is achieved.

The SCLA Learning Team will work individually with students to consider alternatives when needed.
