# THK101: Critical Thinking and Structured Problem Solving

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Critical Thinking and Structured Problem Solving is a foundational reasoning course designed to help students analyze information, evaluate ideas, and make logical decisions in academic, professional, and real-world situations. In modern workplaces and leadership roles, success depends on the ability to think clearly, ask the right questions, and solve problems in a systematic way.

This course teaches students how to use structured thinking methods to break down complex problems, identify assumptions, evaluate evidence, and develop effective solutions. Students will learn how to avoid common thinking errors, organize ideas logically, and approach challenges with discipline and clarity.

Through exercises, case studies, discussions, and real-world scenarios, students will practice applying critical thinking skills in situations involving decisions, planning, communication, and leadership.

By the end of the course, students will have a repeatable framework for analyzing problems and making thoughtful, well-reasoned decisions.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the principles of critical thinking
• Learn structured problem-solving methods
• Identify assumptions and biases
• Evaluate evidence and arguments
• Organize ideas logically
• Break down complex problems
• Use step-by-step decision processes
• Avoid common reasoning errors
• Improve judgment and decision making
• Develop a personal thinking framework

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will analyze a real-world problem using structured problem-solving methods and present a logical, evidence-based solution demonstrating clear reasoning and critical thinking.
