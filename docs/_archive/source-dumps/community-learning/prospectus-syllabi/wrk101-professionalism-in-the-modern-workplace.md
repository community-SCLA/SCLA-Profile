COURSE DESCRIPTION

Professionalism in the Modern Workplace is a career readiness course designed to help students understand the expectations, behaviors, and communication skills required for success in today’s professional environments. Modern workplaces are changing quickly due to technology, remote work, artificial intelligence, and global collaboration. Because of these changes, professionalism now includes adaptability, clear communication, reliability, and strong interpersonal skills.

This course teaches students how to present themselves professionally, communicate respectfully, manage responsibilities, and build trust with coworkers, supervisors, and clients. Students will explore workplace etiquette, accountability, teamwork, and ethical decision-making. The course also covers digital professionalism, including email communication, virtual meetings, and online presence.

Through exercises, role-plays, reflections, and real-world scenarios, students will practice professional behavior in situations they are likely to encounter in school, internships, and future careers. The course emphasizes responsibility, respect, and consistency as the foundation of professional success.

By the end of the course, students will understand how to behave professionally in both in-person and virtual environments and will be prepared to meet the expectations of modern employers.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand workplace expectations
• Practice professional communication
• Improve reliability and accountability
• Learn workplace etiquette
• Develop teamwork skills
• Build trust and credibility
• Communicate professionally online
• Handle feedback constructively
• Demonstrate responsibility
• Prepare for modern work environments

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create and present a Personal Professionalism Plan that demonstrates workplace expectations, communication standards, accountability habits, and strategies for maintaining professionalism in academic, virtual, and career environments.
