COURSE DESCRIPTION

RIASEC Career Interests Inventory is a self-assessment and career exploration course designed to help students understand their interests, strengths, and personality preferences using the RIASEC model of career types. The RIASEC framework identifies six interest areas — Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, and Conventional — that help explain what kinds of work people enjoy and where they are most likely to succeed.

In this course, students will complete the RIASEC assessment and learn how to interpret their results. The course guides students through reflection activities, career research, and planning exercises to help them connect their interests to possible academic paths, job roles, and future career options.

Students will also explore how interests can change over time, how skills and personality influence career satisfaction, and how to make informed decisions about education, internships, and work opportunities. The course emphasizes self-awareness, curiosity, and thoughtful decision-making as key parts of career development.

By the end of the course, students will have a clearer understanding of their career interests and will create a plan for exploring paths that fit their strengths and goals.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the RIASEC model
• Complete the RIASEC inventory
• Interpret assessment results
• Identify personal interests
• Connect interests to careers
• Explore academic pathways
• Research job roles
• Reflect on strengths and preferences
• Develop career awareness
• Create a career exploration plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create and present a Career Exploration Portfolio that includes RIASEC results, reflection on interest types, career research, and a personal plan for exploring education and career options that match their strengths.
