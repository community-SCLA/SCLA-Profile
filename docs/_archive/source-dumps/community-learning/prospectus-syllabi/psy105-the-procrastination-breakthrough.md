COURSE DESCRIPTION

The Procrastination Breakthrough is a productivity and personal effectiveness course designed to help students understand why procrastination happens and how to overcome it using practical, science-based strategies. Many students struggle with starting tasks, staying focused, and finishing work on time, even when they know what they need to do. This course teaches how habits, emotions, and thinking patterns influence productivity.

Students will learn how to identify the causes of procrastination, manage distractions, improve focus, and build systems that make it easier to take action. The course emphasizes small changes that lead to consistent progress rather than relying on motivation alone.

Through exercises, reflections, and applied activities, students will develop routines, goal-setting methods, and time-management skills that can be used in school, work, and personal life. By the end of the course, students will have a clear strategy for managing their time, reducing procrastination, and following through on commitments.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand why procrastination happens
• Identify personal productivity patterns
• Learn focus and time-management techniques
• Reduce distractions
• Build better habits
• Improve self-discipline
• Use goal-setting methods
• Increase motivation through action
• Develop consistent routines
• Create a personal productivity system

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create a Personal Productivity Plan describing how they will manage time, reduce procrastination, and build habits that support academic and career success.
