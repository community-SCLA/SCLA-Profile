COURSE DESCRIPTION

Leading with Adaptability is a leadership development course focused on building the ability to adjust, respond, and lead effectively in changing environments. In today’s fast-moving world shaped by technology, artificial intelligence, and global uncertainty, adaptability is one of the most important leadership skills.

This course teaches students how to remain flexible, think clearly under pressure, and respond to unexpected challenges. Students will explore how mindset, emotional regulation, decision-making, and communication influence the ability to adapt. The course also examines how strong leaders stay effective when plans change, goals shift, or new information appears.

Through exercises, reflections, and real-world scenarios, students will practice adapting to new situations, working through uncertainty, and maintaining focus during change. The course helps students develop the confidence to lead themselves and others even when outcomes are unclear.

By the end of the course, students will understand how adaptability strengthens leadership, improves performance, and prepares them for modern careers where change is constant.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the importance of adaptability in leadership
• Recognize reactions to change and uncertainty
• Develop flexible thinking skills
• Practice emotional regulation under pressure
• Learn strategies for handling unexpected situations
• Improve decision-making in changing conditions
• Strengthen problem-solving ability
• Build resilience and persistence
• Communicate effectively during change
• Apply adaptability to career and life planning

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will present a personal adaptability plan describing how they respond to change, how they will improve flexibility, and how they will apply adaptability skills to leadership, career development, and future challenges.
