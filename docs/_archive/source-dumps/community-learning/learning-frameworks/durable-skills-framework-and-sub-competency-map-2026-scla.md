# Durable Skills Framework and Sub-Competency Map 2026_SCLA

Initiative

Initiative in the 21st century includes self-efficacy and the capacity to identify needs and respond proactively. Individuals who demonstrate initiative exhibit four core sub-competencies: self-awareness and learning from both “failure” and “success.” They lead without an official “leader” role or title, motivate others, and catalyze necessary action.

Sub-competencies

Lead Without a Title - Motivate and guide others; raise the morale of their group; and take responsibility for the tasks at hand.

Demonstrate Self-Awareness- Enlarge the conversation; Evaluate their own performance; recognize their motivations; and manage their anxiety.

Act as a Catalyst- Galvanize action; identify and seize opportunities for change; and overcome inertia.

Learn from Experience- Welcome and apply feedback; resist defensiveness; see “failure” as an opportunity for greater understanding; and cultivate optimism by consciously challenging negative self-talk.

Collaboration

Collaboration in the 21st century includes the capacity to work productively with different individuals and groups toward a common goal. Effective collaborators exhibit four core sub-competencies: strengthening relationships; incorporating diverse perspectives; using active listening skills; and focusing on solutions rather than problems.

Sub-competencies

Strengthen Relationships- Create networks through which they access and provide resources, information, and support.

Listen Actively- Form productive working relationships; ensure that others feel heard and valued; and grasp and retain information.

Incorporate Diverse Perspectives- Enlarge the conversation; challenge their own thinking; and maximize group effectiveness.

Focus on Solutions – Create forward momentum that advances the organization's work.

Manage Ambiguity

Creative problem-solving in the 21st century workplace includes the capacity to solve problems in new ways. Effective creative problem solvers exercise four core sub-competencies: both divergent and convergent thinking; identify patterns that may not be obvious; manage ambiguity; and apply an iterative process for generating, testing and refining ideas.

Sub-competencies

Exercise Convergent + Divergent Thinking- Generate fresh ideas and apply a process for evaluating them.

Identify Patterns - Make sense of data; recognize  commonalities among seemingly unrelated situations; and frame novel problems in familiar terms.

Manage Ambiguity - Trust the process; manage their own anxiety; and gather information before settling on solutions prematurely.

Creative Problem-Solving - Systematically test ideas; challenge  their own preconceptions; and expand the range of potential solutions.

Resilience

Resilience in the 21st century encompasses the capacity to recover in the face of stress, challenge, and adversity. Resilience in the 21st century includes the capacity to learn from experience, exhibit flexibility, demonstrate self-awareness, and focus on solutions.

Sub-competencies

Focus on Solutions – Create forward momentum that advances the organization's work.

Demonstrate Self-Awareness – Motivate and guide others; raise the morale of their group; and take responsibility for what needs to be done.

Exhibit Flexibility - Adapt and adjust to new and changing situations.

Learn from Experience- Welcome and apply feedback; resist defensiveness; see “failure” as an opportunity for greater understanding; and cultivate optimism by consciously challenging negative self-talk.
