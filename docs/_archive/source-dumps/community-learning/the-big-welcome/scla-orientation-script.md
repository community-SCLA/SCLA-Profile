# SCLA Orientation Script

SCLA Orientation Script

“The BIG Welcome” — 50 Minutes

Timing

Intro + Icebreaker — 7 min

What SCLA Is — 8 min

Why Community Matters — 8 min

Opportunities + Programs — 15 min

First Steps + Action Plan — 5 min

Q&A — 7–10 min

2. What SCLA Is (7–15 minutes) (Iman)

Slide: Our Community

(SLIDE 3)

Script

“SCLA is a national organization built around student success.

We were founded in 2014 and today include:

• 125,000+ members
 • Students across 850+ campuses nationwide

That means when you join SCLA, you are entering a network that goes far beyond your own university.”

Slide: Mission

(SLIDE 4)

Script

“Our mission is simple:

Empowering students to build careers that matter.

We believe students deserve more than just recognition.

You deserve:
 • Real opportunities
 • Real connections
 • Real career development”

Important line:

“SCLA is **more than an honor society.
 It’s a professional development and success platform.” Wether you are looking to land your first job or you are switching careers, SCLA has something for you to elevate and support your journey.

3. Why Community Matters (15–23 minutes) (Iman)

Slide: Career Success

Script

“Let’s talk about something real.

Right now, the job market is extremely competitive.

Students are sending hundreds of applications.

Many employers want experience before they hire you.”

Explain briefly:

“Some estimates show the odds of landing a job can be extremely low because of application volume.”

Transition:

“So the question becomes:

How do you stand out?

You build:

• Skills
 • Experience
 • Networks
 • Proof of your abilities”

Key Message

“This is where the SCLA community comes in.”

Explain:

SCLA helps members through:

Learning opportunities

Professional development

Connections with peers and mentors

Career exploration

Experiential opportunities

5. What You Can Do in SCLA (30–45 minutes) (Jenna) Slide 8-12

Explain opportunities and programs.

Career Development

SCLA helps students develop high-agency and durable skills for the modern workforce.

Explain examples:

• leadership
 • communication
 • problem solving
 • project management

Events & Community Programs

“Every week there are events happening in the community.”

Examples:

First Step Forums

Networking sessions

Career labs

Mentor talks

Social events

Explain that the community runs weekly programming designed to help members connect and grow.

Micro-Internships

“This is one of the most exciting opportunities.”

Explain briefly:

Micro-internships are short real-world projects with companies where students produce deliverables like research reports or presentations.

Benefits:

• portfolio experience
 • real employer feedback
 • resume material

“These projects can take just a few weeks but provide real career experience.”

Community Platform

Explain briefly:

Members can access:

events

channels

career tools

learning resources

recognition badges

The platform also tracks member progress through stages like onboarding, connecting, and applying career tools.

Next Steps: Your First 30–90 Days (45–50 minutes) (SLIDE 14)

Explain simple roadmap.

First Week

Complete your member profile

Introduce yourself in the community

Attend one event

First Month

Connect with other members

Attend a networking session

Explore career tools

First 90 Days

Join a program or experience

Apply for internships or opportunities

Build your professional network

Closing Message

“Here’s the most important thing I want you to remember.

SCLA is not something you simply join.

It’s something you participate in.

The more you engage, the more opportunities you unlock.”

Pause.

“We’re excited to see what you do here.”

Q&A (7–10 minutes)

Ask:

“What questions do you have?”
