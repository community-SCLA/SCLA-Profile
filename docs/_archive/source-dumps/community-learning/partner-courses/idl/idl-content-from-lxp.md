Research suggests that when we see ourselves clearly, we are more confident and more creative. We make sounder decisions, build stronger relationships, and communicate more effectively.

Internal Self-Awareness

1️⃣ Although most people believe they are self-aware, true self-awareness is rare. A recent large-scale investigation shed light on some of the biggest roadblocks, myths, and truths about what self-awareness is — and what it takes to cultivate it.

2️⃣ In an article written by Harvard Business Review called "What Self-Awareness Really Is (and How To Cultivate It)," two broad categories of self-awareness kept emerging.

3️⃣ The first, which was dubbed internal self-awareness, represents how clearly we see our own values, passions, aspirations, fit with our environment, reactions (including thoughts, feelings, behaviors, strengths, and weaknesses), and impact on others. HBR found that internal self-awareness is associated with higher job and relationship satisfaction, personal and social control, and happiness; it is negatively related to anxiety, stress, and depression.

External Self-Awareness

1️⃣ The second category, external self-awareness, means understanding how other people view us in terms of the above factors. HBR research shows that people who know how others see them are more skilled at showing empathy and taking others’ perspectives.

2️⃣ Building self-awareness and understanding your tendencies and motivational drivers can enable you to unlock the potential in yourself and your team.

👆 Applying a Growth Mindset

Seeing the potential in people is like discovering hidden gems; it is a testament to visionary leadership and the art of nurturing greatness.

The big question is, how do you lead yourself?

Throughout this course, we've been discussing identity leadership, which is self-leadership based on the philosophy that you can only lead others once you've first led yourself.

🎯 A game-changer for taking charge of leading yourself is to review your mindset.

🌎 A mindset is how you perceive the world around you, profoundly influencing how you lead your life. Some mindsets can be realistic, while others can be limiting, and some can become distorted.

🗣️ Like Stedman Graham said, the conversations you have with yourself are some of the most profound you will ever have. They determine the outcomes of your life and significantly influence all of those around you. We generally categorize mindsets as either fixed or growth mindsets.

The Neuroscience of Growth Mindset and Intrinsic Motivation

In the 21st century, there has been an intense proliferation of research on growth mindset and intrinsic motivation in learning. The constructs of mindset and motivation have been important foci among educators seeking to impact student learning and outcomes positively. The underlying mechanism for students to exercise their agency in acquiring new knowledge is intrinsic motivation.

🫸 Fixed Mindset

A fixed mindset refers to a belief that one's abilities, intelligence, and talents are static traits that cannot be significantly developed or changed. Individuals with a fixed mindset tend to believe that their basic qualities are innate and that these inherent traits determine success or failure.

📈 Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is a belief that one's abilities, intelligence, and talents can be developed and enhanced through dedication, hard work, learning, and resilience. Individuals with a growth mindset view challenges as opportunities for growth and recognize that effort is a vital factor in achieving mastery.

Fostering a Growth Mindset

If you foster a fixed mindset, you believe you cannot improve or change. Your life is fixed and determined. By contrast, if you foster a growth mindset, you think that you can continue to grow and learn with time and effort. This also means asking for help and guidance when you need it. The sky is the limit!

The truth is that mindsets are very changeable, dynamic, and evolving. One of the most gratifying feelings you can experience is learning something new for yourself or helping someone reframe a situation and overcome their barriers by challenging their false, limiting, ineffective, or fixed beliefs.

In his book, Working with Emotional Intelligence, Daniel Goleman says:

"If we lack the belief that we can handle [life’s] challenges, we can start to act in ways that doom us. The thought ‘I can’t do this’ is crippling." - Daniel Goleman

Thinking they can’t get out of a situation is how people get stuck and disengage. So, how do we overcome this negative, fearful inner narrative?

Reaching the Highest Level of Self-Actualization

Our need for self-actualization stands at the top of the pyramid. This is a crucial concept to grasp when discussing reframing one’s mindset.

According to Maslow (1943, 1954), human needs were arranged in a hierarchy, with physiological (survival) needs at the bottom, and the more creative and intellectually oriented ‘self-actualization’ needs at the top.

Maslow’s Hierarchy Of Needs

✅ Maslow’s hierarchy of needs is a motivational theory in psychology comprising a five-tier model of human needs, often depicted as hierarchical levels within a pyramid.

✅ The five levels of the hierarchy are physiological, safety, love/belonging, esteem, and self-actualization.

✅ Lower-level basic needs like food, water, and safety must be met first before higher needs can be fulfilled.

✅ Few people are believed to reach the level of self-actualization, but we can all have moments of peak experiences.

✅ The order of the levels is not completely fixed. For some, esteem may outweigh love, while others may achieve self-actualization despite poverty. Our behaviors are usually motivated by multiple needs simultaneously.

WATCH - Stedman Graham discusses Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

Maslow argued that survival needs must be satisfied before the individual can fulfill the higher needs. The higher up the hierarchy, the more difficult it is to meet the needs associated with that stage, because of the interpersonal and environmental barriers that inevitably frustrate us.

Higher needs become increasingly psychological and long-term rather than physiological and short-term, as in the lower survival-related needs.

Stop Fearing the Unknown

Fear of the unknown is one of the most significant challenges you will face when traveling the Nine Step Success Process journey. Pursuing success requires you to leave your established comfort zone and push into areas where you may initially feel that you have less control. You must be willing to grow and push your talents to the outer limits. That means going beyond what you know and taking calculated risks.

The idea of comfort zones is rooted in research conducted by psychologists Robert M. Yerkes and John Dillingham Dodson in 1908. They developed the Yerkes–Dodson Law, which states that performance increases as stress levels increase, and performance decreases as stress levels decrease. There is a sweet spot where peak performance benefits from stress.

Making Change Work for You

There’s no doubt about it—change is a constant in our lives. By learning to let go of things and sticking to your plan, you can start making change work for you. Learning how to control your anger, develop patience, and handle stress are three keys that will help you adapt to change and use it for your good.

One important way to handle stress is to ask for help from others, rather than carrying the burden alone. Support from others is a key to your success—no one truly makes it on his or her own.

Here are a few suggestions on what you can do to help yourself let go of the past to make positive changes in your life.

🎙️ Tune into your emotions. Monitor your feelings and your attitudes toward those around you. Don’t allow your emotions to cloud your judgment or to spark negative behavior.

🥹 Recognize that sadness and anger are common reactions to change. There is no need to fight these feelings. That only brings more turmoil. Channel the sadness or anger into positive energy by accepting it and understanding why you feel the way you do. If you're feeling sad, try doing something that makes you laugh or helps elevate your mood. If you are angry, use that energy to do something constructive that you’ve been putting off, such as exercising or working on a class project.

💗 Acknowledge your feelings and any sense of loss. Put your moods into perspective and grant yourself permission to feel whatever you feel, whether it's sadness, anger, or something else. There is no reason to feel guilty or angry at yourself for having perfectly valid emotions.

👋 Say goodbye. Bid farewell in your way to the people, places, and things that will be left behind when things change. Accept what has happened, acknowledge how you feel about it, and embrace the change that has resulted.
