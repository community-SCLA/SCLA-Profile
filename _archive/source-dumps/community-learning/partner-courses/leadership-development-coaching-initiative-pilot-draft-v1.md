# Leadership Development Coaching Initiative Pilot_Draft V1

A SCLA High Potential Leadership Development Initiative 
Small Group Transformational Leadership Development Coaching
Facilitated by Dr. Tom C. Hogan, SHRM-SCP, GPHR, SPHR
Senior National Advisory Board member
Penn State University Chapter Advisor

Introduction

This proposal is designed to develop a SCLA high potential leadership development initiative that would be value-added and augment existing programs. The initiative would be piloted spring 2026 with the Penn State University Chapter and launched on a national basis fall 2026. It involves conducting small group transformational leadership development coaching sessions for high potential SCLA members facilitated by Dr. Tom C. Hogan, SHRM-SCP, GPHR, SPHR. Dr. Hogan is a professor of practice in Human Resource Management at Penn State University, School of Labor and Employment Relations. He is an internationally recognized subject matter expert on human capital management and is the author of and teaches LHR 160N/ AA 160N – The Virtual Transformational Leadership Development Experience. This innovative learning experience is a general education, interdomain, cross-listed course which embeds small group transformational leadership development coaching sessions. Also, Dr. Hogan is a graduate of the Georgetown University Institute of Transformational Leadership and earned an executive certificate in transformational leadership coaching.

What is a Transformational Leader?

In short, transformational leaders possess the ability to unlock the talents, gifts, potential, passions, and aspirations of followers. Transformational leaders are important because they challenge communities, organizations, and society to re-imagine a better future. Below are additional attributes of a transformational leader:

They raise the consciousness of followers by appealing to higher ideals and values.

They value diversity (i.e., life experiences and divergent thinking) and leverage individual differences for competitive advantage.

They are committed to inclusion excellence and promote psychological safety and belonging.

They practice self-management and Kaizen (i.e., continuous improvement).

What is Transformation Leadership Development Coaching?

Transformational Leadership Development Coaching is an intervention designed to promote individual growth and development and inclusive leadership skills. It involves a coach partnering with clients in a thought-provoking and creative process that inspires clients to maximize their personal and professional potential. Fundamentally, it is a business relationship between a coach and a client involving self-discovery, self-awareness, choice, and change. It involves helping clients recognize and creatively address challenges, barriers, and dilemmas they face to improve personal effectiveness and organizational results. It involves behavioral change. In short, transformational leadership development coaching is designed to help clients become their best leader selves.

Small Group Interactive Sessions

A cohort of 20 high potential students based on the Penn State Chapter leaders board results will be invited to participate in Five 30-minute theme-based coaching sessions delivered synchronous via Zoom

Learning Objectives

Below are the learning objectives:

Engage SCLA members, promote student networking and the development of career readiness skills.

Provide a safe learning space designed to help develop the next generation of leaders to serve as agents of change in pursuit of a more civil, equitable and just workplace and society.

Enhance critical and divergent thinking and reasoning skills.

Connect the interrelationships between thoughts, emotions, actions, results, and impact.

Promote self-reflection, self-awareness, and self-knowledge.

Promote openness, curiosity, exploration, and discovery.

Leverage High Impact Teaching Practices

To accomplish the above-mentioned learning objectives, high impact teaching practices will be employed including:

Experiential and Peer Learning

Employs the See…Think…Wonder approach pioneered by Harvard University.

Micro-lessons (e.g., current news stories, short videos, pictures, poetry, and works of art)

Rapid learning

Leverage the arts to promote the development of Emotional Intelligence (EQ), Cultural Intelligence (CQ) and empathy.
