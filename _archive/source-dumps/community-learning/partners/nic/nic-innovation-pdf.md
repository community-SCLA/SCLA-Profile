Section 2: Learn the Tool — The Futures Wheel (15 mins)
How it works:
• Center: Place your innovation (e.g., “AI-powered mental health chatbot”).
• First Ring: Direct effects → immediate consequences.
• Second Ring: Indirect effects → ripple impacts on systems or behaviors.
• Outer Ring: Long-term/unintended effects → shifts in culture, law, or values.
Optional resource: Using the Futures Wheel — Where to Begin
https://youtu.be/heJClCCZVTc
Section 3: Explore & Choose an Innovation (20 mins)
Step 1: Criteria for Choosing
Pick an idea that is:
• Already being tested or feasible in 5–10 years
• Capable of reshaping work or access
• Bold enough to spark both excitement and concern
Guiding questions:

---

• Who could this help or harm?
• Could this close gaps — or widen them?
• What systems or values might need to adapt?
Step 2: Browse examples by sector
• Healthcare: early cancer detection, mental health chatbots, organ regeneration
• Policy & Governance: AI citizen feedback, predictive policing, policy simulations
• Finance: algorithmic credit scoring, fraud detection, microloans for underserved groups
• Engineering & Infrastructure: generative design, autonomous construction, smart grids
• Education: adaptive AI tutors, tools for neurodiverse learners, research synthesis
• Climate & Sustainability: carbon tracking, conservation drones, disaster forecasting
• Media & Design: AI writers, deepfakes, real-time personalization
Video: https://youtu.be/heJClCCZVTc
Step 3: Document Your Choice (3–4 sentences)
• Name your innovation
• Describe what it is & who it’s for
• Explain why you chose it
Example:
“I chose AI-generated architectural design tools. They create hundreds of structural variations in
seconds. I’m excited about the efficiency and creativity this unlocks — but concerned about its
effect on early-career architects.”
Section 4: Create Your Futures Wheel (30 mins)
How to build it:
• Center: Your innovation
• First Ring (Direct): 3–5 immediate consequences
• Second Ring (Indirect): 3–5 ripple effects on systems/industries
• Third Ring (Long-Term): 2–3 unintended or systemic consequences
Tips:
• Consider social, ethical, economic, and environmental impacts
• Ask: What could go really well? What could go really wrong?
• Aim for perspective, not perfection
• Use visuals, icons, or colors if helpful

---

Section 2: Performing Trend Analysis (20 mins)
Definition: Trend analysis observes patterns over time to anticipate future changes.
Tools:
• Google Trends → keyword interest over time
• Feedly → news/blog/journal aggregator
• Flipboard → visual curation of trending stories
• Advanced tools → Trend Hunter, Exploding Topics

Section 3: Reporting on Trends (20 mins)
Gathering data is only step one — leaders must communicate findings clearly.
Key components of a trend report:
• Title and focus
• 3–5 bullet-point summary

Instructional Time: 60 minutes
Homework / Independent Study: 90 minutes
Section 1: The Fishbone Diagram (20 mins)
The Fishbone Diagram helps map complex problems by organizing potential causes into
categories.
Steps:
1. Define the problem (head of the fish)
2. Draw the backbone (straight line)
3. Add categories: People, Process, Data, Tools/Technology, Environment

---

4. Brainstorm possible causes under each category
5. Analyze: Which causes repeat most?
Example problem: AI chatbot gives irrelevant answers
• People: limited testing, lack of diverse testers
• Process: no feedback loop or monitoring
• Data: biased or insufficient training data
• Tools: not fine-tuned, outdated software
• Environment: tested only on high-speed internet
Video: https://youtu.be/JbRx5pw-efg
Section 2: The 5 Whys Technique (15 mins)
The 5 Whys uncovers root causes by repeatedly asking “Why?”
Steps:
1. State the problem clearly
2. Ask: “Why did this happen?”

---

3. Record the answer → ask “Why?” again
4. Repeat ~5 rounds (or until you reach a fundamental cause)
5. End with a cause that is actionable
Example problem: People uninstalling a fitness app
• Why? It keeps crashing
• Why? It uses too much memory
• Why? It stores all data locally
• Why? No cloud storage integration
• Why? Team didn’t test on older phones
Root cause: Testing gap → app wasn’t tested across devices
Optional video: https://youtu.be/8Q1VnwcpW7E

• Voice assistant doesn’t recognize non-Western accents

• Translation app misses cultural meaning/tone
• Recommendation engine suggests irrelevant ads/videos
• Or propose your own AI-related challenge
Step 2 — Select your tool:
• Prefer visual mapping → Fishbone Diagram
• Prefer logic breakdown → 5 Whys
Step 3 — Apply the tool:
• Fishbone: problem → categories → brainstorm → highlight likely root cause(s)
• 5 Whys: problem → ask “Why?” 5 times → conclude with root cause
Reflection Questions
• Did your root cause surprise you?
• What might you miss if you stop at surface-level symptoms?
• How could these tools apply beyond AI (education, business, policy)?

---

• Fishbone = maps multiple causes visually

Lesson 20: Final Reflection Post — Share
Your “Aha” Moment
Learning Outcomes
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
• Explain how reflection consolidates learning
• Identify and articulate a personal “aha” moment or key insight
• Analyze why it mattered and how it connects to growth
• Apply a reflection framework to communicate insights clearly
• Draft and refine a 300–500 word reflection post
Instructional Time: ~90 minutes
Homework / Independent Study: ~90 minutes
Section 1: Introduction to Reflection (20 mins)
Concept (5 mins)
Reflection = looking back to move forward. It helps you:
• Consolidate insights
• Deepen understanding
• Recognize transformative moments
• Plan next steps with intention
Mini-Activity: Quick-Write (10 mins)
Write 5 sentences each:
• One thing you learned that surprised you
• One thing you learned that challenged you
• One thing you learned that you’ll actually use

---

Framework: What? So What? Now What? (5 mins)
• What? Describe the moment/insight
• So What? Why does it matter? What changed for you?
• Now What? How will you apply it going forward?
Section 2: Identifying Your “Aha” Moment (25 mins)
Guided Prompts (10 mins)
• Did a concept suddenly click?
• Did something challenge your assumptions?
• Did you discover a skill that felt deeply useful?
• Did a moment reshape your goals or purpose?
Peer/Pair Option (10 mins, optional)
• Share your aha moment with a peer (or write it as if explaining to a friend)
Mini-Synthesis (5 mins)
Write 2–3 sentences in a “headline-style” insight.
Example:
“I realized equity in AI isn’t automatic — it must be designed into systems.”
Section 3: Deepening the Reflection (25 mins)
Activity: Reflection Map (15 mins)
Outline using the framework:
• What?
• So What?
• Now What?

---

(Optional: create a visual mind map first.)
Discussion/Journaling (10 mins)
• How might this insight affect your future career, studies, or community role?
• Could it shape how you approach problems in the next year?
Assignment Twenty: Final Reflection Post (300–500 words)
Write a reflection that includes:
• Description of your aha moment
• When/how it occurred (lesson, activity, discussion)
• Why it was meaningful (how it challenged or reinforced your thinking)
• How it will shape your personal/professional journey going forward
Guidelines:
• Be specific (reference actual lessons/exercises)
• Be authentic
• Prioritize clarity and depth over perfection
