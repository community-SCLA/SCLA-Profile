# Step Four: LinkedIn and Career Connections 

**Slide 1**

Step  Four:  LinkedIn & Career Connections

---

**Slide 2**

SURPRISING FACT:  LINKEDIN IS THE MOST UNDERUTILIZED SOCIAL  NETWORKING TOOL .
Hint:  This means it is perfect for those who learn how to use it effectively.  Only one or two percent of all users actually post anything, so that puts you in a prime spot to emerge as a thought leader in your industry, ask thoughtful questions to expand your network or authentically promo your work and potential.

---

**Slide 3**

Our  Career Connections  module leverages AI and LinkedIn integration to streamline job searching and networking for students.
It provides personalized job suggestions, connects students with their closest professional contacts, and encourages short, impactful 15-minute career chats.
With AI-crafted introductions, scheduling tools, and follow-up prompts, you can easily build your network, gain valuable insights, and enhance your career prospects—all in a focused, efficient process.

---

**Slide 4**

For  networking :  Don’t just cast a wide net looking for generic meetings, instead, ask industry-specific, thought-provoking questions. Quality over quantity, always. Or, instead of asking a question, try offering a piece of thought leadership on a topic you’re an expert in.
For  sharing your work :  Frame work around statements like, ‘my first’ or ‘my biggest’ or create build-up like, ‘this was the most challenging launch, but here’s how I pulled it off’ or ‘I submitted this story thinking it was the worst thing I’ve ever written, but my editor loved it’ - you will always get way more engagement on the request.
P.S. If you simply need to ask a mentor, colleague, or collaborator for a testimonial of your work and character, we recommend leveraging LinkedIn’s “request a recommendation” tool. It makes the process quick and easy and organizes your recommendations in a centralized location while adding more value to your profile

Show where to go and how to use the  Career Connections

**Slide 6**

Part 1:    Personalized Job Search Suggestions
API Connection to LinkedIn :
Step : Students begin by accessing a LinkedIn job search, where AI analyzes the information they’ve shared (e.g., major, career goals, skills).
AI-Powered Prompts :
Before entering their search criteria, students are prompted with  personalized job search suggestions  tailored to their academic background, experience, and goals.
Example: “Based on your field of study and previous goals, here are some suggested job titles and industries to explore: [Suggested Jobs].”

---

**Slide 7**

Part  2. Job Search and Contact Connections
Traditional Job Search Results :
Step : Once students finalize their search criteria, they are presented with a  list of job results  through LinkedIn.
Close Connections :
AI-Enhanced Feature : When a student clicks on a job listing, the system automatically pulls the  5 closest professional connections  (via LinkedIn API) who have experience related to that job.
Personalized Prompts : The system suggests AI-generated  introductions  that students can send to these contacts.
Example: “Hi [Contact Name], I’m exploring opportunities in [Industry/Job] and saw you’ve worked in this field. I’d love to connect for a quick chat to get your insights.”

---

**Slide 8**

Part  3. Career Chat Outreach and Scheduling
15-Minute Career Chat Request :
Goal : Encourage students to request a  15-minute career chat  with professionals.
AI-Supported Messages : Students receive AI-generated  suggested introductions  and  requests  for quick, focused chats with professionals.
Example: “I’d love to schedule a brief 15-minute conversation to learn more about your experience in [Industry]. I appreciate your time!”
Scheduling and Communication : The system offers integrated  scheduling tools  to help students and professionals book and confirm meetings directly within the platform.

---

**Slide 9**

Part  4. Follow-Up and Rating
Post-Chat Prompts :
Thank You and Follow-Up : After completing the chats, AI generates  thank-you messages  and  follow-up prompts  for the students to send, reinforcing good professional etiquette.
Example: “Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I appreciate your insights on [Topic] and will definitely apply them to my search.”
Private Rating : After each chat, students can privately  rate the conversation  to reflect on how helpful it was and provide feedback on the professional interaction
