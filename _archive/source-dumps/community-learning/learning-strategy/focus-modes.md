# Focus Modes

The Modes

The SCLA Focus Modes

Focus Modes guide students toward a clear, themed path.  Each “Mode” should sound like an experience or mission rather than a course title, so that students feel they’re entering a focused environment with a purpose.  If Duolingo’s genius is in making learning feel like a world rather than a list, then your Focus Modes can act like themed “worlds” or “missions” that cater to different motivations: exploration, mastery, creativity, competition, and purpose throughout the Member Lifecycle.

Draft Outline: Engagement and Leadership Development Framework

1. Purpose

- Strengthen long-term engagement and retention in SCLA chapters

- Expand leadership development opportunities for members

- Connect participation more clearly to career readiness outcomes

2. Possible Core Components

- Leadership Pods (small peer communities led by ambassadors)

- Peer Mentoring Networks

- Leadership Coaching Sessions

- A recognition and engagement system that highlights consistent participation

3. Scholarship and Leadership Grants

- Leadership scholarships tied to sustained engagement

- Micro-grants for student-led leadership or community projects

- Conference or professional development opportunities

4. Program Measurement

- Engagement indicators (events attended, mentoring sessions, coaching sessions)

- Retention indicators (30–60–90 day engagement)

- Leadership participation and project involvement

- Career conversations and networking outcomes

5. Engagement Pipeline Cycle (Concept)

- Student joins SCLA

- Student participates in onboarding or introduction event

- Student is placed into a small peer group or “pod”

- Student connects with a peer mentor and leadership coach

- Student participates in leadership activities and professional conversations

- Student gains recognition and develops leadership experience

- Student becomes an ambassador, mentor, or coach for newer members

- New students enter the next cycle

6. Viral and Gamification Elements (Concept)

- Peer-to-peer invitations that encourage members to bring in and welcome new students

- Recognition systems (points, badges, or milestones) to make leadership progress visible

- Leaderboards or chapter recognition highlighting engagement and mentorship contributions

- Incentives tied to leadership participation, mentoring impact, and community building
