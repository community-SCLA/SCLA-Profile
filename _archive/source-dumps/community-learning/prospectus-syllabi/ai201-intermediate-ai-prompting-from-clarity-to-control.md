# AI201: Intermediate AI Prompting – From Clarity to Control

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Intermediate AI Prompting – From Clarity to Control is a skill-building course designed to help students move beyond basic AI use and develop precise, intentional prompting techniques. As artificial intelligence becomes a regular part of academic, professional, and creative work, the ability to communicate clearly with AI systems is an important skill.

This course teaches students how to write structured prompts, guide AI responses, refine outputs, and use AI tools responsibly. Students will learn how clarity, context, and constraints improve the quality of AI results. The course also explores how to evaluate AI responses, correct errors, and maintain human judgment while using AI assistance.

Students will practice using AI for writing, research, problem solving, planning, and creative tasks. Emphasis is placed on control, accuracy, and ethical use rather than speed alone.

By the end of the course, students will be able to design effective prompts, manage AI workflows, and use AI tools with confidence in academic, career, and leadership settings.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Improve prompt writing skills
• Learn structured prompting methods
• Use context and constraints effectively
• Refine AI outputs
• Evaluate AI responses critically
• Reduce errors and confusion
• Apply AI to real tasks
• Understand responsible AI use
• Build repeatable prompt systems
• Develop advanced AI communication skills

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will submit an Advanced Prompt Portfolio including structured prompts, revised outputs, and a personal system for using AI effectively in academic, professional, and creative work.
