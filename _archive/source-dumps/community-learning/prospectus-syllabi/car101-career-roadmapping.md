# CAR101: Career Roadmapping

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Career Roadmapping is a strategic planning course designed to help students create a clear, structured path toward their long-term professional goals. Many individuals work hard but lack a defined direction, which can lead to frustration, slow progress, and missed opportunities. This course teaches students how to design a realistic, flexible career roadmap that guides their decisions and actions over time.

Students will learn how to identify long-term goals, break them into achievable steps, and adjust their plans as industries, technology, and personal interests change. The course emphasizes planning, self-awareness, adaptability, and intentional decision-making in order to build a career that is both meaningful and sustainable.

Through guided exercises, reflection activities, and real-world planning tools, students will develop a complete career roadmap that includes short-term goals, long-term goals, skill development plans, and strategies for navigating change.

By the end of the course, students will have a detailed career roadmap they can use as a guide for future academic, professional, and leadership decisions.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the purpose of career planning
• Identify long-term career goals
• Break goals into achievable steps
• Evaluate strengths, interests, and values
• Learn how to adapt plans over time
• Identify required skills for future roles
• Build a personal development timeline
• Create decision-making strategies
• Learn how to track progress
• Develop a complete career roadmap

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.
