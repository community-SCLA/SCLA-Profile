# COM102: Career Development Conversations

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Career Development Conversations is a professional communication course designed to help students confidently engage in meaningful conversations about career growth, opportunities, performance, and future goals. In today’s workplace, the ability to speak clearly about your value, ask for guidance, and build professional relationships is essential for advancement.

This course teaches students how to prepare for and participate in career-related conversations with supervisors, mentors, colleagues, and professional contacts. Students will learn how to ask thoughtful questions, request feedback, discuss goals, handle difficult conversations, and communicate professionally in both formal and informal settings.

Through role-plays, guided exercises, and real-world practice, students will develop confidence in speaking about their career path and learn how to use conversations to create opportunities rather than wait for them.

By the end of the course, students will have a structured approach to career conversations that can be used throughout their professional life.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the importance of career conversations
• Learn how to prepare for professional discussions
• Practice asking for feedback
• Develop confidence speaking with supervisors and mentors
• Learn how to discuss career goals clearly
• Practice difficult conversations respectfully
• Improve listening and response skills
• Build professional relationships through communication
• Use conversations to create opportunities
• Develop a personal career conversation plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will prepare and present a complete career conversation strategy, including scripts, preparation methods, and real examples of professional conversations conducted during the course.
