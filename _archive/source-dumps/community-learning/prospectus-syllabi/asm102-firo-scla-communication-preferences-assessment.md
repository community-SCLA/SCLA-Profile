COURSE DESCRIPTION

FIRO-SCLA Communication Preferences Assessment is a self-awareness and interpersonal development course designed to help students understand their communication style, social needs, and interaction patterns using the FIRO framework adapted for the SCLA learning model. Understanding how individuals prefer to communicate, collaborate, and respond to others is essential for success in academic, professional, and leadership environments.

This course guides students through the FIRO-based assessment process, helping them identify preferences related to inclusion, control, and openness in communication. Students will learn how these preferences influence teamwork, leadership, conflict resolution, and professional relationships. The course emphasizes awareness first, followed by practical strategies for adapting communication to different situations and people.

Through guided assessments, reflection exercises, and real-world scenarios, students will learn how to work more effectively with others, reduce misunderstandings, and build stronger professional relationships. The course also helps students recognize both strengths and blind spots in their interaction style.

By the end of the course, students will have a personalized communication profile and a plan for improving effectiveness in group, leadership, and career settings.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Complete the FIRO-SCLA communication assessment
• Understand personal communication preferences
• Learn the concepts of inclusion, control, and openness
• Identify strengths and blind spots
• Recognize different communication styles
• Improve teamwork skills
• Reduce misunderstandings
• Adapt communication to different situations
• Strengthen leadership awareness
• Create a personal communication development plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.
