# AI305: Work in the Age of Intelligence

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Work in the Age of Intelligence is a future-of-work course designed to help students understand how artificial intelligence, automation, and rapid technological change are transforming careers, organizations, and the skills needed to succeed. As intelligent systems become more capable, the value of human judgment, creativity, communication, and adaptability continues to grow.

This course explores how work is evolving in the age of AI and what individuals must do to remain relevant, competitive, and effective. Students will examine how technology changes job roles, how new opportunities are created, and how professionals can position themselves for long-term success in an unpredictable environment.

Through discussion, reflection, and applied activities, students will learn how to think strategically about their future, develop human-centered skills, and build the ability to learn continuously. By the end of the course, students will have a clear understanding of how to navigate careers in the age of intelligence.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the future of work
• Learn how AI is changing careers
• Identify human skills that matter most
• Develop adaptability and resilience
• Think strategically about career growth
• Understand automation and opportunity
• Strengthen decision-making skills
• Improve learning agility
• Build long-term career awareness
• Create a future-ready career plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create a Future Career Strategy describing how they will adapt to technological change, develop human-centered skills, and remain effective in the age of intelligence.
