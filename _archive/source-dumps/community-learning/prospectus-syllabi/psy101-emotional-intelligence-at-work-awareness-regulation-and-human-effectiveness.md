COURSE DESCRIPTION

Emotional Intelligence at Work: Awareness, Regulation, and Human Effectiveness is a professional development course designed to help students understand and manage emotions in academic, workplace, and leadership environments. Emotional intelligence is one of the most important human skills in the modern workplace, especially in a world increasingly influenced by automation and artificial intelligence.

This course focuses on self-awareness, emotional regulation, empathy, communication, and interpersonal effectiveness. Students will learn how emotions affect decision-making, relationships, motivation, and performance. The course also teaches practical strategies for handling stress, managing conflict, staying focused, and responding thoughtfully instead of reacting impulsively.

Through reflection exercises, real-world scenarios, and guided practice, students will develop the ability to remain calm under pressure, communicate with clarity, and work effectively with others. These skills are essential for leadership, teamwork, career advancement, and long-term success.

By the end of the course, students will have a personal emotional intelligence framework that helps them operate with greater awareness, control, and effectiveness in professional and personal situations.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand emotional intelligence and why it matters
• Increase self-awareness
• Learn emotional regulation techniques
• Improve stress management
• Develop empathy skills
• Strengthen communication awareness
• Respond effectively in difficult situations
• Improve teamwork and collaboration
• Build professional maturity
• Create a personal emotional intelligence plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE
