COURSE DESCRIPTION

The Imposter Syndrome Reset is a personal development course designed to help students recognize, understand, and overcome feelings of self-doubt, insecurity, and the belief that they do not belong in academic, professional, or leadership environments. Many capable individuals experience imposter syndrome, especially when entering new roles, facing challenges, or working toward ambitious goals.

This course explores the psychological patterns behind imposter syndrome and teaches practical strategies for building confidence, developing self-trust, and maintaining a strong sense of identity even in high-pressure situations. Students will learn how thoughts, habits, and past experiences influence confidence and performance.

Through reflections, exercises, and guided activities, students will practice reframing negative thinking, setting realistic standards, and developing a growth mindset. By the end of the course, students will have a clear strategy for managing self-doubt and performing with confidence in school, work, and leadership settings.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand what imposter syndrome is
• Recognize personal self-doubt patterns
• Learn how mindset affects performance
• Build self-confidence
• Practice positive self-talk
• Develop emotional awareness
• Reduce fear of failure
• Strengthen personal identity
• Improve resilience
• Create a confidence development plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Students must complete assignments with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will create a Personal Confidence Plan describing how they will manage self-doubt, build self-trust, and maintain confidence in academic, professional, and leadership situations.
