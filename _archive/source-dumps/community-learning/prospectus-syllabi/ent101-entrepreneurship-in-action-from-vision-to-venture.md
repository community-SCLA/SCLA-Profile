# ENT101: Entrepreneurship in Action: From Vision to Venture

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Entrepreneurship in Action: From Vision to Venture is a practical course designed to help students turn ideas into real opportunities. In today’s rapidly changing world, entrepreneurial thinking is valuable not only for starting businesses, but also for solving problems, creating innovation, and leading projects inside organizations.

This course guides students through the process of developing an idea, testing it, and building a clear plan for how it could become a real venture, project, or initiative. Students will learn how to identify problems worth solving, evaluate opportunities, design solutions, and communicate ideas effectively to others.

The course focuses on action, not just theory. Students will work step-by-step to move from vision to execution using structured tools such as idea validation, basic business planning, audience analysis, and presentation skills. Emphasis is placed on creativity, persistence, and learning from feedback.

By the end of the course, students will have a developed venture concept and a clear plan showing how the idea could be implemented in the real world.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the entrepreneurial mindset
• Identify problems worth solving
• Generate and evaluate ideas
• Learn basic venture planning
• Understand value creation
• Practice creative problem solving
• Learn how to test ideas
• Develop communication and pitching skills
• Build confidence in taking initiative
• Create a venture or project plan

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will present a Venture Plan that includes a problem statement, proposed solution, value explanation, and action plan for how the idea could be developed in the real world.
