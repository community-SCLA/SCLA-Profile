COURSE DESCRIPTION

Mastering Focus with Pomodoro is a productivity and performance course designed to help students improve concentration, manage time effectively, and complete work with greater consistency. In a world full of distractions, the ability to focus deeply is an essential skill for academic success, career growth, and leadership development.

This course introduces the Pomodoro Technique as a structured method for working in short, focused intervals followed by breaks. Students will learn how to plan tasks, reduce distractions, maintain mental energy, and build habits that support sustained productivity. The course also explores the science of attention, motivation, and cognitive load to help students understand why focus is difficult and how to improve it.

Through practice sessions, reflections, and structured exercises, students will build a personal system for managing time, completing assignments, and staying engaged even during challenging work. The course emphasizes discipline, self-awareness, and consistency as key factors in long-term success.

By the end of the course, students will have a reliable focus system they can use for studying, working, and managing complex projects.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand how attention and focus work
• Learn the Pomodoro Technique
• Practice working in focused intervals
• Reduce distractions
• Improve time management
• Build productive habits
• Increase work completion rate
• Manage mental fatigue
• Stay motivated during long tasks
• Create a personal focus system

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will design and present a Personal Focus System that includes a Pomodoro schedule, distraction control plan, task management method, and strategies for maintaining productivity during academic or professional work.
