# THK102: Creative & Systems Thinking for Complex Challenges

Course Title & Number: THK102 – Creative & Systems Thinking for Complex Challenges

COURSE DESCRIPTION

Creative & Systems Thinking for Complex Challenges is a problem-solving and leadership course designed to help students think more effectively in situations that involve uncertainty, multiple variables, and real-world complexity. In modern academic, professional, and leadership environments, simple answers are often not enough. Success requires the ability to see patterns, understand systems, and develop creative solutions.

This course introduces students to creative thinking techniques and systems thinking frameworks that help them analyze problems, generate ideas, and make better decisions. Students will learn how to break down complex challenges, understand how different parts connect, and design solutions that work in real situations.

Through case studies, exercises, group activities, and real-world applications, students will practice thinking in new ways and develop the ability to approach problems with flexibility, creativity, and structure.

By the end of the course, students will have a repeatable process for solving complex problems and making thoughtful decisions in academic, career, and leadership settings.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Students will:

• Understand the difference between simple and complex problems
• Learn creative thinking techniques
• Practice brainstorming and idea generation
• Understand systems thinking principles
• Identify patterns and relationships
• Break down complex challenges
• Evaluate possible solutions
• Improve decision-making skills
• Work through real-world scenarios
• Develop a personal problem-solving framework

Course work requires approximately 4–6 hours per week for 12 weeks.
Assignments must be completed with a score of 80% or higher.

COURSE TIMELINE / OUTLINE

Capstone Project:
Students will analyze a complex real-world challenge and present a solution using creative thinking and systems thinking tools learned in the course.
