## Who SCLA Is

The Society for Collegiate Leadership & Achievement (SCLA) is the leading College-to-Career Honor Society. SCLA identifies high-achieving students at 850+ partner campuses, invites them based on GPA (3.0+ undergrad, 3.4+ grad), and gives them the tools, credentials, and connections to build purpose-driven careers.

- **100,000+ members** across 850+ campuses
- **Founded:** 2012–2014 development; first member March 2015
- **Founder:** Pat Sidhu
- **HQ:** 1775 Tysons Blvd, 5th Floor, Tysons, VA 22102
- **Membership fee:** $95 one-time

Core offerings: Career Readiness Certification, ISPI strengths assessment, 550+ skill development videos, AI-powered Career Hub, CEO Speaker Series, peer networking, scholarship opportunities.

## How the Team Works

**Tools:** Slack (internal comms), Gmail (external), Google Drive (all documents), Zoom (meetings, with Gemini auto-notes), Canva (Team Projects tracker), Claude Pro (AI assistance — recently adopted)

**No task tracker** — work is coordinated via Slack + Gmail. The Team Projects tracker in Canva is the closest thing; if a task isn't there, it doesn't officially exist for project reporting.

**Meeting cadence:** Community Team Mondays, every Monday at 4:00 PM CDT, ~60 min via Zoom. Amy sets the agenda. Gemini takes notes automatically. Starts with a warm-up (music, personal check-ins) — this is intentional.

**Document home:** Community Google Drive. Finished and in-progress work goes there. When a doc is added, ping Kierra in Slack so she can ingest it into Claude.

## This Project

`scla-profile` is the community team's internal knowledge base. It captures SCLA's brand identity, org knowledge, workflows, and a source of truth the whole team can work from — built from the website, meeting notes, and stakeholder input via an automated pipeline.

Pipeline outputs live in `scla/`. Human-maintained context lives here in `context/`.
