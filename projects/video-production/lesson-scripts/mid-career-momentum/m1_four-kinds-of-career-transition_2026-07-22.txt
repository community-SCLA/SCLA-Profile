Lesson: Mod 1 — Define Your Next Move
Title: Four Kinds of Career Transition

You've accumulated years of experience—projects delivered, teams supported, stakeholders managed. But your next step probably feels less clear than your first job did.

[On screen: Title text "Define Your Next Move" with sublabel "Promotion · Lateral Move · Role Redesign." Background: subtle grid or path motif.]

In this module, we're going to simplify your options into three common paths for mid-career momentum: promotion in your current lane, a lateral move to a better-fit role, or redesigning your current role to better match how you work and what you care about.

[Graphic: above graphic]

Let's start with Reinvention. A reinvention path focuses on deepening your influence and scope where you already are. This could mean leading larger initiatives, supervising others, taking on more strategic responsibility, or moving into a higher-level title in the same general area. Promotion is often the right path when you still believe in the setting you're in, but you want more responsibility, greater visibility, or stronger recognition.

[Graphic: Reinvention tile enlarges; overlay bullets: "More influence," "Higher scope," "Strategic responsibility," "Greater visibility." Optional simple bar chart trending upward.]

Next is the Rebuild. A rebuild move doesn't necessarily mean "sideways" in a negative sense. It often means moving into a better-fit role, team, department, or organization that allows your strengths to show up more clearly. Sometimes a lateral-type rebuild move is the smartest long-term growth choice because it gives you better alignment, stronger mentorship, or more meaningful work.

[Graphic: Rebuild tile enlarges; an animated arrow moves horizontally between two "role" boxes labeled "Current Team" and "New Team," with subtle emphasis on "Better Fit." Icons for "Alignment," "Mentorship," "Meaningful Work."]

The third path is the Pivot. This is about reshaping your current role without leaving entirely. You may not need a new title or employer to gain momentum. Sometimes what you really need is a change in focus, boundaries, projects, or responsibilities. Pivots might involve negotiating for different initiatives, reducing low-value tasks, stepping into more strategic work, or creating a hybrid role around your strengths.

[Graphic: Pivot tile enlarges; a block labeled "Current Role" smoothly rearranges into blocks labeled "More strategic work," "Fewer low-value tasks," "New projects."]

The final path is the Forced Reinvention — not necessarily of your own doing, but one that allows you to explore other options.

None of these paths is "better" in general. The right path is the one that aligns with your values, energizes you, and fits your life constraints right now.

[On screen: Simple Venn diagram of "Values," "Energy," "Constraints," with a center area labeled "Right Path for Now."]

In this module, you'll reflect on your current role, your desired role, and what success would look like over the next year. Then you'll use an AI activity to analyze the projects that energize you—so your choice is grounded in evidence, not just impulse. Finally, you'll capture your direction in a simple Next Move Statement for the next 6–12 months.

[Graphic: A three-step vertical list animation: 1) Reflect, 2) Analyze with AI, 3) Write Next Move Statement. Each step lights up as Ann names it.]

You don't need to solve your entire career here. You only need to decide which of these three paths makes the most sense for you right now. That clarity is what creates momentum.
