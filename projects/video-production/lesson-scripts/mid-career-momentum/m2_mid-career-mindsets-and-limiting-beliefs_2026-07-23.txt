===== SCRIPT (verbatim from dashboard) =====

Let's talk honestly about mid‑career mindset—especially the beliefs that quietly keep people stuck.

[On screen: Title text “Mid‑Career Mindset” with a small sublabel “Reframing Limiting Beliefs.” Background: subtle gradient or clean neutral.]

Some common beliefs sound like this: “It’s too late to change.” “If I speak up, I’ll seem ungrateful.” “If I don’t grab the first opportunity, I’ll miss my chance.” These thoughts feel protective, but they often keep you playing smaller than your experience allows.

[Graphic: Three speech bubbles appearing one by one with those phrases; each fades slightly as the script highlights them as limiting.]

Let’s reframe. First, mid‑career is a leverage point. You’ve built skills, credibility, and relationships. That gives you more—not fewer—options. The challenge isn’t a lack of opportunity; it’s learning to choose intentionally.

[Motion graphic: A simple “scale” or “lever” animation showing experience, skills, relationships on one side, “options” expanding on the other.]

Second, instead of thinking in terms of one big, all‑or‑nothing pivot, we’ll think in terms of **career experiments**. That might mean testing a leadership role on a project, trying a hybrid responsibilities arrangement, or exploring an adjacent function with a small assignment before you commit fully.

[Graphic: Three small experiment cards: “Lead one project,” “Try hybrid role,” “Explore adjacent function.” Each with a small test‑tube icon to reinforce the “experiment” idea.]

Finally, we’ll use **re‑design thinking**. Instead of viewing your role as fixed, you’ll look for ways to adjust projects, boundaries, responsibilities, and skills so your work better matches who you are now.

[Graphic: A simple wireframe illustrating a job description “box” morphing into a more flexible shape with new blocks labeled “New projects,” “Clearer boundaries,” “Higher‑value work.”]

This track will help you notice when a belief is limiting you, then replace it with small, practical experiments that build confidence instead of fear. For now, your task is simple: acknowledge where you feel stuck, and commit to exploring one new path rather than staying in neutral.

[On screen: Text prompt: “Where do you feel stuck?” followed by “What small experiment could you try next?” with a subtle animated underline.]
