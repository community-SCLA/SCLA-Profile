Lesson: Mod 2 — Reposition Your Experience
Title: From History to Signal: Why Re-positioning Matters

By mid-career, your resume and professional profiles often turn into archives—a long record of everything you've done. That makes it harder for others to quickly see where you're heading next and why you're ready for your next role.

[On screen: Title text "From History to Signal" over a split visual—left side a long scroll of grey "past jobs," right side a clean, focused profile summary.]

When a manager, sponsor, or hiring committee looks at your materials, they're scanning for a few things:
* What kinds of results you've delivered.
* The level of scope you've handled.
* How well your experience lines up with the role you want next.

[Graphic: Simple checklist graphic labeled "Results," "Scope," "Fit for Next Role" with checkmarks appearing.]

If your resume reads like a task list—"Responsible for X," "Duties included Y"—they have to work much harder to see your impact. If your LinkedIn headline describes only your current role, they may never realize that you're aiming for a different kind of future.

[Motion graphic: A bullet reading "Responsible for managing projects" morphs into "Led cross-functional project that reduced onboarding time by 30%."]

In this module, we'll focus on three shifts:
1. Discovering the experiences that best support your future trajectory.
2. Rewriting key resume bullets to show results, scope, and impact.
3. Updating your LinkedIn headline and summary so they speak to your future-you, not just your past-you.

[Graphic: Three tiles labeled "Choose Experiences," "Rewrite Bullets," "Update LinkedIn," appearing one by one.]

The goal is not to rewrite your entire history. It's to curate and highlight the parts of your story that make your Next Move Statement believable and compelling. When you reposition your experience, you make it easier for decision-makers to connect the dots from who you are now to who you're becoming.

[On screen: Visual of a dotted line connecting "Current Role" to "Target Role," with a few key experiences highlighted along the path.]

As you work through this module, keep one question in mind:
"If someone only saw my resume and LinkedIn profile, would they understand the future I'm aiming for?"

By the end, you'll be much closer to a "yes."
