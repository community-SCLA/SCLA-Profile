===== SCRIPT (verbatim from dashboard) =====

When you’re repositioning your experience, one of the most powerful questions you can ask is:
“Which experiences show that I’m ready for the **future** I want, not just the roles I’ve already had?”

[On screen: Title “Curate Your Story for the Future” with a simple highlight around the word “future.”]

Think of your resume and LinkedIn profile as a curated gallery, not a storage room. You don’t need to display every piece—you need to choose the pieces that tell the right story.

[Graphic: Gallery wall with a few framed “projects” highlighted, and a larger storage area faded in the background.]

To do that, look for experiences that:

- Use the strengths you want to be known for.
- Match the kind of scope you’d like to handle going forward.
- Reflect the impact that matters in your target roles—such as improved outcomes, better processes, stronger relationships, or growth.

[Motion graphic: Icons for “Strengths,” “Scope,” “Impact” hovering over sample project cards.]

For example, if your Next Move Statement points toward a leadership or strategic role, you might highlight projects where you:

- Led teams or cross‑functional initiatives.
- Influenced decisions.
- Owned results that had visible impact.

If you’re aiming for a lateral move into a new domain, you might spotlight experiences that show how you:

- Learned quickly in new environments.
- Translated skills across contexts.
- Collaborated with that domain, even if your title was different.

[Graphic: Two columns labeled “Leadership Path” and “Lateral Path,” each with 2–3 example experiences listed.]

In this module’s activities, you’ll pick 3–5 experiences that best support your Next Move Statement and rewrite their bullets using a simple formula:
**Responsibility → Action → Measurable outcome → Scope.**

[On screen: The formula displayed; below it, a bullet animates through each component.]

This isn’t about inventing a new story. It’s about telling the true story of your work in a way that makes your future direction clear. When you choose experiences that match the future you’re aiming for, you make every bullet work harder for you.
