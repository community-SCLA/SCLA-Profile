===== SCRIPT (verbatim from dashboard) =====

Networking is one part of your career momentum. The other part is **visibility**—making sure the right people can actually see your contributions and potential.

[On screen: Title “Visibility Actions” with a soft spotlight effect over the word “Visibility.”]

Visibility isn’t about self‑promotion for its own sake. It’s about making sure that decision‑makers, sponsors, and peers understand what you’re working on, where you’re growing, and how you add value

Research on professional visibility shows that behaviors like speaking up, sharing results publicly, contributing to high‑impact projects, and building a reputation in your field can significantly affect career outcomes

[Graphic: Four icons labeled “Speak up,” “Share results,” “High‑impact projects,” “Contribute publicly.”]

So what are **visibility actions**? They’re small, intentional behaviors that:

- Put your work and insights where others can see them
- Link your name to meaningful contributions
- Build a consistent reputation over time.

[On screen: Definition box: “Visibility actions = consistent, observable behaviors that make your value visible.”]

Examples of visibility actions include:

- Volunteering to present a project update at a cross‑department meeting.
- Sharing a short summary of a success or learning on an internal channel or LinkedIn.
- Asking thoughtful, forward‑looking questions in meetings.
- Taking on a stretch role in a high‑impact project.
- Offering to mentor a colleague or run a short learning session on a skill you have.

[Motion graphic: A calendar with small visibility actions marked across the month.]

Notice that none of these require you to be loud, extroverted, or constantly “on.” They are **focused, purposeful moments** that help others connect your name to useful contributions and growing capabilities.

In this module, you’ll design 3 visibility actions tailored to your context and Next Move Statement—for example:

- “Share a brief overview of my [project] outcomes with my leadership team.”
- “Ask one strategic question at the next all‑staff meeting.”
- “Offer a 30‑minute mini‑session on [skill] to my team or community.”

[On screen: Sample visibility actions displayed as cards.]

Then, you’ll choose one to test in the next 30 days and reflect on what you learn.

Visibility is not about being perfect. It’s about practicing small behaviors that make your work and future direction easier to see—and easier for others to support.
