When you are repositioning your experience, one of the most powerful questions you can ask is this. Which experiences show that I am ready for the future I want, not just the roles I have already had?

Think of your resume and your LinkedIn profile as a curated gallery, not a storage room. You do not need to display every piece. You need to choose the pieces that tell the right story.

To do that, look for experiences that do three things.

First, they use the strengths you want to be known for.

Second, they match the kind of scope you would like to handle going forward.

Third, they reflect the impact that matters in your target roles — improved outcomes, better processes, stronger relationships, or growth.

Strengths, scope, impact. Which of your experiences point that way?

For example, if your Next Move Statement points toward a leadership or strategic role, you might highlight projects where you led teams or cross-functional initiatives, where you influenced decisions, or where you owned results that had visible impact.

If you are aiming for a lateral move into a new domain, you might spotlight experiences that show how you learned quickly in new environments, how you translated skills across contexts, or how you collaborated with that domain even when your title was different.

In this module's activities, you will pick three to five experiences that best support your Next Move Statement, and rewrite their bullets using a simple formula. Responsibility, then action, then measurable outcome, then scope.
