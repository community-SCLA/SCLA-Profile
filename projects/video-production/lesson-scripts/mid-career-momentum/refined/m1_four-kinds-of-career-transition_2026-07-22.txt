You've built up years of experience. Projects delivered, teams supported, stakeholders managed. And yet your next step probably feels less clear than your first job did.

So in Module 1 — Define Your Next Move — we're going to walk through four kinds of career transition, and simplify your options into three common paths you can actually choose between.

Start with Reinvention. A reinvention path deepens your influence and scope right where you already are. That can mean leading larger initiatives, supervising others, taking on more strategic responsibility, or moving into a higher-level title in the same general area. Promotion is often the right path when you still believe in the setting you're in, but you want more responsibility, greater visibility, or stronger recognition.

Second is the Rebuild. A rebuild move gets called "sideways," and that word does it no favors. What it usually means is moving into a better-fit role, team, department, or organization — one where your strengths show up more clearly. Sometimes a rebuild is the smartest long-term growth choice available to you, because it gives you better alignment, stronger mentorship, or more meaningful work.

Third is the Pivot. This one reshapes your current role without leaving it. You may not need a new title or a new employer to gain momentum. Sometimes what you really need is a change in focus, boundaries, projects, or responsibilities. A pivot might look like negotiating for different initiatives, cutting low-value tasks, stepping into more strategic work, or building a hybrid role around your strengths.

There's a fourth kind, and it's the one nobody picks off a menu: Forced Reinvention. This one isn't of your own doing, but it opens up options for you to explore.

None of these paths is better in general. The right path is the one that lines up with your values, energizes you, and fits your life constraints right now. Values, energy, constraints — where those three overlap is your right path for now.

Here's how you'll find it. First you'll reflect on your current role, the role you want, and what success would look like over the next year. Then you'll run an AI activity to analyze the projects that energize you, so your choice is grounded in evidence, not just impulse. Finally, you'll capture your direction in a Next Move Statement for the next six to twelve months.

You don't have to solve your entire career here. You only have to decide which of these three paths makes the most sense for you right now. That clarity is what creates momentum.
