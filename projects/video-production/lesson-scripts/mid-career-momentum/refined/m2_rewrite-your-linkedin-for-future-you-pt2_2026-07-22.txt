Your LinkedIn profile is probably doing less work for you than you think.

Many mid-career professionals treat it as a static biography. A list of titles, and a vague summary that says something like "experienced professional in the industry." Accurate. Also invisible.

In reality, your LinkedIn is one of the most important positioning tools you own. Recruiters, managers, and peers often see your headline and your About section before anything else. Those two pieces of text are making your introductions all day, whether you wrote them on purpose or not.

So let's write them on purpose.

A strong mid-career headline and summary does three things. First, it speaks to the roles you're aiming for next, not only the ones you've already held. Second, it highlights the strengths and skills you want to be known for. Third, it shows the value you create, in a way that's clear and human.

Target role. Key skills. Value you create. Hold onto those three, because they're also the shape of your headline.

Here's the structure. Your target role or direction, then your key strengths or skills, then the value you create or the area you focus on. Three parts, separated by a divider.

Here's what that looks like in practice. Before: "Project Manager at X Company." That's a job label. It tells people where you sit. After: "Senior Program Leader | Driving cross-functional initiatives | Improving outcomes in higher ed." That's positioning. It tells people what you're moving toward, what you're strong at, and who benefits.

Notice what changed. Nothing about that person's experience. Only what they chose to put in front of it.

Then your About summary. Think of it as a short narrative in three moves. It connects your past to your future. It names two or three themes or strengths you want attached to your name. And it describes the kind of problems you love to solve, and who you love solving them for. Past, then strengths and themes, then future direction.

So in this module, you'll draft a future-oriented headline and summary, and then use AI feedback to sharpen your positioning — whether you're going for a promotion, a lateral move, or a redesign of the role you're already in.
