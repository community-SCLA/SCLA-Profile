The most useful question about AI and your career isn't "will it replace my job?" It's "how will it change my job — and how do I aim myself at the more valuable part?"

That's a harder question. It's also the one you're equipped to answer. Mid-career, you have the leverage to do exactly that, because you already understand your role deeply enough to see which parts are shifting.

So here's a practical way to look at your current role and your next move. Three questions, in order.

First: what tasks might AI do faster or cheaper? Answer that one honestly rather than defensively. Assume those tasks get automated or accelerated, and don't build your case around defending them.

Second: what becomes more valuable as a result? Usually it's the judgment, the relationships, the creativity, and the oversight around those tasks — the very things your experience already makes you good at.

Third: how do you reposition toward that more valuable part? This is where AI-era thinking meets everything else in this course. Your Next Move Statement, your repositioned resume, and your visibility can all be aimed at the same target: the human, high-judgment work that grows more valuable as AI spreads.

Here's what that looks like in practice. AI can draft a first pass of almost any document, analysis, or plan. That makes the drafting less scarce. And it makes one person more valuable, not less — the person who knows what's worth saying, who can judge quality, who owns the client relationship, and who will stake their reputation on the call.

Mid-career, that person is you. Aim your next move at that part.
