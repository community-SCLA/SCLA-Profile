Networking is one part of your career momentum. The other part is visibility—making sure the right people can actually see your contributions and your potential.

Visibility isn't about self-promotion for its own sake. It's about making sure that decision-makers, sponsors, and peers understand what you're working on, where you're growing, and how you add value.

So what are visibility actions? They're small, intentional behaviors that put your work and insights where others can see them, link your name to meaningful contributions, and build a consistent reputation over time.

Examples of visibility actions include volunteering to present a project update at a cross-department meeting, sharing a short summary of a success or a learning on an internal channel or on LinkedIn, asking thoughtful and forward-looking questions in meetings, taking on a stretch role in a high-impact project, and offering to mentor a colleague or run a short learning session on a skill you already have.

Notice that none of these require you to be loud, extroverted, or constantly "on." They're focused, purposeful moments that help others connect your name to useful contributions and growing capabilities.

In this module, you'll design three visibility actions tailored to your context and your Next Move Statement. For example: "Share a brief overview of my project outcomes with my leadership team." Or, "Ask one strategic question at the next all-staff meeting." Or, "Offer a thirty-minute mini-session on a skill to my team or community."

Then you'll choose one to test in the next 30 days, and reflect on what you learn.

Visibility isn't about being perfect. It's about practicing small behaviors that make your work and your future direction easier to see—and easier for others to support.
