When people hear "strategic networking," they jump straight to tactics. Who should I email? What should I say? How do I ask for help without it being awkward?

Those are important questions. But they aren't the first ones.

Before you move into outreach and visibility, slow down for a deeper one. Who do I actually want walking alongside me in this next chapter — and why?

This isn't about making a list of names. It's about deliberately designing a small constellation of relationships that could matter for your future, not just for your inbox this week.

As you think about your next move — a role transition, a new project, a shift in direction, or simply becoming more visible in the work you already do — certain people will matter more than others.

Some of them already understand parts of your story. They've seen you work. They know your strengths.

Others can help you see new possibilities: fields you haven't considered, roles you didn't know existed, paths that look different from what you see day to day.

And some sit close to the actual opportunities you care about — decisions about roles, projects, budgets, hiring, internal moves.

That gives you four kinds of people. Peers, who walk alongside you. Sponsors, who advocate for you. Opportunity holders, who shape roles and projects. And mentors, who help you think clearly.

In this activity, you'll name three to five specific people who could play a real part in your next chapter, and you'll look at each one through four lenses.

Who are they, and where do they sit in relation to your current or target field?

What type of relationship do they represent — peer, sponsor, opportunity holder, or mentor?

Why do they matter for your Next Move Statement?

And what would moving that relationship forward look like in the next ninety days?

Instead of treating networking as a numbers game — talk to as many people as possible — you're treating it as a values and direction game. Who genuinely fits the story I'm building about my career? What kind of relationship would be meaningful and realistic with each person?

So write down why each person is relevant to your Next Move Statement. That might be what they can see that you can't yet. It might be the opportunities they sit close to. It might be the perspective or support they could offer.

Then name one specific, respectful outcome you'd like to reach within ninety days. One twenty-minute conversation about your next move. Sharing your updated profile or resume and asking for feedback. Exploring internal opportunities on their team. Or asking for an introduction to someone in your target field.

At the end, step back and read the whole list for patterns. Are most of these relationships inside your current organization? Do you have a mix of peers, sponsors, and opportunity holders, or is everything weighted toward one type? Which single relationship matters most to nurture first, and why?

Those answers matter, because they guide where you put your energy when you start taking action.

You're not building a contact list. You're choosing who you want in your corner while you build this next chapter.

So take your time here. The more honest and specific you are now, the clearer and more grounded your outreach and visibility will feel later.
