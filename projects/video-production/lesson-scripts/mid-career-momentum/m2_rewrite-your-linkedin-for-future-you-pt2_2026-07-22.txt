Lesson: Mod 2 — Reposition Your Experience (vid 2)
Title: Rewrite Your LinkedIn for Future You

Many mid-career professionals treat their LinkedIn profile as a static biography: a list of titles and a vague summary about "experienced professionals."

[On screen: A bland LinkedIn profile mockup with a generic headline like "Experienced professional in X industry."]

In reality, your LinkedIn is one of your most important positioning tools. Recruiters, managers, and peers often see your headline and "About" section before anything else.

[Graphic: Zoom on the headline area with a label "Positioning Tool, not Job Label."]

A strong mid-career headline and summary does three things:
1. Speaks to the future roles you're aiming for.
2. Highlights the strengths and skills you want to be known for.
3. Shows the value you create in a way that is clear and human.

[Motion graphic: Text formula overlay "Target Role · Key Skills · Value You Create."]

For your headline, you can use a simple structure:
[Target role or direction] | [Key strengths/skills] | [Value you create or focus area].

[On screen: Example transformation
Before: "Project Manager at X Company"
After: "Senior Program Leader | Driving cross-functional initiatives | Improving outcomes in higher ed."]

For your "About" summary, think of it as a short narrative that:
* Connects your past to your future.
* Names 2–3 themes or strengths.
* Describes the kind of problems you love to solve and for whom.

[Graphic: Three-paragraph layout showing Past → Strengths/Theme → Future Direction.]

In this module, you'll draft a future-oriented headline and summary, then use AI feedback to sharpen your positioning for promotion, lateral move, or role redesign.

[On screen: Visual showing "Current Profile" sliding to the left and "Future-You Profile" sliding in on the right.]

When you reposition your LinkedIn this way, you make it easier for new doors to open—because people can immediately see how your experience and strengths match the roles and challenges you're excited about next.
