How to Find & Create a Career Purpose Statement

Everything you have done so far — your criteria, your energy themes, your mapped options — comes together in one short, personal artifact: a Career Purpose Statement. — a north star you can actually use.

A career purpose statement is one or two sentences. This is not a corporate mission statement, and it is not permanent.  It's a working answer to "what am I trying to do with my work right now, and why?" — specific enough to guide a decision, honest enough to be yours.

A simple structure that works:

Examples in the wild:

"I want to use my knack for making complex things simple to help first-gen students navigate systems that weren't built for them — because I needed that help and didn't have it."

"I want to get genuinely good at building products, in a place that invests in me, so I can eventually solve problems I care about with real leverage."

I want to use my strengths in clear writing and organization to help small nonprofits communicate better, by starting in a coordinator or comms role, while building toward program design."

Notice: no grand claims, no buzzwords. Just direction, contribution, and a reason. Yours should sound like you.

It can feel awkward to write. That is normal — you are putting words to something that was fuzzy five minutes ago. Aim for honest and rough over polished and empty. You can revise it every time you run the decision loop.

A useful statement usually blends three things:

What energizes you (from previous activity).

Who or what you want to help (your "helps others" criterion made specific).

The kind of value you want to build (the skills or contribution you are growing toward).

Script

Finding your purpose starts with Career Mapping, but it doesn't end there. That is just the beginning. You want to be on a path to your career and make decisions about pursuits. This exercise puts the 5 ingredients into practice in an easy framework.  You can complete this activity either through the AI-chat or the Workbook.

Let's Find & Create Your Career Purpose Statement

The pace of job searching and career decisions is getting faster and faster.  The number of opportunities is exploding. How do you choose what to focus on and what to ignore? The answer lies in clarity about our career purpose, mission, or vision.

Here are the key attributes of a good purpose or career purpose statement:

It's inspiring and uplifting.

It's driven by emotional energy.

You would commit to it for 5 years.

It gives you a mission, a focus.

It feels "true & authentic" for you.

It is brief, easy to remember, and easy to repeat.

What follows is a series of probing questions to help you understand what's deeply motivating you. You can skip any of them, but please answer at least 3. Please take the time to play full out!

[FORM] Activity · Create Your Career Purpose Statement

This is the capstone of the track. You've earned it — you have the raw material.

How to do this activity:

Pull up your notes: your top priority, energy patterns, criteria, and the direction you roughed out.

Draft your statement using the structure below. Messy first drafts are expected — write three versions and pick the one that rings truest.

Submit it in the form. [link to form].  You can also download this PDF, complete the PDF and upload it to the Form section.

Fill-in template:

I want to ________________________________________ (the kind of work you want to do / the strengths you want to use) to ________________________________________ (who you want to help / the outcome you want to create) because ________________________________________ (what makes this matter to you)

Form fields:

Your career purpose statement (long text)

One near-term step this statement points you toward (short text)

(Optional) One thing you're still uncertain about (short text)

👉  You have a decision process you can reuse for life, and a purpose statement in your own words. That's the confidence part of "clarity and confidence" — it comes from having a method, not from having certainty.
