Once you've got a side-hustle idea, the next question isn't only what you want to offer. It's how you want to work.

There are three models to weigh, and each one asks something different of you.

The first is freelancing. Freelancing is usually task or project based. You're hired to complete the work or produce the deliverable. A client needs a workshop run, a set of designs, a messaging refresh, a system set up, a short-term project shipped. In this model, you are often paid for doing the work directly.

The second is consulting. Consulting is a different job. Here, clients hire you for diagnosis, insight, problem-solving, and strategy. You analyze a process, name the real problem, recommend a new approach, and hand over a plan. The focus is more on clarity and outcomes than on being the person who executes every task.

The third is fractional work, and it goes a step further. In a fractional role, you become a part-time leader or part-time owner of a function. You might be a fractional operations lead, a fractional marketing lead, or a fractional program director. You're more embedded in the team, and more accountable over time.

So keep the shorthand close. Freelance is projects. Consulting is insight. Fractional is embedded outcomes.

When you're starting out, freelancing is often the easiest model to test, because the ask is smaller and the path to a yes is simpler. As you build experience and sharpen how you position yourself, you can add consulting projects, or grow into fractional roles.

In this module, you'll hold these three models up against your own preferences and choose one as your starting point, and maybe a second as where you're headed next.

So which one fits how you want to work?
