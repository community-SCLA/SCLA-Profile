One of the biggest reasons early solo work gets stressful isn't that the offer is bad. It's that there's no system around it.

You don't need a complicated business operating system to begin. You need a few simple pieces.

First, a place to track who you're talking to and what stage each opportunity is in. This can be as simple as a spreadsheet, a Notion board, or a document.

Second, a basic intake process. If someone says yes, what happens next? What questions do you ask? What information do you need? What do you send?

Third, a few templates. Proposal language, follow-up emails, meeting agendas, project checklists. Templates save you time and keep your work consistent.

Fourth, a weekly review ritual. One hour a week to look at your outreach, your active work, your admin, and your next steps keeps solo work from turning reactive.

And finally, boundaries. How many hours will you give this? What kinds of work are out of scope? What kinds of clients or timelines aren't a fit?

AI can help you build templates and organize information, but the boundaries are still your call. Sustainability is part of strategy.

In this module, you'll define your time budget, choose a few systems to build now, and decide where you need guardrails before your path grows.
