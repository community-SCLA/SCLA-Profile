Once you have a side-hustle idea, the next question is not just what you want to offer. It is how you want to work.

[On screen: Three labeled columns appear: Freelance, Consulting, Fractional. Each has an icon: wrench, map, and embedded team diagram.]

Freelancing is usually task or project based. You are hired to complete work or produce deliverables. A client may need a workshop, a set of designs, a messaging refresh, a system setup, or a short-term project. In this model, you are often paid for doing the work directly.

Consulting is different. Here, clients hire you for diagnosis, insight, problem-solving, and strategy. You might analyze a process, identify problems, recommend a new approach, and deliver a plan. The focus is more on clarity and outcomes than on being the person who executes every task.

Fractional work goes further. In a fractional role, you become a part-time leader or part-time owner of a function. You might be a fractional operations lead, a fractional marketing lead, or a fractional program director. You are more embedded in the team and more accountable over time.

[Graphic: Text appears — “Freelance = projects. Consulting = insight. Fractional = embedded outcomes.”]

If you are just starting out, freelancing is often the easiest model to test because the ask is smaller and the path to saying yes is simpler. As you gain experience and clearer positioning, you may later add consulting projects or move into fractional roles.

In this module, you’ll compare these three models against your own preferences and choose one as your starting point—and possibly another as a future evolution.
