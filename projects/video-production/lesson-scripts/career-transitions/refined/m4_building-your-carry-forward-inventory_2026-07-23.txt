What deserves to come with you?

That's the question at the center of this lesson, and the tool that answers it is the Carry-Forward Inventory — a structured list of what you built in your prior career that is genuinely portable into your next chapter.

Notice the word genuinely. Not everything is portable. Some things belong to the old context, and it's okay to leave them there. But here's what most career changers get wrong: the honest inventory is longer than they realize. You've built more that travels than you think — and building that list explicitly is one of the single highest-leverage exercises in this whole track.

Your inventory has four categories.

First: Skills and Capabilities — what you can do that translates. The question to sit with is this: what can I do at a professional level that has nothing to do with my old title?

Second: Values and Drivers — what you care about that carries. Ask yourself: what did I care about deeply that I don't want to lose?

Third: Relationships and Network — who travels with you. This one takes some honesty. Ask: which of my relationships were about the role, and which were genuinely about the person? The ones about the person come with you.

And fourth: Insights and Perspective — what you see that others don't. Ask: what patterns did I learn in my last field that would be non-obvious in a new one? That outsider's eye is something people who've spent their whole career in one place simply don't have.

So work the list, one category at a time. What you can do that translates. What you care about that carries. Who travels with you. What you see that others don't.

This isn't nostalgia — it's an asset count. And we'd put money on yours being longer than you expect. When you can see in one place what genuinely comes with you, your next chapter stops feeling like starting from zero. Because you're not starting from zero. You're carrying forward.
