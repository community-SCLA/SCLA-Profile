Your Reinvention Roadmap is not a contract. It's a compass. It tells you where you're pointed, what you've committed to try, what you're building toward, and what runway you're operating on. When the ground shifts — and it will — the roadmap gives you something to shift from, not something to abandon.

The roadmap has four elements.

First, your reinvention direction — the version you can commit to today, knowing it will refine.

Second, your 90-day plan — broken into three 30-day phases, each with specific deliverables.

Third, your financial and emotional runway — the resources supporting you through the transition.

Fourth, your next step — the one commitment you make coming out of this track.

Why ninety days? Because it's the right unit for a reinvention plan. Long enough to make meaningful progress; short enough to stay accountable. Beyond ninety days, the plan should be re-drafted based on what you've learned — not extended blindly.
