## The spine — three pillars

Every piece of SCLA writing carries at least one of these and contradicts none.

### 1. Warm-but-demanding — a high bar is an act of belief

We expect a lot *because* we see what members are capable of. High standards, never a velvet
rope. The coach who sees more in you than you see in yourself. Rigor and warmth aren't opposites.

- **Do:** "You didn't get invited by accident. We hold the bar high because we know you'll clear it."
- **Don't:** gatekeep ("only the elite get in") or coddle ("anyone can do this, no effort").

### 2. The insider playbook — the moves nobody teaches you

We hand members the scripts, frameworks, and quiet rules that usually stay hidden until you've
learned them the hard way. Generous with what we know. The member is the talent; we handle the map.

- **Do:** plain, candid, step-by-step. "Here's the truth," then the actual move.
- **Don't:** vague inspiration with no next step; jargon that hoards knowledge.

### 3. Belonging is the engine — you were never meant to do this alone

The pillar we lean into hardest. Achievement sticks when it happens inside a community. SCLA is a
*society* — a national network pulling for each other, not a certificate you frame alone. Name the
room, invite people in, remind members the network is theirs. (Current blog under-indexes here —
expand the belonging lane.)

- **Do:** "we," "your people," "the network is yours," "you're in good company."
- **Don't:** solo-grind framing, or membership as a transaction.

## Voice axes

| Axis | Position | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Formal ↔ Casual | Casual with professional grounding | Contractions, direct address, light humor — never stiff or legalistic. |
| Technical ↔ Plain | Plain | Consumer-facing; define terms before using them. |
| Earnest ↔ Playful | Earnest with deliberate wordplay | Confident wordplay ("Secret Weapon," "Cutting Through the Noise"). Warm, never sarcastic. |
| Authoritative ↔ Supportive | Supportive *with high expectations* | Enabler, not gatekeeper — but name a high bar and express belief members will clear it. |
| Institutional ↔ Human | Human | Short sentences, real talk, acknowledges doubts, "you"/"your" throughout. |
| Solo ↔ Communal | Communal | Default to "we" and "your people"; achievement happens inside the society, not alone. |

## Phrases and patterns to avoid

| Avoid | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| "exclusive club" / "only the elite" | Contradicts belonging + the invitation-based framing. High bar ≠ velvet rope. |
| Competitor honor society names | Brand clarity |
| Legalistic register ("pursuant to," "heretofore") | Breaks the plain, human voice |
| "just" as a minimizer ("just a student") | Undercuts member confidence — opposite of pillar 1 |
| Passive voice ("students are empowered by") | Site copy is active ("SCLA empowers students") |
| "prestigious" alone | Reads as gatekeeping; pair with belief in the member or what it unlocks |
| Pure solo-grind framing | Misses pillar 3 |
| Vague inspiration with no next step | Misses pillar 2 — tie aspiration to a concrete move |

## Write this, not that

| Write this | Not that | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "The SCLA" | "SCLA" (no article) in prose | Body copy uses "The SCLA" |
| "Honor Society" (capitalized) | "honor society" as SCLA's title | Proper noun |
| "100,000 members are in your corner" | "we have over 100,000 members" | Make scale relational, not a stat (P3) |
| "the move nobody teaches you is…" | "tips for success" | Insider playbook beats generic advice (P2) |
| "we hold the bar high because we believe you'll clear it" | "membership is prestigious and exclusive" | High standards as belief, not gatekeeping (P1) |
| "you're not building this alone" | "build your future" (solo) | Belonging is the engine (P3) |
| "you don't need years of experience to…" | "even beginners can…" | Direct second person is warmer; "even beginners" frames the reader as less-than |
| "SCLA is designed as an active development experience" | "membership gives you access to…" | Active beats transactional |
| "from campus to career" | "post-graduation success" | Use tagline language consistently |
